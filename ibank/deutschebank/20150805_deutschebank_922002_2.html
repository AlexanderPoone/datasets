US Economics - US Daily Economic Notes - Joseph LaVorgna

This morning's ADP private employment survey will anchor market expectations for Friday's nonfarm payroll report. Our forecast for the former is roughly in line with the 12-month trailing average of the series. While ADP tends to be one of the better predictors of the monthly change in private nonfarm payrolls, it is far from perfect. Case in point, over the past two years, the median absolute difference between the initial ADP print and private payrolls has been a little less than 40k. Hence, it would take a sizeable downside miss on ADP for us to revise our payroll forecast. The hurdle may be even higher if the employment component of the nonmanufacturing ISM survey moves back toward the higher end of its post-recession range. Details on page 11

India Equity Strategy Marketscope

Abhishek Saraf: Earnings revisions turn neutral in July'15. For the first time in the current calendar year, the earnings revision ratio for DB coverage has turned neutral, with DB analysts raising FY16/FY17 earnings for 23/25 companies and cutting FY16/FY17 earnings for 26/24 companies. Until now cuts had far exceeded raises as Dec’14 and Mar'15 earnings had been largely disappointing. In addition, July'15 witnessed the first instance of positive Sensex EPS revisions in 2015, with DB est for FY16/FY17 Sensex EPS raised by 0.3%/0.1%. This is in line with our thesis that the worst of earnings cuts may be over as (i) several macro indicators are showing improvement, (ii) the government has started to spend, and (iii) earnings have already been cut sharply since the start of the year. In July'15, oil companies witnessed the largest earnings raises, with HPCL/BPCL EPS raised 10%-12%. Other key stocks to see meaningful EPS raises were Bharti (10%/7%for FY16/17), TCS (6%/1%), RIL (4%/2%), Coal India (3%/4%) and Infosys (3%/3%), among others. Details on page 13
