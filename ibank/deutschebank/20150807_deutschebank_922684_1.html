China Orient Asset Management --Consent solicitation is credit neutral. Maintain Buy, dated Aug 4

The company is seeking to remove the Debt Ratio Failure covenant embedded into the Deed of Equity Interest Purchase and Investment Undertaking on its USD bonds. We believe that should the covenant be removed, such an event would be credit neutral for ORIEAS bonds. Investors would still be able to rely on the Keepwell Deed and on the Change of Control provision.

Lifestyle - 1H15 resilient; weak retail conditions to persist, dated Aug 3 Lifestyle's 1H15 results were resilient. Credit metrics were modestly softer; we think still supportive of a BBB- profile. Management is cautious for 2H but believe HK stores' performance will be steady. Management noted 'insignificant' wealth effect at its Shanghai and Suzhou store following the A-share correction in July but the impact is more felt at its HK stores with July doing worse than June. We have a CreditHold on LIHHK curve.
