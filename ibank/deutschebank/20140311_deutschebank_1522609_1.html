Wechat m-commerce welded to JD; relief from margin erosion

Tencent's strategic investment in JD +vc on margins

Tencent earlier today announced plans to acquire 15% of B2C platform JD preIPO, and a call option to take up to an additional 5% of the enlarged post-IPO co at IPO price. On top of a US$215m cash offering, the co will also inject a series of e-commerce assets into JD (Figure 1) and grant JD its highest gateway access (Level 1) to the Wechat platform/Wechat Payment. The deal furthers Tencent's e-commerce strategy, as it: 1) sticks to an asset light platform model as traffic gateway and payment provider, while 2) inviting capable, infrastructure-heavy vertical players to partner through equity investment/asset exchange (Dianping, Sogou, etc), and as a result 3) allows margin improvement thanks to de-consolidation of loss making subsidiaries while retaining strategic exposure.

Building a holistic Wechat/QQ eco-system; closing the 020 e-commerce loop We expect strictly managed public accounts to be the main interface for e-commerce merchants. Offline-to-online (020) deals and settlement will be supported by Wechat payment. Tencent will likely classify merchants through differentiated Wechat/mobile 00 access levels. Charging models include: 1) commissions levied on vendor revenues generated on the platform (-0.6% as reported), 2) WeChat Payment settlement commissions, and 3) public account performance-based ads. In exchange, Tencent partners (Dianping, CSC, WFJ, etc) will facilitate with: 1) warehousing, logistics and supply chain mgmt, 2) 020 deployment and execution and 3) merchant creditability assurance.
