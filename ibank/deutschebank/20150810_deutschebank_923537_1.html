Strategy Update



Reiterating sizable 2H upside thanks to fundamental improvement

After the technical market rebound we forecasted in early July (see Policy enhanced to the next level; technical rebound likely, 8 July), we detailed in our 50-page 2H15 China Equity Outlook - Fundamentals to drive the next leg up. published on 17 July, that we see sizable (>20%) upside potentials in H-shares based on our expectation of stronger fiscal stimulus over the coming weeks and the subsequent economic and earnings growth pickup. We see an even better risk/reward after our recent investor meetings as we find institutions generally have light positions in H-shares and most hold clear reservations about the case of notable upside (see Investor feedback reveals light positions; reiterating rosy 2H outlook, 3 Aug).

Looking ahead, we maintain our positive market view as we expect the earnings growth pickup and still elevated ERP (equity risk premium) to attract global fund inflows and send HSCEI to 14,000 by end-2015. Our top-5 picks (in this pecking order) include property (COLI), infrastructure (CRCC), cement (BBMG), insurance (Ping An) and TMT (BIDU). Also see page 37-38 of our 2H15 Outlook for sector recommendations and top-10 stock portfolio.

Below we reiterate four key reasons about our constructive market stance:

  • ■   1. We see stronger fiscal stimulus over the coming months: In the most

recent mid-year economic review Politbüro meeting chaired by President Xi on 30 July, we notice top leaders stipulated they would "make sure of an expansionary fiscal policy". We believe this implies that fiscal policy was actually not very supportive in 1H15 as land sales fell visibly, and we should see this rectified in 2H15.

  • ■   2. We expect GDP to edge up thanks to infrastructure and property FAI:

We expect infra. FAI growth to accelerate in 2H15 as fiscal stimulus intensifies, and LGFVs may effectively leverage up again through various channels, we believe property FAI will stabilize after the 5-year secular deceleration since early 2010, thanks to the usual 6-month lagged boost effect from the steady pickup of property sales growth.

  • ■   3. We expect non-financials to stage a notable growth recovery in 2H15

due to the dampened base in 2H14 (-4% vs. 5% in 1H14) and demand pick-up as FAI projects kick in. For 2015, we project 2.9% and 6.0% earnings growth in non-financials and financials respectively to achieve 4.6% overall H-share growth.

  • ■   4. We look for fund inflows to H-shares to enjoy the elevated ERP: the

current elevated implied H-share ERP of 6.9% implies investors' light positions and low expectations. If Chinese fundamentals improve in 2H15 as we expect, this 6.9% level looks quite attractive on a 6-month horizon and may attract fund inflows, in our opinion.
