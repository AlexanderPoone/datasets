2Q; equity investments expand losses


2Q: top-line in line; equity investments widening loss

JD.com reported 2Q GMV of RMB114.5b (+82% YoY/+30% QoQ), in line with DBe. Net rev was RMB45.9b (+61% YoY/+30% QoQ), beating high-end guidance/consensus by 3%/4%, and in line with DBe. GPM saw continued improvement of 180bps YoY to 12.9%, due to the favorable mix shift to marketplace. Non-GAAP operating loss margin narrowed by 50bps YoY /60bps QoQ, which was 30bps/170bps better than DBe/consensus. Non-GAAP net loss widened by 92% YoY to RMB16m, vs DBe of marginal profit. The result slightly beat cons, mainly on adjustments from business acquisition and equity investment loss. The co guided 3Q revs of RMB43.2b-44.7b (+49%-54% YoY), with mid-point 2%/1% lower than DBe/cons. Given the impact from 020 spend and minority investment margin drag, the co maintained FY15 NPM guidance at breakeven to -0.5% Maintain Hold.

Expecting marketplace to drive top-line growth

Mobile orders grew strongly at 294% YoY to 47% of total orders. Marketplace saw a strong rebound in sales after CNY (GMV +110% YoY), and rose to 44% of total GMV vs 42% in 1015, driving the improvement in GPM (+190bps YoY). We note industry smartphone and tablet sales growth is sluggish, and hence we bring down GMV growth of 3C products for FY15E. We envision a higher GMV contribution from marketplace (DBe 45% of FY15 GMV) on the back of a solid customer growth outlook (20% of new customers acquired from WeChat/Mobile QQ) and category expansion through cooperation in autos (Bitauto) and travel (Tuniu).

Investing in 020; minority investments drag margins

The co announced an investment in Yonghui hypermarket (US$700m for a 10% stake), aiming to leverage the co's leading position in grocery retailing and to support JD Daojia offerings. As the core 020 product, JD Daojia now has expanded to 7 cities. It leverages crowd-sourcing logistics in certain cities to enhance delivery efficiencies and minimize cost. JD's 020 strategy focuses on product-oriented offerings instead of services, as JD’s strong logistics capacity can provide operating leverage. JD meanwhile invested in Tuniu and Bitauto (in 10) to expand these categories. We however expect these minority investments could lead JD to bear investment losses in the near term.

Reducing TP by 1.5% to US$33; Maintaining Hold

We increase FY15/16/17E GMV forecasts by 1%2%/2% to reflect marketplace growth. We widen our FY15 non-GAAP net loss margin by 10bps to reflect equity investment margins drag. A new TP of US$33 is based on an unchanged equal weighting of DCF and FY15E EV/Sales. Risks: better/worse-than-expected take-rate; intensifying competition on marketplace, better/worse performance on new 020 initiatives.
