1H15 result: world class sector position

Delivering superior sector growth and capital returns

Rio delivered impressive 1H15 earnings of US$2.9b, well ahead of consensus of US$2.4b, but in-line with our US$2.9b estimate. Stronger FCF on lower capex resulted in net debt of US$13.7b, below our US$14.5b estimate. Significant further progress has been made in reducing capex with no adverse impact to what is already sector leading production growth from the Pilbara, copper and bauxite. The extra US$1.5b drop in capex is setting up Rio for another US$2b buyback in February 2016, even at US$50/t Fe, in our view. We have lifted EPS by 2% and NPV by 4% (to A$71 /sh). We continue to think that Rio is the stand-out big miner on valuation, FCF, growth and buy-backs; Buy.

Significant capex reductions without sacrificing growth

The big reduction in capex over the next two years should not impact production growth and should increase the probability of another US$2b buyback in February 2016. Capex guidance lowered to US$5.5b in 2015 and then increase to US$6b in 2016 and then is expected to increase to US$7b in 2017. Production growth is still climbing over the next 5 years with the Pilbara 360 project delivery and a rebound in copper volumes. Rio's 2017 capex estimate compares to our US$4.6b forecast; however, we do not include the South of Embley bauxite and Zulte South mineral sand projects which should be approved in 4Q15. We also forecast a further drop in the AUD to 63c. The development decision on the Silvergrass iron ore project was previously delayed to 2016. Sustaining capex is expected to remain at around US$2.5b.

Strong performance from aluminium and iron ore

The transformation of the aluminium division continues. Divisional earnings of US$793m were in-line with our US$781 m forecast but 35% ahead of consensus (for the third year in a row). Higher metal product premiums, bauxite prices and lower costs drove the result. We expect primary metal costs to continue to fall post the ramp-up of Kitimat and a further fall in operating currencies. Iron Ore underlying earnings of US$2.1 b compared to our US$2.2b estimate. Unit costs (C1) of US$16.2/t for the Pilbara were in-line with our US$16.1/t estimate with Rio stating at current spot AUD and diesel unit costs are around US$15/t. We expect unit costs to fall to US$11/t in 2017E (FX and cost out)


Valuation and risks

Our PT is set broadly in-line with our A$70.89/sh DCF-derived valuation.

Downside risks include weaker commodity prices and higher costs (refer p.13).
