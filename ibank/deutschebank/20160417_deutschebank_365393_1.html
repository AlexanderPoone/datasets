CBEC update: loosening regulation on liquid milk

Liquid milk is included in the CBEC positive phase II list

The Ministry of Finance ("MOF”) published a phase II positive list for the new cross-boarder ecommerce ("CBEC”) regulations. This time, liquid milk is included into the list and will be allowed to be imported from CBEC channels. (Also refer to our note published on April 8, “Potential benefits of new CBEC regulations").

Likely to increase competition on domestic high-end liquid milk

We expect that CBEC channels accounts only a small portion (i.c.<10%) of imported liquid milk. However, loosening of the regulation will still increase supply and bring additional competition. The positive side for domestic names (Mengniu, Yili and Bright) is that imported milk from CBEC channel will subject a higher tax rate (from 0 to 11.9%).
