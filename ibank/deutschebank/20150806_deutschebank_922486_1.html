Cutting earnings and TP on weak sales; D/G Great Wal I-A to Sell

Sales downtrend continued in July, with softness even in its SUV models Great Wall Motor's (GWM) H-shares price fell by 34.2% since July 2015 (vs. HSCEI Index's 14.5% correction), mainly due to market concerns on slowing sales growth amid intensifying SUV segment competition and weaker-than-expected preliminary 1H15 results (announced on 27 July), in our view. On 6 August, the company announced that they sold 47,445 units of vehicles in July, implying a 1.7% YoY decline. More importantly, GWM's SUV business, which has been the stronghold of the company, only recorded 12.8% YoY growth (vs. 48.7% in 1H15) to 39,931 units, with a 13.0% YoY decline in sales volume of the industry leading Haval H6 model (including H6 Coupe). In summary, the weakening sales momentum at Great Wall Motor echoes ours and market worries that GWM's SUV segment leadership is fading.

Lower operating efficiencies and higher selling costs likely to drag margins Year-to-date, GWM recorded 17.0% vehicle sales volume growth YoY to 462,784 units, with 43.8% volume growth for SU Vs. The overall sales volume just accounts for 53% of the company's 2015E sales target. While we still expect Great Wall to post 7.7-12.2% volume growth in FY15-17 on more new SUV launches (e.g. Haval H7 in 2H15E), we are now doubtful that the company could meet its sales target for the year. What is more, with competitors posing greater threats to GWM, we now envision higher costs for GWM to defend their market position, including 1) higher promotional costs in the way of discounts and advertizing, 2) higher component costs to ensure product qualities, and 3) higher R&D costs to stay competitive in the long run. Last but not least, with slowing sales momentum, we expect under-utilization in GWM's new capacity and hence operating leverage will be affected.

Earnings and target price cuts on lower volume and margins; maintaining Hold on H shares and downgrading A share to Sell

We cut FY15-17 net revenue forecasts by 6.4-6.9%, on lower vehicle sales volume assumptions. With higher cost base and lower utilization, we cut FY15-17 net profit forecasts by 14.0-14.7%. Our new target prices for both A and H shares are set at 7.Ox average FY15/16E P/E (from 8.5x), about 25% above the company's historical H share trading average. We believe GWM still deserves a valuation premium to the historical average P/E, largely because its SUV-focused strategy has played out only in the last few years amid strengthening SUV demand in China. Our target P/E for Great Wall is also at the lower end of the range we apply to the peers under our coverage (7-11x ex-BYD), which we believe is fair given that its growth outlook is also lower than that of its peers (e.g. 8% FY1 7E EPS growth for Great Wall on competition, compared with 12-19% FY17E for peers). The implied average FY15/16E P/BV of 1.5x also seems reasonable considering sustainable ROE of about 20-21%. As we foresee downside risk to its SUV sales on competition, we maintain Hold on GWM H shares. We also downgraded GWM's A share to Sell given its widening valuation premium to H share with limited justification, in our view. Key upside/downside risks: stronger-/weaker-than-expected sales and competition.
