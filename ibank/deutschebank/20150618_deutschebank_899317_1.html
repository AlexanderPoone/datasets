THEME OF THE DAY

Update - China: State Council approves further fiscal stimulus (Zhiwei Zhang) The State Council held a meeting today and decided to launch more investment projects to boost growth. We take this as another significant fiscal easing measure (for other fiscal easing measures please see our notes issued on May 14, 15, and 18). It reinforces our view that growth will pick up slightly to 7.0% in Q3 and 7.2% in Q4 from 6.8% in Q2. (Zhiwei Zhang - 852 2203 8308) http://pull.db-qmresearch.eom/p/7206-4756/68690783/DB Update 2015-06-17 0900b8c089d17226.pdf

China Insurance Sector - May premium - mixed Life; slight pick-up in P&C (Esther Chwei)

Life premium growth ranged from -19.8% to 29.9% in May (vs. -6.6% to 32.5% in Apr), with China Life leading the way. China Life, Ping An, Ch Taiping and CPIC recorded positive growth, which we believe was driven by strong agency business. NCI and PICC Group, on the other hand, recorded negative growth. P&C growth saw a slight pick-up, ranging from -2.1% to 23.4% (vs. -3.1% to 15.0% in Apr). We reiterate our preference for Life over P&C on expected pricing pressure in auto insurance. Top Picks are CPIC and Ch Taiping. (Esther Chwei - 852 2203 6200) http://pull.db-qmresearch.eom/o/560-A4C0/62587750/0900b8c089c810d7.odf

China Containerboard - Conference call takeaways - further paper price hikes planned (Johnson Wan)

We hosted investor conference calls with NDP and LMP this week. Both companies believe that the worst is over due to stringent environmental measures to cut obsolete capacity. As a result, paper price hikes of RMB50-150/t have successfully been passed through in South and East China. This coincided with lower OCC costs in 1Q15 so margins have rebounded. Recently, OCC costs have begun to rise therefore NDP and LMP are planning further price hikes before the end of June. The recent hike in OCC prices is also an indication of improving downstream demand. Debt levels are expected to decline and therefore there should be no need to consider equity financing. (Johnson Wan- 852 2203 6163) http://pull.db-qmresearch.eom/p/357-9CFD/63265080/0900b8c089d0f34b.pdf
