China Property


Pre-positioning for more supportive policies and improving supply picture


Valuations back to more reasonable levels

Since June 2015, along with the overall weaknesses in the A-share and the H-share market, the China property stocks have also recorded meaningful share price pullbacks. From 1 June to now, share prices of the China property stocks have fallen by an average of 17% - with Greenland HK having fallen the most at 46% while Evergrande was up 13% After such share price pullbacks, valuations in the sector have more or less returned to the levels at end-1015, and the sector is now trading at NAV discount (39% market-cap weighted), PE (7.Ox) and PB (0.9x) that are close to "-1SD" of the historical range.
