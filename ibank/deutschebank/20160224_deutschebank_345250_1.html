Industry Update



January AC: output cut softened helped by export demand

AC industry channel inventory issue remains; top pick Midea

January AC production data were softened, helped by export demand. Domestic sell-in sales fell by 26% yoy, mainly due to the weak demand in the retail end and the channel inventory issue. We have not seen demand pick up in the retail end, and channel inventory is likely to remain high. The property relaxation should help drive AC sales in a 6-9 months lagging manner. We expect air conditioner channel stocking pressure to ease by mid-2016 and Midea to be a key beneficiary of the destocking.

January AC production softened; retail end not picked up yet

China IOL released air conditioner (AC) output and sales volume for January 2016. Production volume fell slightly by 2% yoy to 8.5m units in January; softened from a decline of 12% in 2015. This was helped by an 11% yoy increase in exports. Domestic sales declined by 26% yoy, mainly attributable to the weak demand in the retail end (down 5% yoy in January 2016 vs. down 4% in 2015) and the over-stock in the channel. The peak season for AC production is March-June, which accounts for 43-44% of full-year AC production volume.

Channel inventory was seven months of full-year sales by end-2015

According to China IOL, channel inventory increased by 4.8m units yoy to 36.4m units as of December 2015, implying 7 months of full-year sales in 2015. We believe the channel inventory is still at a high level, while the channel inventory is likely to digest, helped by the output constraint. This is slightly better than our estimate of eight months’ inventory as of October 2015. On the supplier side, inventory declined by 16% yoy in 4015 and inventory turnover was 1.5 months as of end-2015.

Tailwind on low-tier property market: MoF lowers transaction taxes

The Ministry of Finance has lowered the deed tax and business tax in order to relax the property transaction on February 22. Deed tax should be lowered by 1-2 ppts to 1-2%, and business tax for non-ordinary housing should be waived from 5% of the gain from the property transaction. This is the second property relaxation policy followed by relaxation on mortgages released in the same month. We believe the relaxation on mortgage policy will help the recovery in AC sales.

Midea is our top pick; Gree is suspended on possible major M&A deal

Midea is the largest AC exporter in China, taking 32% of the export market in January. Midea’s exports rose 24% yoy in January, leading an 11% yoy export rebound. Midea is our top pick as we believe: 1) it is the key beneficiary of the destocking of air conditioners in 2016, 2) it has a good balance of market share gain to build up brand equity and profitability and 3) its motivated management team focuses on shareholder returns under a stable dividend payment and share buyback plan. According to Gree's announcement, it should release detailed information on the possible M&A by 29 February.

Valuation and risks

Our primary valuation methodology is DCF, which captures the future cash flow of white goods companies. We also use PE multiples as cross-checks. Upside risks include recovery in the macro economy and property market. Downside risks include a macro economic slowdown, a weak property market, hikes in raw material prices and fierce competition.
