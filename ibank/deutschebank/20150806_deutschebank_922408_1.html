Reiterate Sell on CGN ahead of mediocre 1H15 results

CGN Power: 1H15 preview - an unexciting 4% yoy growth

CGN is going to report its 1H15 results on 19 Aug 2015. Compared with market expectation of a 9% yoy growth for full year earnings, we expect 1H15 earnings will be less satisfactory at Rmb2.66bn or up 4% yoy. In 1H15, consolidated output down 2.7% yoy and its most profitable Daya Bay units saw a 23% yoy decline in output on maintenance, though this could be offset by the higher contribution from JV & associates, which were loss-making in 1H14.

Still one of the most expensive utilities, reiterate Sell

Trading at 21 .Ox 15E P/E, CGN is among the most expensive utilities as compared with China IPPs (8.2x, DBe: 4-54% earnings growth in 1H ex. Datang), China Wind Developers (14.4x, DBe: 53-59% earnings growth in 1H15), China Gas (16.6x, DBe: 9-24% earnings growth in 1H15 ex. Kunlun), China Water (21.1x, DBe: 8-32% earnings growth in 1H15).

On the other hand, we see no fundamental improvement to our concerns on 1) Taishan project delay, 2) rising competition, 3) utilization downtrend, and 4) tariff risks. Up to now, Taishan Nuclear has not started cold testing for its 1st unit after reported component quality issues by supplier Areva, indicating high risks of project delay vs. its target operation of early 2016. Reiterate Sell with a target price of HK$2.9 that values the stock at 16.8x 15E P/E (2014-17E EPS CARG of 16%).

Prefer to get nuclear exposure via Huadian Fuxin: Fuqing-2 connected to grid Compared with CGN Power, Huadian Fuxin's (0816.HK, Buy, current price 3.18 HKD) valuation appears much more attractive at 9.4x15E PE and a preferred choice to get nuclear exposure.

On 6 August, unit 2 of Fuqing Nuclear (39%-owned by Fuxin) was successfully connected to grid, laying a solid foundation for incoming operation (within next three months). Together with the scheduled operation of unit 3 and 4 in early 2016 and 2017, we expect Nuclear to contribute 30% of Fuxin's net profit in 2017. While Fuxin's 1H15 earnings could be dragged by a poor 1Q, we expect its full-year earnings will pick up to 26% yoy and still able to achieve a 17% EPS CAGR in 14-17E even after factoring in 1) another 4% thermal tariff cut in 4Q15; 2) 13% thermal utilization drop in total in 2016-17E, and 3) two rounds of equity placement in 2015-16. Buy with a target price of HKD5.1
