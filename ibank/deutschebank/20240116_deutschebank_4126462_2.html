Implications for domestic economic policy

On the domestic side, the key watch point ahead will be the beginning of the new Legislative Yuan session, and where the new House Speaker and Deputy House Speaker will be elected. The dynamics there would indicate whether KMT and TPP are looking to convert their pre-poll understanding intoa more formal coalition. TPP leader Mr Ko has thus farsaidthat "in order to play the roleof a critical minority, the TPP will not work with a fixed side. We will cooperate with whoever is right."

What are likely to be the priorities of the Lai administration?

Reducing economic reliance on China. Since the pandemic and the introduction of US export controls on semiconductors to China, Taiwan exports to China have been on the decline - dropping from a peak of 30% in 2020 to 22% in 2023 (Fig ure 3). This is mainly due to the reduction of machinery exports (in which electronic exports make up a sizable portion) to China, which make up -70% of Taiwan's exports to China (Figure 4). Indeed, electronic exports to China (as a % of total machinery exports by Taiwan) have declined from 31% in 2020 to 23% in 2023 (Figure 5).

It is likely that this number will continue to fall, not just due to the tightening of US export controls on China, but also due to: (a) the ongoing diversification in electronics supply chains away from China towards countries like Vietnam and India; and (b) Taiwan's desire to reduce its economic reliance on China, particularly with China trying to limit Taiwan's exports to the Mainland.

Chinese authorities recently announced they would suspend tariff concessions on 12 types of petrochemical products from Taiwan. These products had enjoyed zero tariffs since 2013 as part of the so-called "Early-Harvest" list under the Economic Cooperation Framework Agreement (ECFA) signed in June 2010 between Taiwan and mainland China. According to China, more restrictions could be forthcoming. These include exports of agricultural, fishery, machinery, auto parts and textile products. Although for now these measures should have a limited impact on Taiwan's exports, since most of these products make up only a small part of Taiwan'sexportstoChina, the Taiwanese authorities will still likely take preemptive steps to reduce their dependence on the Chinese market.
