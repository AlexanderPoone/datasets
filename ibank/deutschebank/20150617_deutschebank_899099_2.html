China Insurance Sector

May 2015 premiums

Premium snapshot

Life premiums: China Life reported 5M15 life premiums of Rmb198,400m (+19.0% yoy), Ping An reported Rmb114,862m (+17.7%), CPIC Rmb51,466m (+1.5%), NCI Rmb62,475m (+4.9%), Taiping Life Rmb44,689m (+24.1%). and PICC Life and Health Rmb74,781m ( + 12.6%). For monthly premiums in May-2015, China Life achieved Rmb24,559m (+29.9% yoy), Ping An Rmb15,466m (23.2% yoy), CPIC Rmb7,618m (+8.7% yoy), NCI Rmb5,109m (-5.7% yoy), Taiping Rm4,349m(+20.8% yoy) and PICC Life + Health Rmb3,039m (-19.8% yoy).

P&C premiums: PICC reported May-2015 premiums of Rmb23,412m (+11.2% yoy), Ping An reported Rmb13,046m (+16.0%), CPIC Rmb7,873m (-2.1%), and CTIH Rmb1,289m (+23.4%). For 5M15, PICC had premiums of Rmb117,265m (+10.4% yoy), Ping An Rmb67,972m (+18.5%), CPIC Rmb40,444m ( + 1.0%) and Taiping Rmb6,312m (+20.5%).

Life premiums - mixed performance

May-2015 life premium performance was a mixed bag, with China Life, Ping An and Ch Taiping leading the growth while NCI and PICC Group recorded yoy declines. On a YTD basis, Ch Taiping has enjoyed the fastest growth at 24.1% for 5M15 (from 24.5% in 4M15), followed by China Life at 19.0% (from + 17.6%), Ping An at 17.7% (from 16.9%), PICC Group at 12.6% (from 14.5%), NCI at 4.9% (from 6.0%) and CPIC at 1.5% (from 0.3%).
