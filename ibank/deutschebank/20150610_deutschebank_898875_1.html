Company Update


More recapitalization exercises likely in next 3-4 weeks; be selective

More share placements or rights issues likely before July

In our report, "China Prop: High likelihood of recapitalization in May-July; be selective", of May 6, we argued that potential share placements presented an overhang for highly geared developers and that there had been a high amount of recapitalization exercises during previous bull markets. Since then, 11 listed developers (e.g. CR Land, Evergrande, Franshion, CIFI and SZI) have opted for recapitalization, raising about US$3.6bn. For the next 3-4 weeks, we expect more such recapitalization activities for other developers, before the 1H15 results blackout periods start in July. We advocate being selective and remain cautious on developers with high gearing or aggressive acquisition records.

Chance of recapitalization remains high for Sunac, Shimao, KWG, Evergrande During the 2007 bull market, 12 major developers came out with new equity issuances, raising more than US$6bn. In 2009, 29 key developers raised c.US$8.3bn in new equity. In 2015 YTD, excluding recapitalization related to asset injections, 15 developers have raised c.US$6.6bn. Despite recent share price consolidation, latest valuations generally have remained better than those before April 2015; hence, we still see a high chance of new equity issues for developers with high leverage or track record of aggressive acquisitions. On our analysis, developers with high leverage include Evergrande (even after recent share placement), Poly-A (new issue plan announced earlier), Shimao, Agile, and Poly HK. Developers with relatively aggressive land acquisition records in the past and/or those that have been prudent in acquiring new land bank in the past 24 months (hence higher potential to accelerate new land acquisitions) include Sunac and KWG. Developers with a relatively higher frequency of new equity raisings in the past include Hopson, Poly-A, Gemdale, and CRL (just raised).

We do not see much recapitalization overhang for the following developers On the other hand, we see fewer chances for share placements for the following developers in the near term: 1) COLI (as the company is completing the asset injection from the parent); 2) R&F and Wanda (as new equity issuances for these developers would require CSRC approval, which takes time, and R&F has resumed the application for an A-share IPO); 3) COGO, Sino Ocean, Greentown and CCRE (as the majority shareholders of these developers might not want to see their holdings diluted); 4) Vanke (given a strong balance sheet); and 5) Joy City, Franshion, CR Land and Beijing North Star (which already had recapitalization in 2015 to help improve their financial positions).

Be selective; more market consolidation likely in June

Our top picks are based on 5 investment themes: 1) quality players delivering consistent, solid growth - CSCEC, COLI, Joy City, CR Land, Franshion, Longfor; 2) high SOE reform/restructuring potential - Joy City, Greentown, Sino Ocean; 3) Tier-3/4 cities performing better than expected - COGO, Wanda, CCRE; 4) improving HK/Shanghai Grade A offices - SHKP, Hang Lung Prop; 5) stronger outlook for China retail property, given policy stimulus and positive wealth impacts from strong stock markets - Joy City, CR Land, HLP, Wanda. We remain cautious on developers with high leverage, records of aggressive acquisitions, or slowing growth, and we maintain a Sell on Sunac, Shimao, Evergrande, Country Garden, Yuexiu, Yanlord, Agile and Gemdale. We base target prices on NAV discounts. Risks: unexpected economic/policy volatility.
