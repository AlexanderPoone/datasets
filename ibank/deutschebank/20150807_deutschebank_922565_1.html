Company

Sunac China Holdings Limited


Muted July sales; expect lackluster 1 H15 earnings


Muted July contract sales; September to be more exciting

Sunac announced after-market yesterday muted July contract sales of RMB5.31b, 19% lower vs. June's RMB6.56b. July's property sales ASP was RMB18,960/sq m, down 15% vs. 1H levels of RMB22,280/sq m. Although the Shanghai Waitan 188 project launched with decent ASPs of ~RMB56,000/sq m, the presales amount was not high. Our channel checks suggest the average ticket price of that project was around RMB8-9m per unit. We believe this unimpressive launch was partly affected by the stock market volatility. We expect Sunac's sales to be robust in September, with the Beijing Nongzhanguan project launch planned.

Upcoming on-shore bond to help refinance some trust and entrusted loans

We expect Sunac to be able to lower its average costs of borrowing from 2014's 9.1% to 8.5% in 1H15. Furthermore, with the approval of on-shore corporate bond issuances of up to RMB6b (we est. at 5-6% cost), it could further reduce the average financing cost. We expect Sunac to use the proceeds from the upcoming corporate bond for refinancing as well as general working capital. Recall that Sunac has some trust loans and entrusted loans that carry around 9-10% interest rates.

Over R MB10b+ of acquisition activities YTD

Landbanking activities: Sunac did not acquire much land in 1H15 (5 projects with attributable land costs of ~RMB3.6b and attributable GFA of 683k sq m) while it was also sorting things out with Greentown and Kaisa. The company indicated that it is looking to further replenish landbank and seek smaller-sized acquisition opportunities. It is still keen to enter Shenzhen and Guangzhou as well as some strategic Tier-2 cities. Last month, the company entered into Chengdu with its acquisitions of some projects from CC Land for RMB3.2b. Together with the net cash outflow of transactions with Greentown for JV platform projects of RMB3.4b, we estimate Sunac's acquisition costs for land and projects to be over RMB10b, which looks alright vs. its contract sales size.

Potential buying opportunity if bond yields hike post-lackluster results

We believe there could be a potential buying opportunity of its bonds if their yields hike post results. In our view, Sunac's interim results will likely be lackluster with 1H2015 GPM similar to 2014's low levels and net debt/total equity rising to 70-75% in June from 45% as of end-2014. Strategy-wise, management seems to look for stable/mild growth on contract sales with a longer-term return to better profitability while balancing financial health. However, we see its GPM recovery as a 2016 story, at the earliest. Historically, Sunac's completion schedule has been skewed to the 2H and we expect this year to also see little GFA delivery in 1H vs. 2H. At the current juncture, we maintain our CreditHOLD recommendation on the Sunac curve. Upside risks include further policy stimulus, equity placement or introduction of valuable partners, and faster than expected property sales. Downside risks to our call include much more aggressive landbanking and slower than expected property sales.
