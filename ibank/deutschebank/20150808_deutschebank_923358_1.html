1 H15 inline; market share gains not enough to offset sliding margins

1H15 EPS of HKD0.23/sh, down 26% yoy on lower ASPs

CRC announced its 1H15 results with top line down 12% yoy and bottom line down 26% yoy. Stripping out HKD499m in tax write back (due to overprovision of 2013 and 2014 income tax), CRC's core earnings fell by 53% yoy. This suggests that volumes were quite strong for CRC, up 8% yoy but margins suffered as GP/t fell 40% yoy to HKD71/t. In 1H15, ASPs fell by 16% yoy due to new supply in South China, weak demand and cheaper cement inflows from nearby provinces. The company also announced an interim dividend of HKD0.06/sh, implying a dividend payout ratio of 26%, unchanged from FY14.

Market share gain continues in South China

During 1H15, CRC was able to utilize its low cost advantage to gain market share in its core markets of GD and GX (69% of sales). Sales volume in GD/GX grew by 11% yoy versus the market that grew by only 2% yoy. If we stripped out its Guizhou/Yunnan new lines rolled out in 2H14, organic volume growth for CRC was at 4%, besting the nationwide demand growth of -5.3%. However, competition for pricing was also fierce as GD ASPs fell by 18% yoy after a high base in 1H14. The lower cement ASPs did however help its concrete business which saw unit GP expand by 28% yoy to HKD120/cu.m.

Balance sheet remains healthy

Despite the poor operating conditions, CRC's balance sheet had remained strong with net gearing falling by 1 ppt to 55%. CRC has also acquired 0.6mt of Huying Cement and formed a JV with Kungang cement in 1H15. As CRC finishes its M&A with Kungang cement likely in 2H, net gearing could worsen. CRC’s AR days also increased slightly from 52 days in FY14 to 61 days due to tight credit.

Outlook

With the peak season approaching, price declines in Southern China may have come to an end. Producers in the PRD regions have announced a price hike of RMB20-30/t as of Aug 6. Demand has also gradually recovered with leading producers achieving a balance in sales and production, significantly better than other parts of China. Despite Conch's new 12,000t/d line in Yangchun rolled out in July, we believe prices will continue to see a mild recovery headed into 4Q with infrastructure demand picking up leading to better price coordination among leading players.

What to do with the stock?

Despite sluggish cement demand nationwide, CRC is still in a good position, having one of the strongest balance sheets among its peers, the highest utilisation rates and exposure to one of the better supply-demand regions of South China. Maintain Buy with TP of HKD5.37.
