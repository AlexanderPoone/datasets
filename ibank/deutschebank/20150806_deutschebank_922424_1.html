Proposed acquisition of 80.6% stake in Hanghui Expressway

Proposed acquisition of 80.6% stake in Hanghui (estimated I RR: 10.4%)

ZJE announced that it has agreed to purchase an 80.614% stake in Zhejiang Hanghui Expressway Co. Ltd. (Hanghui) from Zhejiang Communications Investment Group (ZJE's parentco) for a cash consideration of RMB 1,699m. The implied P/B is 5.8x P/B, which looks higher than ZJE's acquisition in Yongjin in 2013 (1.5x) and JSE's acquisitions in Ningchang Zhenli and Xiyi in 2015 (1x-1.7x). However, management guided it normally uses DCF to value its acquisition target. It considers the IRR ratio as most important (above 8%) for M&A. The highlights are (more details to be released on August 24):

  • 1.    Length: Hanghui Expressway in the Zhejiang Section of the Hangzhou-Ruili Expressway (G56), a four-lane expressway with a total length of 122.29km. Upon the completion of the acquisition before end-2015, the total length of expressways managed by ZJE will increase from c. 460km to 582km.

  • 2.    Concession period: The concession period of Hanghui Expressway will not be below 25 years (starting from 2004-06). It is still pending final approval by the relevant government authorities. Approval is expected before 30 September 2015.

  • 3.    Earnings: Hanghui recorded a net loss of Rmb67m in 2014 and ZJE expects it to become profitable in 2016 with the decline in interest expenses as ZJE will repay the debt of Rmb2bn on Hanghui's behalf.

  • 4.    IRR ratio: ZJE expects the deal to be completed by end-2015 pending the approval by its shareholders and Zhejiang SASAC. Management has guided for the IRR on this investment to be above 10.4% (vs Yongjin Expressway's 9.6%, which was acquired by ZJE in 2013).

Deutsche Bank view: ZJE’s valuation to be enhanced by 5-10%

We think the earnings impact is limited in 2015-2016 (only +/-1-2%), but we believe the acquisition will have a larger positive impact on ZJE's earnings in the long term. The unit construction price for this road was only c.Rmb42m per km (vs currently c. RmblOOm per km). By our rough estimate, ZJE’s valuation will increase by 5-10%, all else equal. In our view, the acquisition is a good move for ZJE and provides good indications of further investment in coming years. It has been two years since ZJE acquired Yongjin Expressway from its parentco in 2013. In addition, we expect rising M&A opportunities, if the amended regulation of toll road administration can be approved. A healthy cash position could also support its potential asset acquisitions. These projects could drive ZJE's long-term growth. We maintain our Buy rating, given its solid toll road business, attractive valuation and potential M&A opportunities. Catalysts include strong 1H15 results, Zheshang's A share IPO in 1H16 and potential extension of SHNE's concession period. ZJE is trading at a P/E of 10.1x 2016E, attractive on the back of 14% EPS CAGR in 2014-16 with 5-6% dividend yields.
