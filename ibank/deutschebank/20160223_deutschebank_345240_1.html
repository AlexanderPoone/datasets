Shanghai adjusted land supply rules for more small-medium sized units

Supply of small-medium sized units in city area will not be lowered than 70% As reported by mainland media Jiemian News, there is a document from the Shanghai City Government regarding the land supply in Shanghai. The extract of the document are as below:

The document mentioned that the city will increase the ratio of small and medium sized units (GFA under 100sqm) for the new land supply for commodity properties. For lands in city area, supply of small and medium sized units is required to be higher than 70% of the total. For lands in the outskirt area, the ratio is set at 60%. However, for those outskirt areas with serious supply-demand imbalance, the ratio of the supply on small and medium sized units is also required at 70%.

On the lands for social housings in city area, 100% of the unit supply has to be small and medium sized units; for areas in the outskirts, the ratio is 80%.

In addition, as to encourage the development of communities leading by public transportation, there is also related requirements: For the lands in areas of where the city railway station locates (in 600 sqm area in city area and in 1,500 sqm in the outskirts), the supply of small and medium sized units have to accounted for not less than 80% of the total units of supply.

Moreover, there is also adjustment for the land supply rules for commercial properties. For the commercial sites, developers have to hold at least 80%/40% of the commercial/office properties that to be developed, and the minimum holding period is 10 years. For areas that are with large supply recently, developers have to hold 100%/60% of the commercial/office properties and the holding period has to be over 10 years or for long term holding.

It is reported that the notice was issued on 14 February.

Essentially this shows that while we are seeing more policy loosening for the lower-tier cities that have inventory issues, we are seeing stricter policies and regulations on the Tier-1 cities where ASPs are rising quickly.
