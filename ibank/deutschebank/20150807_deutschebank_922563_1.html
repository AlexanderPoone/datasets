Juhuasuan commission discounts should support GMV growth

Alibaba will announce 2015 June quarter earnings on 12 Aug. We expect China retail GMV to total RMB682b (+36% YoY, +14% QoQ); Tmall GMV to rise by 60% YoY to 37% of total GMV; and mobile to grow by 107% YoY to contribute 50% of total GMV. The co's reduction in commission schedule for its Juhuasuan discounting channel leads us to believe that GMV should not disappoint. We assume an improved mobile monetization rate of 1.7% (+21 bps YoY), and a soft 3% take-rate for PC (-3bps YoY) in the Q, for a blended take rate of 2.35% (-17bps YoY). We expect net rev of RMB20.2b (+28% YoY/+16% QoQ) vs consensus of RMB21b. We assume no YoY rise in non-GAAP EBITDA margin at 54%, vs consensus at 51%, due to deep investment in ad exchange and various investees. Non-GAAP net income therefore should grow 29% YoY to RMB9.4b (+22% QoQ), 2% higher than consensus estimates.

Expectations management improving

We have seen encouraging improvement in investor communications and expectations-setting over the past few mos. We view the 0.7% cut in sellside rev expectations and 4.4% reduction in FY16 non-GAAP NP over the past three months (per Bloomberg consensus) as a possible precursor to a more sustainable beat on a quarterly basis. We seek further clarity on BABA's 50/50 joint venture with affiliate ANT Financial into O2O-focused "Koubei" (which is reportedly spending RMB6b in 020 subsidies, etc.)

Short term puts and takes

Alibaba's share price continues to flirt with a post-IPO low of USD76.21. While investors are keenly aware of the nearly 1.6b shares (63% of total shares outstanding) unlocking on Sept 19, we note that BABA could achieve a MSCI weighting of 2.6% as of Nov, 2015, and 4.8% in May, 2016, assuming a 25% free float. A resumption in lottery distribution would also be a positive catalyst (the ongoing suspension reduced Jun Q mobile revs -7-8%). Investors longer term are likely to focus on GMV share, particularly cast against continued strong growth at B2C rivals such as JD and VIPS, and what seems to be rapidly expanding mobile commerce on Tencent's Wechat platform. We maintain our Buy recommendation on what we view to be a very reasonable 2016 PE.
