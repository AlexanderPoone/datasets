FY15/1HFY15 results review/preview

Week of 10 August - Eclat, sportswear, Sun Art and WHG/Shuanghui results We expect Eclat's results to be ahead of our estimates but earnings growth to decelerate from 2H15. Meanwhile, several sportswear companies (Li Ning -loss of RMB142m for 1H15, as well as 361 and Peak) will announce their interim results, and we expect an across-the-board industry recovery, although we believe Anta should benefit most. We also expect Sun Art to report a 7% decline in NP given no macro improvement in 2Q on SSSg. We expect WHG's NPAT to decline 1% yoy (ex biological assets FV change), with sequential improvement in China, but tough comps in the US (high base in 2Q14). Top picks - Buy L'Occitane, Anta and Hengan; Sell Eclat and Li & Fung.

Anta's strong results show its strength plus industry recovery

Anta's 1H15 EBIT beat our estimate by 11%, mainly thanks to better GPM, sales and other income. We expect gross margin expansion in the apparel segment through 2016, driven by Fila, as well as a recovery in the footwear business. Store adjustment at the Anta brand has been faster than expected and should boost company operations. In-line SSSg and order book growth also point to a sustainable operational recovery. Potential M&A in 2H15 at the earliest may further adhere to Anta's multi-brand strategy and serve as a share price catalyst (assuming the acquisition valuation is reasonable).

Lifestyle -1H15 core EBIT in line; investors wonder how it can unlock value Although management commented that SSS for its CWB store in July was worse than in June, it targets positive SSS growth for full-year 2015, as July's performance alone is not sufficient to draw any conclusions about the year. Meanwhile, the number one question in almost every meeting was whether management will be able to unlock the value of the company's underlying property assets via privatization or any other means. Management explained that this is currently unlikely as: 1) regarding privatization, there is a lock-up period post-recent general offer; 2) the major shareholder has no requirement for cash personally; and 3) should its property assets be disposed of (especially for the CWB store), the company's market cap will be much smaller given the NP decline. Liquidity of the shares remains an issue.

Top picks - Buy L’Occitane, Anta and Hengan; Sell Eclat and Li & Fung

We have a Buy rating on L'Occitane (SSSg recovery and Euro tailwind), Anta (strong leader with industry tailwind to perform better than others), and Hengan (strong sell-through to support restocking in 2H15). We have a Sell rating on Eclat (we expect a good 2015 result on a low base but decelerating YoY growth in 2H15) and LF (we expect a weak 2015 with a market earnings downgrade post-1 H15 results).

Valuation and risks

We use DCF, P/E-to-growth, and relative P/E as the tools to value the sector. DCF captures the future cash flow of consumer companies, while PE/G and relative P/E show a company's relative value vs. peers. Downside risks: higher-than-expected raw material prices, SSSg failing to pick up, and intense competition. Upside risks: a greater-than-expected rise in overall SSSg or sales volume, higher operating leverage, and weaker-than-expected input costs.
