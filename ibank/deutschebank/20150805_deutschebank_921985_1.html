Asia Economic Diary: Aug 10 -14


Date

5 August 2015

Taimur Baig, Ph.D

Chief Economist (+65) 64238681 taimur. baig@db.com


The second week of August will feature monetary policy meeting in the Philippines and South Korea; we expect both the central banks to remain on hold. Malaysia's Q2 GDP (5.1% vs. 5.6%) will likely moderate as past month has seen a number of economic indicators like industrial production, manufacturing and motor vehicle sales weaken. Meanwhile, it will likely report a lower current account surplus of MYR9.5bn in Q2 (vs. MYRIO.Obn in Q1). GDP growth in Hong Kong (2.5% vs. 2.1%) is expected to rise as worst phase of decline in consumption is past. Singapore's Q2 GDP will likely moderate (1.5% vs. 1.7%) as chronic weakness persists in the manufacturing sector, while from expenditure side we expect a tad weaker contribution from consumption and net exports.

It is a key week for India as it releases data for CPI, IP and trade. CPI inflation (4.7% vs. 5.4%) is likely to moderate in July, while we expect a rise in IP growth (3.1% vs. 2.7%) in June. July exports (-13.5% vs. -15.8%) and imports (-14.8% vs. -13.4%) will likely continue to contract, printing a higher trade deficit of USD11.8bn (vs. -USD10.8bn). China will also report its IP (6.8%), retail sales (10.6%) and FAI (11.4%) data for July; we expect unchanged outturn in July for all the releases. Further, M2 growth will likely moderate to 11.6% in July from 11.8% in June.

Indonesia is likely to post a lower trade surplus of USD0.3bn in July (vs. USD0.5bn in June) as exports (-4.0% vs. -12.8%) continue to disappoint while imports (-6.0% vs. -17.4%) fall, albeit at a slower pace. Other important data releases include June retail sales for Singapore; we expect a moderation in both real (4.5% vs. 6.4%) and nominal (4.0% vs. 6.1%) terms. Malaysia’s June IP (6.1% vs. 4.5%) growth will likely rise, while manufacturing sales (-2.8% vs. -4.2%) will continue to contract, albeit at a slower pace.
