2Q beat; aggressive hiring to sink margins

2Q beat on EC performance; limited visibility on margin floor

SFUN delivered 2Q revs of US$211m (+25%YoY/+71%QoQ), beating DBe/consensus by 19%/5% on faster-than-expected ramp-up in e-commerce business, which GMV +3x QoQ. Non-GAAP OPM came in at 11% (-38%YoY), ahead of DBe by 4ppt and in-line with consensus. Non-GAAP NP of US$20m was ahead of DBe/consensus at US$13m/US$1 Om. The co raised FY15 top-line guidance from 15% to 20%YoY on the back of sprouting new transactionbased EC business. In return, the co plans massive hiring (>40K by YE15) and light monetization for market share gains. We expect continued margin erosion in 2H15. Maintain Hold.

New EC biz well on track; legacy business performed better than expected The new primary EC business is well on track with GMV up by 169% QoQ and the revenue contribution hitting 42% of total primary EC revs. Higher-margin in-house sales contributed more than 70% of total primary EC revenue in 2Q (vs 60% in 1Q), and the subsidy to home-buyer remained stable at 40%. Legacy primary marketing and secondary business performed better than expected (beating DBe by 5%/36%) thanks to a macro tailwind.

Secondary EC ramping nicely; introducing strategic investor

The secondary EC business saw 8x sequential GMV growth in 2Q, primarily driven by 1) continuous aggressive pricing strategy (0.5% commission vs 2% for offline agencies), 2) sales force expansion (added 4k sales agents in 2Q, totaling 7,000) and 3) macro tailwind and favorable policy to encourage transactions. Meanwhile, the co plans to introduce new strategic investors, led by Carlyle and IDG, injecting US$1 b (50%/50% for new shares/CB) to finance the new transaction-driven initiatives. The implied equity dilution (excluding CB) is -16% on a diluted basis.

Aggressive hiring plan to sink margins

The co's total headcount reached 30k as of 2Q15 ( + 13k QoQ), with the majority of net additions coming from secondary and rental segments. The co significantly revised up its total headcount target for YE15 to be >40k (vs. 26k previously), with most new recruits focusing on e-commerce initiatives. Given the co's NT priority on market share, we expect such hiring plan to weigh significantly on margins. We lower our FY15/16/17E non-GAAP OPM forecasts by 0.5ppt/1.5ppt/0.7ppt to 7.7%/12.5%/16.8%, respectively.

Tweak down TP by 1% to $7.2; maintaining HOLD

We raise our FY15/16/17 net revenue forecasts by 5%/2%/2%, while cutting non-GAAP net income by 7%/5%/1%. Our new target price of US$7.2 is based on an unchanged 0.8x PEG against FY15-17E non-GAAP EPADS CAGR of 78% (vs. prior at 65%), implying 62x/31x PE in FY15/16E. Maintain Hold. Key risks: faster-than-expected monetization of e-commerce; significant margin retreat.
