Silvergrass lining


The high returning Silvergrass mine is underway

The delivery of Rio's Pilbara brownfield mine strategy at US$9/t has played a big part in the US$1.5b capex guidance reduction over 2015 and 2016. The new guidance includes a smarter, capital-light development of the Silvergrass mine, increasing Pilbara system mine capacity to 360Mtpa in our view. Rio confirmed that mining of Silvergrass is already underway although approval of the larger 20Mtpa Phase 2 will be in 2016. The revised Silvergrass project should save Rio over US$1 b and will fill the remaining 30Mtpa of spare infrastructure delivering an IRR of over 20% at just US$45/t Fe. Rio remains our top pick in the sector, an undervalued stock with high cash returns. Buy.

Silvergrass should cost less than US$1 b

Rio stated with its 1H15 results that a development decision on Silvergrass will not be required until 2016. However our industry channel checks suggest that the early starter mine at Silvergrass was awarded to a contractor in late July and Rio confirmed that a trucking operation to Nammuldi had commenced already. We now think Silvergrass has been split into two stages; a 10Mtpa mobile crushing and contract mining operation with ore haulage from Silvergrass back to Nammuldi, and a second phase 20Mtpa conveyor and fixed plant project which we think will be approved by the board in 1H16. We also think that by using niche contractors, together with lower labour rates and the weaker AUD that Silvergrass can be built in just 9 months and for well under the current US$1 b guidance, much lower than the original US$2b budget. This should be a good news story; lower capex, hitting the 360 production target in 2017, increasing project returns, and maintaining the Pilbara blend. Our analysis shows that Silvergrass generates an IRR of 60% at our long run US$66/t Fe and over 20% at USS45/t.

Lower opex and capex = more free cash flow

Operating costs in the Pilbara dropped to US$16.2/t in 1H15. Rio stated that at current spot AUD and diesel, unit costs are around US$15/t. We expect unit costs to fall to US$11/t in 2017 (nominal) driven by a weaker AUD (we forecast 63c in 2017), economies of scale, a reduction in overheads, and automation. The lower opex, and capex savings, should lead to a significant boost in FCF. Even at below spot Rio is trading on a 6% FCF yield and can hold gearing flat.


Valuation and risks

Our PT is set broadly in-line with our A$70.89/sh DCF-derived valuation.

Downside risks include weaker commodity prices and higher costs (refer p.8).
