Key takeaways from analyst briefing


Kunlun released an in-line 1H15 results last night and held an analyst briefing this morning. After a 39% earnings decline in 1H15, we see little improvement in the near-term given the still low oil price that is negative to both its E&P and downstream LNG sales, and continued over-supply risks in LNG liquefaction plant (loss could be enlarged when the new plants are transferred from workin-progress). Although management indicated strategic shift to more downstream business, the ramp up will take a while to bear the fruit. We expect a 30% yoy decline in full-year 2015E earnings. Potential asset injection of Kunlun Gas could be a positive but the pricing term is less likely as favorable as previous injections. Meanwhile, city gas distribution business is also facing challenges of weak volume growth. Maintain Hold.

1H15 net profit down 39% yoy; or 49%<38% of DBe/consensus FY15E

Kunlun's 1H15 net profit were down 39% yoy to HKD1,921m, which accounts for 49%/38% of DBe/consensus full-year estimates. The earnings decline was mainly due to 1) an Rmb133m loss in E&P given a 46% decrease in realized crude ASP (vs. Rmb844m gain in 1H14); 2) an Rmb98m loss in natural gas sales, which is attributable to a substantial fall in gas selling price under cheaper oil (vs. Rmb494m gain in 1H14), 3) an 33% profit decline in LNG terminal, though partially offset by 4) an 17% yoy increase in contributions from the natural gas pipeline business.

Briefing takeaways: eyes on potential Kunlun Gas injection

Transmission: In 1H15, transmission volume was up by 6.8% yoy to 15.7bcm. Excluding the robust 26% yoy growth in Beijing (43% of total transmission volume), implied volume for ex-Beijing regions should see a 4.2% yoy decline (mainly Shanxi/Hebei). Under the slowing demand, the construction of Shaan-Jing Pipeline IV is also suspended. Besides, management admits that a transmission tariff cut is not impossible for Shaan-Jing Pipeline l&ll despite still unclear timing. During 1H15, the higher unit transmission tariff is due to change in volume mix (more supply to Beijing/Tianjin which bear higher transmission fee).

M&A: Management reaffirmed that the company position as PetroChina's downstream platform will not change and that the asset injection of Kunlun Gas is only a matter of time and won’t wait for long. On the other hand, the injection of Caofeidian Terminal is unlikely in the near-term given the poor profitability (e.g. utilization of existing Dalian/Rudong terminal is only at 8%/22%).

Strategy: Stake sells in some LNG liquefaction plants is also being considered to optimize its portfolio amid severe oversupply situation. Kunlun will be more proactive on 1) city gas distribution, likely via JV or acquisition, 2) gas-fired power generation, and 3) development in vessel refilling stations. Capex spent in 1H15 was HKD3.2bn, and capex in 2H15 should be at similar scale. Capex will be spent more on developing high end downstream market.
