FY16 outlook - the tough get going;

maintaining Buy

We lower FY16/17 forecasts by 24/26% and introduce our FY18 forecast

Our revisions reflect weaker SSS assumptions and operating deleverage in HK. Despite CTF's efforts to renegotiate rental, the rental ratio will likely stay high. Management hopes to reduce costs in other areas. We believe SSSG should improve in 2HYF16 given the base effect. We maintain our positive view that PRC consumers prefer HK jewellery brands, especially for gem-set, and CTF will benefit. We cut ourtarget price by 20%to HK$9.52 but maintain Buy.


FY16 - focus on cost control for both HK and China markets.

FY15 results were broadly in line with DBe/market forecasts, but were helped by a lower effective tax rate. Looking into FY16, as SSSG remains volatile, CTF is reducing its new store openings from 180-200 to 150-160 stores for China. As for HK, it plans to close down stores that it fails to renew with a lower rent, so as to minimise the impact of operating deleverage. It is looking into the cost structure (A&P, staff per store ratio, etc) and some benefits have already been reflected in 2HFY15, with opex declining by 3% yoy.

HK remains a travel destination; PRC consumers prefer HK jewellery names We believe weak SSS in HK, especially in 2HFY15, is partly due to a high base effect and local demonstrations, but, more importantly, currency weakness is helping popular travel spots like Japan and Europe. We thus believe that conditions will normalise as they did after the FY14 gold rush (but in a different direction) and SSSG will improve in 2HFY16. In the longer term, we maintain our view that PRC consumers prefer HK jewellery brands to locals, especially when they trade up to gem-sets. Separately, the three most frequently asked questions when management attended our DB Global consumer conference in Paris were: 1) Will HK remain a travel destination for PRC consumers?; 2) When does CTF expect SSS to recover?; and 3) Store closure and rental renewal in HK.


Reduced target price of HK$9.52; maintaining Buy

We maintain Buy as we remain positive on CTF: it is one of the beneficiaries of the secular trend of consumers trading up for gem-set with the emergence of the middle income class. We base our DCF-derived target price on a COE of 8.6%, beta of 1.0 and long-term growth rate of 2.5%. Key downside risks: expansion risk; management execution; and overexpansion.
