2Q preview: 020, mobile games & ads


Focus on mobile games; seeking recovery plan in wake of FWJ, other titles Tencent will announce 20 results on 12 Aug. We expect net revs to total RMB23.4B (+19% YoY/+4%QoQ), vs consensus of RMB24b (+22%YoY). We expect revs from mobile games, incl YingYongBao, to reach RMB4.6b (+4% QoQ) on a gross basis. We could see possible downside to this assumption given the run-away success of desktop-to-mobile reincarnations of competing games such as Netease's FWJ title. While Tencent maintains a healthy pipeline of casual mobile games, it is not clear how long it will take for the company to respond definitively to the rise of competing self-developed mid and hardcore mobile games. A simple grafting of titles such as Dungeon Fighter and Crossfire could indeed get the company on the map. These two desktop games however are licensed, and thus conversion of these titles and others could take a few-to-several quarters.

Mobile ads likely strong as expected; still very early days with Moments

We expect mobile P4P ad revenue to reach RMB1.37b driven by strong growth of Mobile Q-zone and WeChat official account ads. We meanwhile expect little upside surprise from the more embryonic Moments ads business. While the opportunity is massive, we believe that ad deployment on Moments remains highly manual in nature, largely to protect user engagement. PC games should see mild YoY growth of 13% YoY (or -2.7% QoQ) to RMB 10.2b due to 2Q seasonality. We expect non-GAAP EBIT of RMB10.4b (+36% YoY/+4%QoQ); non-GAAP net income should reach RMB7.8b (+33% YoY/+11% OoQ), vs consensus of RMB7.5b (+28%YoY).

Are subsidies on the rebound?

One of the many building attractions to Tencent in 1H related to a cessation in subsidies amongst investees and associates (taxi-hailing investee Didi Taxi merged with rival Kuaidi Taxi, and online classified leader 58.com bought #2 Ganji). We now envision upside risk to marketing spend associated with subsides in the wake of a growing arms race in areas of offline-to-online (020) investment such as food & groceries delivery, and group-buying in general. Baidu's recent dramatic scaling up in 020 investment is likely merely the latest salvo in a longer-term race to win 020. We specifically view Tencent investees Didi-Kuaidi, Dianping, ele.me and others as possible channels of heightened marketing spend.

Maintain Buy and TP

We view any further weakness in share price relating to the risks profiled above as attractive opportunities to invest in one of China's most persuasive long-term Internet stories.
