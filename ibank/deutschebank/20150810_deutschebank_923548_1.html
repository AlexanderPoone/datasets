Lowering retail price in China for higher demand

Lowering retailer prices in China from 1st Aug

The company announced on 29 Jul that it will lower part of the retail prices of its products in China from 1st Aug. This is a move to help sales in China. Retail price of some of the products will be lowered by 10% on average but some as be as high as 31%. Although management commented that lowering of import tariff on certain cosmetic products from 1 Jun 2015 has limited impact to its cost, the company is supporting Chinese government's policy and adjust retail price of certain products. This is a move to boost domestic consumption.

Deutsche Bank view - an excuse to reset retail price in China

L’Occitane is not the first cosmetic brand that announced an adjustment in its retail price in China, L’Oreal (OREP.PA. Hold), Estee Lauder (ELN, Buy), SKII and Shiseido (4911.T, Buy) adjusted by 10-20% over the past 2 months. AmorePacific (090430.KS, Buy)'s Innisfree and Shuwhasoo also reported retail price reduction of as much as 12%, according to HK Economic Journal on 25 Jul.


We believe the move by most industry players is to take China's tariff cut as an excuse to adjust their global pricing hierarchy, which has been distorted by the recent global currency movement. It is a brand's interest to balance its retail price and brand equity especially in developing market. It also targets to eliminate parallel imports on websites like taobao or JD.com (JD.OQ. Hold) in our view. While GPM might be lowered in China as a result, it is expected to be offset by increase in regular demand.
