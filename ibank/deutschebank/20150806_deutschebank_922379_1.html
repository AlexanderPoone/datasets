Daily prices and news: US coal exports fell to a five-year low

Industrials (/lb): aluminum $0.72 (-1.4%), copper $2.35 (-1.1%), lead $0.78 (-0.6%), molybdenum $6.10 (+1.5%); nickel $4.93 (+0.3%); zinc $0.86 (-1.3%); Precious (/oz): gold $1,085 (-0.3%), silver $14.60 (0.0%), Steel (/mt): China HRC $361 (+0.2%), SHFE Rebar $337 (+0.4%), MB Scrap Index $230 (+0.0%); Bulks: iron ore (/dmt) $57 (+2.7%), coking coal (/mt) $86 (-0.9%), freight (BCI) 2,604 (+3.7%); Energy: Brent (/bbl) $49.59 (-0.8%), WTI (/bbl) $45.15 (-1.3%), natural gas (/mmBtu) $2.80 (-0.5%), thermal coal (/t) $60 (+1.4%).

US coal exports fell to a five-year low in June of 5.8m st (-33% YoY), reflecting the expiration of long-term contracts not being replaced by new sales on weak pricing and stronger USD. 1H15 exports totaled 41.7m st (-20% YoY). (Argus)

India may import 900-1,000 tons of gold in 2015-16 compared to 945 tons in 2014-15 with July imports expected to be 70-75 tons and August imports to be >75 tons, according to gold refiner MMTC-PAMP. MMTC-PAMP is a joint venture between the Indian government and a Swiss metals refiner. (Reuters)

China closes -550 small coal mines in 1H15 with ~34Mtpa of combined capacity (targeting to shut 1,131 mines in 2015 with a combined capacity of +64Mtpa) to ease domestic oversupply situation and address safety concerns. However, as many of these complexes are producing below nameplate capacity, the impact on China's 3.8bn tpa output will be minimal. (Argus)

Japan plans to restart its first nuclear reactor (890MW Sendai 1) by midAugust (after a two-year break), which is expected to impact coal consumption in the short term. The unit is one of three reactors scheduled to come on stream in coming months (890MW Sendai 2 in October and 890MW Ikata 3 by YE15) lowering coal demand by 700ktpm. (Argus)

US hot-rolled coil price (HRC) remained flat WoW at $467/st, for week ending 8/5. However, Chinese domestic HRC price was up $6/t WoW to $346/t (exwarehouse, Shanghai), on output controls to be put in place before September alongside some speculative actions from traders. QTD average US HRC price is $466/st vs. $452/st and $530/st in 2015 and 1015, respectively. (CRU)

US ferrous scrap prices likely down $20-30/lt MoM. Early transactions for August appear to have settled at $250/lt for No. 1 busheling (vs $270/lt in July) and at $230/lt for shredded auto scrap ($265/lt) in Chicago. Pressured by Canadian prime imports, Detroit No. 1 busheling prices slid to $241/st ($271/It) while shredded scrap settled at $240/lt ($260/lt). (AMM, SBB)

Vale plans to raise $287m from debt sale to expand Carajas railway. Company intends to sale infrastructure notes in two portions, with maturities of five and seven years and could increase it by another $100m. (Reuters)

Noranda's New Madrid aluminum smelter (253ktpa) remains in production following an explosion that suspended production at attached billet casthouse (91 ktpa). Extent of damage and restart of casthouse still unknown. (Company)
