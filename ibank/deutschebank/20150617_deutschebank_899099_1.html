May premium - mixed Life; slight pick-up in P&C

Agency-led growth in Life, better growth in P&C

Life premium growth ranged from -19.8% to 29.9% in May (vs. -6.6% to 32.5% in Apr), with China Life leading the way. China Life, Ping An, Ch Taiping and CPIC recorded positive growth, which we believe was driven by strong agency business. NCI and PICC Group, on the other hand, recorded negative growth. P&C growth saw a slight pick-up, ranging from -2.1% to 23.4% (vs. -3.1% to 15.0% in Apr). We reiterate our preference for Life over P&C on expected pricing pressure in auto insurance. Top Picks are CPIC and Ch Taiping.

Life - mixed performance

China Life led May growth at 29.9% (vs. 30.3% in Apr-15) driven by strong agency sales with the company's renewed focus on agency business. Ping An followed at 23.2% (+21.9%), Ch Taiping at 20.8% (+10.8%), CPIC at 8.7% (+10.6%), NCI at -5.7% (-6.6%), PICC Group at -19.8% (+32.4%). According to Taiping disclosure, the May-15 growth was mainly driven by the individual channel with single premium +119.7% yoy and regular +24.9% On a YTD basis, Ch Taiping leads at 24.1% yoy (vs. 24.5% in 4M15), followed by China Life at 19.0% (+17.6%), Ping An at 17.7% (+16.9%), PICC Group at +12.6% (+14.5%), NCI at 4.9% (+6.0%) and CPIC at +1.5% (+0.3%).

P&C - slight acceleration

P&C growth slightly accelerated in May-15 with Ch Taiping leading at 23.4% (vs. 15.0% in Apr-15), followed by Ping An at 16.0% (+14.1%), PICC at 11.2% (+7.7%), and CPIC at -2.1% (-3.1%). CPIC’s continued negative growth from March to May was, we believe, a result of its stricter underwriting discipline, which should translate into margin improvement, in our view. In terms of YTD performance, Ch Taiping led at 20.5% yoy (vs. 19.8% in 4M15), followed by Ping An at 18.5% (+19.1%), PICC at 10.4% (+10.2%) and CPIC at 1.0% (+1.8%). We believe P&C insurers will face near-term price pressure, given the auto insurance deregulation, potential competition from new online insurers such as Zhong An and price comparison websites.

Prefer Life to P&C

Chinese insurance sector share prices have declined an average of ~6% in the last month and the sector is now trading at 1.3x 2015E P/EV and 5.8x NB multiples. We remain positive on the sector outlook, and we prefer CPIC for its relatively attractive risk-reward. We also have Buy ratings on Ch Taiping, NCI and Ping An. We have Hold ratings on China Life and PICC P&C on valuation grounds. Investment risks include a significant correction in China's A-share markets, asset quality risks and weaker-than-expected VNB growth. The upside risks to our Hold ratings on China Life, PICC P&C and PICC Group are a significant rally in A-share markets and stronger-than-expected VNB/earnings growth.
