Lower asset quality, but better capital.

Upgrade to Hold

Much work to do, but shares are closer to fair value. Upgrade to HOLD

1H15 results missed our earnings ests, particularly driven by higher LLPs than expected. Despite this, capital was a strong beat vs expectations, at 11.5% CET1 vs. 10.7% at 2H14. The group continues to face issues: a low RoE (5.4%), declining income, and rising NPLs which are not expected to reverse soon. It appears to us the renewed focus on improving capital (via lower RWAs & balance sheet) is impacting returns. Creating a sustainable model for returns and capital will be a key target for the group's strategy update before year end. But given the strong beat in capital in 1H15, and shares which have underperformed, we raise our rating from Sell to Hold, and our TP to HK$117.

Capital ahead of expectations, reducing risk of capital raise

Despite higher provisioning in the quarter, capital beat significantly, printing at 11.5% vs our forecast of 11.0% and 10.7% at the end of last year, driven mainly by RWA reductions (6% lower than expected). New CEO Winter's outlook statement makes it clear that no decisions have yet been taken on whether they will seek additional capital, but in our view the strong capital generation in 1H15 reduces the risk. We expect the final decision will be announced at the bank's strategy day (due before the end of the year), when the group should have more clarity on the outcome of the BoE's more EM-focused stress test.

Asset quality deteriorates, no near-term reversal likely

Asset quality deteriorated during 1H15, with $1.7bn loan impairments, NPLs rising to $8.7bn / 3.1% of loans and the coverage ratio slightly higher at 54%. New CEO Winters acknowledged some past failures in the bank risk management framework, made it clear that the impairment provisions in 1H15 were not a 'kitchen sink' but a reflection of current trends, and said that the increase in NPLs was a continuation of adverse trends which show no signs of reversing. We forecast impairments to remain elevated over the next 18 months.

Valuation and risks, TP to HK$117, upgrade to HOLD

Our 201 5 earnings forecasts fall reflecting the 1H15 miss and downgrades to 2H15 earnings. 2016 adjusted earnings rise 1%, 2017 fall 1%. This leaves Standard Chartered trading at 1x 2015 TNAV, 13x 2016 earnings. Our SoTP-derived TP (which no longer includes an assumed capital raise) rises to HK$117 from HK$104. Given limited downside to our target price, we upgrade to a Hold rating. The business continues to face issues in improving returns, and on asset quality outlook, but the stronger capital ratio in 1H15 means we now see the shares as close to fair value. Key upside risks are much stronger-than-forecast capital formation, a bid for SC, or significant improvement in EM growth and commodity price prospects. Key downside risks: deterioration in asset quality, slower EM growth, weaker commodity prices, higher regulatory requirements.
