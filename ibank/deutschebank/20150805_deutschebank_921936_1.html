O&G capex cut, but upping 2015-16 production; Mining still under review

Freeport-McMoRan cuts 2016-17E O&G capex guidance by $1.8bn, while increasing 2015-16 O&G production by 1-8%. This, in an effort to enhance O&G cash flows amid challenging price environment and strengthen balance sheet position (net debt at $20bn as of 2015, net debt/LTM EBITDA at 3.Ox). Flowing revised production figures through our model would increase 2015-2016E EBIFDA by 1-2%, which in addition to capex reductions lower our $21.6bn 2017E net debt by ~$2bn and increase $21/share NAV by $1.5. However, we note aforementioned benefits would largely be offset should current spot prices of copper, molybdenum and oil remain unchanged as they are lower than DBe current price deck. Nonetheless, today's news enhances O&G story ahead of potential IPO of a minority interest being pursued with a September target. Management is still contemplating other actions fie, asset sales, strategic partnerships) to self-fund O&G and next up, we expect a full Mining operation review and cost/capex cuts of similar magnitude likely to be announced with or before 3015 results.

Freeport is deferring investments in several long-term O&G projects; but revised estimated start-up of initial production from its recent drilling success in the Horn Mountain area to 2016 (from prior 2017). Hence, 2016E O&G production would be 8% higher than prior guidance and would increase our company 2016E EBITDA of $7.8bn by 2%. More importantly, 2016-17E O&G capex cut by $900m/year (or $1.8bn in total) to $2bn/year, which under DBe Brent oil of $70/bbl in 2016 and $75/bbl in 2017, division would be able to largely self-fund (not considering interest or taxes) from estimated EBITDA of $1.4bn and $1.7bn, respectively. However, assuming Brent oil ©spot of $50/bbl, yearly O&G EBITDA would be ~$680m lower on average, suggesting division would need additional cash in 2016 and 2017 to fund capex.

Management is undergoing a review of plans at each Mining operation. Revised plans will target lower operating and capital costs, as production at certain operations is challenged by current low commodity prices. Freeport expects to complete this review shortly and will report its revised plans with or before 3Q15 results. During the 2008-09 downturn, company announced copper production reductions to the tune of 200m lbs (or 5% of then production) in next full year and of 500m lbs (11%) in following year, while embarking on an aggressive cost cutting program. Back then, production cuts came largely from reduced operating rates at several North American operations. Latest 2016 copper production guidance at 5.4bn lbs at a net cash cost of ~$1.25/lb (from ~$1.55/lb in 2015). Should Freeport cut its 2016 copper production guidance by 5%, it would reduce DB’s estimated market surplus by -22% (a 10% would cut surplus by 45%), taking surplus relative to estimated consumption to 1.8% (under 5% cut scenario) or 1.3% (under 10% cut scenario) from 2.4%, all else equal.
