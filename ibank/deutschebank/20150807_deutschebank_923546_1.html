As a note to our readers, this will be the last Commodities Weekly for August before we resume publication on Friday, September 4th.

Overview: As commodities reach new multi-year lows on a total returns basis and multi-decade lows relative to equities, fundamentals remain broadly weak. Therefore we the sector is susceptible to any further deterioration in indices measuring manufacturing and industrial activity which remain narrowly in expansion at 51. Out of twenty commodities, only six are now above their 2000-2014 averages in real terms.

Energy: US crude oil imports have fallen to more normal levels and crude inventory draws have returned to the expected pace although this would still leave late September inventory at 14% or 50 mmbbl above the 5Y average. The positive news is that the WTI 2016 curve is now low enough to make only 36% of the US tight oil universe attractive for new investment, down from 56% in late July, implying a greater likelihood of continued supply discipline on a 1-year time horizon if prices remain at the current level.

Precious Metals: The impending September US rate hike continues to weigh on the gold market reflected by the continuing outflow of metal from the ETF's. We continue to see downside risks to the metal, tempered by short periods of price recovery during periods of USD weakness.

Industrial Metals: The base metal complex continues to attract renewed short interest as the outlook for China's economic growth remains muted. Industrial action at some of the Chilean copper mines could offset the weaker demand, keeping the market roughly balanced for 2015.
