No macro recovery, performance to be product-driven

1H15 NPAT of RMB687m, beating DBe RMB602m and consensus RMB585m UPC reported RMB687m NPAT for 1H15 (+93% yoy) with RMB12bn revenue (-3% yoy), beating DBe NPAT/revenue of +70%/-4%, and consensus NPAT of RMB585m. Net of 1Q15 reported NPAT of RMB369m, UPC 2Q15 NPAT would have been RMB318m (+172% yoy). Noodle/milk tea/RTD tea sales were -7%/-18%/-6%, partly offset by strong "Haizhiyan" and "Classmate Xiaoming" sales (over RMB1.2bn). A 5.3ppt GPM improvement and higher interest capitalization contributed to the better-than-expected bottom line.


Performance to be product-driven in 2H15

UPC didn't see any sign of a macro recovery in July, and performance is product-driven. In 2H15, UPC expects the strong momentum of "Haizhiyan" and "Classmate Xiaoming" to continue. It expects mid- to high-end beverages to contribute 25% of beverage sales (2014: 10%), and mid- to high-end noodles to account for 7-8% of its noodle sales in 2015. Management sees low risk of input cost inflation in 2H15, but expects to spend more on A&P in 3Q15.

Capex to remain heavy

UPC plans RMB2.5bn capex for 2015, and expects it to fall to c. RMB2bn p.a. in future, which we think is still quite heavy. The company will hold a full postresults briefing on 19 August in Hong Kong, and we will follow up with an update on the longer-term industry and company outlook.

NPAT and TP revised up

We revise up our 2015-17E NPAT forecast by 7.3-7.8% mainly on margin improvement, and accordingly, raise our DCF-based TP by 7.6% to HKD7/share (3.9% RFR; 5.6% ERP; 1.0 beta; 10% D/DE; 2% TG). We like UPC’s branding capability, but at the current price we don't see much upside/downside and maintain Hold. Key upside risks: better-than-expected new product launch(es). Key downside risks: input cost hikes.
