THEME OF THE DAY

Standard Chartered - Lower asset quality, but better capital. Upgrade to Hold (2888.HK, Hold, Target Price: HKD 117.00, Closing Price: HKD 114.50, David Lock)

1H15 results missed our earnings ests, particularly driven by higher LLPs than expected. Despite this, capital was a strong beat vs expectations, at 11.5% CET1 vs. 10.7% at 2H14. The group continues to face issues: a low RoE (5.4%), declining income, and rising NPLs which are not expected to reverse soon. It appears to us the renewed focus on improving capital (via lower RWAs & balance sheet) is impacting returns. Creating a sustainable model for returns and capital will be a key target for the group's strategy update before year end. But given the strong beat in capital in 1H15, and shares which have underperformed, we raise our rating from Sell to Hold, and our TP to HK$117. (David Lock - 44 20 754-11521)

Great Wall Motor - Cutting earnings and TP on weak sales; D/G Great Wall-A to Sell (Vincent Ha)

Great Wall Motor's (GWM) H-shares price fell by 34.2% since July 2015 (vs. HSCEI Index's 14.5% correction), mainly due to market concerns on slowing sales growth amid intensifying SUV segment competition and weaker-than-expected preliminary 1H15 results (announced on 27 July), in our view. On 6 August, the company announced that they sold 47,445 units of vehicles in July, implying a 1.7% YoY decline. More importantly, GWM's SUV business, which has been the stronghold of the company, only recorded 12.8% YoY growth (vs. 48.7% in 1H15) to 39,931 units, with a 13.0% YoY decline in sales volume of the industry leading Haval H6 model (including H6 Coupe). In summary, the weakening sales momentum at Great Wall Motor echoes ours and market worries that GWM's SUV segment leadership is fading (Vincent Ha - 852 2203 6247)
