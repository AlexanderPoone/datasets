THEME OF THE DAY

China Gas Utilities - Too early to turn positive; maintaining Hold for all except BEHL (Michael Tong)

We expect only a moderate volume recovery for gas distributors after a planned price cut which may reduce price disadvantage rather than restore advantage when demand grew at high-teen. Industrial demand recovery depends more on economy improvement while recovery from direct supply users (i.e. power) are less relevant. The gas utilities stocks are trading at an avg. 17x 2015E P/E, still not attractive with a slower earnings CAGR during 2014-17E vs. 2011-14. Beijing Enterprise (BEHL) is our only Buy-rated stock due to resilient growth, zero connection and cheaper valuation. (Michael Tong -852 2203 6167) http://pull.db-qmresearch.eom/p/1626-6967/5480867/0900b8c089fbe79c.pdf

Anta - From strength to strength; reiterating Buy (2020.HK, Buy, Target Price: HKD 21.50, Closing Price: HKD 19.34, Anne Ling)

Anta's 1H15 EBIT beat DBe by 11%, mainly thanks to better GPM, sales and other income. We expect gross margin expansion in the apparel segment through 2016, driven by Fila, as well as a recovery in the footwear business. Store adjustment at the Anta brand has been faster than expected and should boost company operations. In-line SSSg and orderbook growth also point to a sustainable operational recovery. Potential M&A in 2H15 at the earliest may further meet Anta's multi-brand strategy and serve as a share price catalyst (assuming the acquisition valuation is reasonable). We have slightly increased 16/17E net profit and maintain our target price at HKD21.5; Buy. (Anne Ling -852 2203 6177) http://pull.db-qmresearch.eom/p/1836-F9EB/5698171/0900b8c08a014a0b.pdf

Anta - 1H15 result first take (2020.HK„ Buy, Target Price: HKD 21.50, Closing Price: HKD 19.92, Anne Ling)

Ante reported 1H15 EBIT of RMB1.3bn, up 22.7% HoH and 28.5% YoY. The result is ahead of our higher-than-consensus forecast. We attribute the strong result to better than expected sales and gross margin. Anta's 1H15 sales advanced by 6.4% HoH and 24.0% YoY while gross margin expanded by 1.5ppt HoH and 1.5ppt YoY. Anta recognized higher-than expected income tax and lower-than-expected net interest income, leading to a less significant beat at the bottom-line (vs. EBIT). Anta's 1H15 net profit increased by 7.6% HoH and 20.2% YoY. (Anne Ling - 852 2203 6177) http://pull.db-qmresearch.eom/p/1804-699F/90471436/0900b8c08a029d4a.pdf
