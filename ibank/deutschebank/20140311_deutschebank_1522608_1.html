2013 results beat; mgmt concerned over 2014 industry supply


2013 results above our expectation

Net profit of US$47m was above our forecast of US$11m. Management did a commendable job of reversing the US$15m loss it registered in 1H2013. OOIL registered an EBIT margin of 2.3% in 2H13 versus 0.1%in 1H13. The performance over 2H was also better than most peers in the industry.

Load factor improvement was key

Management credited the 2H results on a better balancing of load factor and rates. Load factor of 72% in 1H2013 improved to 75% in 2H13.

Over capacity in 2014

Management expect demand growth in 2014 to outpace that of 2013, and this should help absorb the additional capacity. January volume and rates were encouraging but it is too early to draw any conclusion. The company has an improved 2014 industry outlook (versus 2013) for the three major trade lanes (Transpacific, Asia - Europe and Intra-Asia).

Our view

We too worry about oncoming supply in the container shipping industry, which is why we prefer the dry bulk shipping industry. We have a Buy rating on OOIL because we think it is inexpensive at 0.6x 2014E PB. We still consider it one of the best managed container shipping lines in Asia.
