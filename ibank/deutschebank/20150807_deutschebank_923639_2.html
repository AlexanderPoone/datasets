Tracking performance of government emergency fund

We track the performance of the government emergency fund through the following three studies:

  • ■    We use the top-10 stocks bought via each of the four branches of CITICS (Beijing headquarter, Wangjing branch, Hujialou branch and Finance Street branch) to proxy the direct stock purchase of CSF. As reported by Caijing Magazine, on 20 July 2015, CSF opened four brokerage accounts at four branches of CITICS to facilitate its investment. As of 4 August, we estimate that this part achieved a positive return of about 9%.

  • ■    We use the NAV performance of four blue-chip ETFs, including HTPR CSI300, China AMC SH50, China AMC CSI300 and HuaAn SH180 ETF, to proxy the investment gain/loss of CSF's subscription. As of 4 August, we estimate this part had a return of -2.6%.

  • ■    We track the performance of the five newly launched mutual funds, worth Rmb200bn, subscribed by CSF during July, which is currently making a slightly positive return of 0.3%.
