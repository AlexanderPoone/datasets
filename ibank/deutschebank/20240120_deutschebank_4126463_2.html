The Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) isdue to meet during the week of 22-26 January. We expect the MAS to keep its policy stance unchanged, given the strength of its economy, despite a faster-than-expected drop in inflation. GDP growth surprised sharply to the upside, accelerating to +2.8% YoY (+1.7% QoQsa), up from +1% (+1.3%), led by manufacturing (+3.2% YoY in Q4 vs. -4.2% in Q3). Construction growth gained further strength (rising to +9.1 % YoY in Q4 from +6.2% in Q3), while services growth moved sideways (+2.4% vs. +2.3%). Although headline CPI inflation fell at a faster-than-expected pace (to 3.6% YoY in November from 4.7% in October), led by transportation, core inflation stood largely stable at 3.2% (vs. 3.3% in the same period). We expect headline inflation to rise to 3.7% in December. The worsening conflict in the M iddle East poses upside risks to inflation in Asia, including Singapore. Weeks in review

China's growth slowed sequentially in Q4, with prices having continued to face downward pressures from the downturn in the property sector and an insufficient recovery in aggregate demand. Consumer sentiment incurred a slight setback in Q4, but consumption was still the main growth driver in 2023. Judging from the mixed message in December data, we believe that China's economy may have passed another near-term trough, and that it ison track toimprove over the next few quarters, on the back of incremental policy support. We also believe that forceful and coordinated policy responses will be necessary to jump-startthe economy. The country's fiscal stance is likely to be more supportive in 2024, with the budgetary deficit being 1.5-2% of GDP more expansionary. Total fiscal spending growth will likely surge to 8% in 2024. Monetary policy is expected to accommodate fiscal expansion. We continue to expect a total of 45bps of MLF rate cuts in 2024, but we now expectthe first rate cut to occur in March. We forecast that China's sequential growth will likely rebound in the first two quarters of 2024. We reiterate our growth forecast of 4.7% in 2024, with respective quarterly growth forecasts of 1.3%, 1.3%, 1.2%, and 1.1% for Q1-Q4 2024.
