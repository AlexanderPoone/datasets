Market Update



What if raw milk price comes down by 10%?

We still prefer downstream

Fonterra cut its farmgate milk price forecast by 27%, and we see 10% downside on China raw milk, based on a stable com price. We still prefer downstream, which has a track record of margin expansion despite a falling raw milk price.

Fonterra cut farmgate milk price forecast by 27%

On 7 August, Fonterra Co-operative (FCG NZ, Buy) cut its farmgate milk price forecast by 27% from NZ$5.25 kgMS to NZ$3.85 per kgMS for the 2015/16 season, and also reduced its milk volume forecast by 2% for the period to 1,589m kgMS. Earlier, Fonterra's Global Dairy Trade (GDT) auction held on 4 August 2015 led to a 9.3% decrease in the overall GDT Price Index, the tenth consecutive decline. We believe New Zealand prices have seen more pressure due to competition from European exporters after the expiry of output quota restriction from April 2015. Commission (EC) estimates skimmed milk powder exports to increase 17% yoy, challenging Fonterra.

China raw milk price to see 10% downside based on milk:corn price ratio

China raw milk prices have not synchronized with global milk prices (48% premium to US farmgate milk price in June 2015), and likewise, the China corn price has had its own rhythm (163% premium to CME corn futures at end-July 2015) supported by the government's protective procurement price system. We are not sure whether the milk price is supported by government intervention, but by its support of the corn price the input cost is heightened which indirectly affects the milk price. Based on the record low raw milk-to-com price ratio at 1.29x in China and assuming the com price stays unchanged, the raw milk price could see 10% downside to RMB3.07/kg. However, we believe a more significant raw milk price decline hinges on whether the China corn price premium will narrow.

If the raw milk price comes down by 10% from the current level, due to slow demand growth, Mengniu could be sitting on high cost inventory, a situation similar to 2H14, during which it expanded its margin by product mix and efficiency improvements. If the raw milk price stays firm, then there is no high cost inventory issue, but Mengniu could see import competition and it becomes difficult to stimulate demand by price cuts. Again, Mengniu will have to count on product mix and efficiency improvement for margin improvement.

Buy Mengniu as half-a brand and Sell CMD as an 85% commodity

We think 2015 is as tough as 2H14, but Mengniu is better prepared. The ultimate question is still if Mengniu is a brand or commodity. To us, Mengniu is half-a-brand as we think UHT milk will be commoditized in the long run, and it is upgrading to a total brand by yogurt/dairy beverage expansion; maintaining Buy. We set our DCF-based t at HKD41.3, factoring in 3.9% RFR, 5.6% ERP, 1.0 beta, debt-free structure, 2%TG. Key downside risk: product quality issues.

China Modern Dairy (CMD) expects c.85% of volume sales to be raw milk in 2015, i.e., it is 85% a commodity, and the raw milk price remains the most important catalyst for CMD. While the raw milk price has remained under pressure, we keep our HKD2.2 TP (1.32x 2014 P/B) and Sell rating unchanged. Upside risk: higher-than-expected raw milk price hike.
