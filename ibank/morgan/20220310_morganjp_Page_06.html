European Commission plan follows similar plan from International Energy Agency. Last week, the IEA introduced a ten point plan that could result in Russian gas imports declining by over one-third over the next year while remainmg consistent with the EU's climate ambitions in which the EU eliminates the need for Russian gas imports by 2030. Within the power sector, the EU was already expected to increase output from renewable sources by over 100 TWh, up over 15% y/y. The IEA calls for a concerted effort to accelerate renewable additions through streamlining permittmg for utility-scale wind and solar (mcremental 20 TWh) and grants for rooftop solar (15 TWh). Additionally, the IEA estimates that incentives for bioenergy, which operated at just ~50% of capacity in 2021, could generate an additional 50 TWh in 2022. Within our coverage, EVA (biomass), SEDG and ENPH (rooftop solar), TPIC (wind), and CSIQ (utility-scale and rooftop solar) have the largest exposure to the European market. See full IEA report here.

Trading Multiples Remain at a Discount

Despite the recent rebound in our coverage, we note that the average multiple remains a discount to the one- and three-year averages on both a FY22E and FY23E basis. Stocks under coverage are trading approximately in-line with the five-year average, despite a significantly larger scale, better ability to compete with other sources of electricity on an economic-merit basis, as well as broader global government support.
