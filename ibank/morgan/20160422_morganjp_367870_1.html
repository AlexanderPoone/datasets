22 April 2016

Orient Overseas Int'l Ltd

Volume ahead of seasonality but rates weak, OCEAN alliance is positive; 0.5x P/B already priced in weak industry outlook

We view the formation of OCEAN Alliance positively for OOIL. We were concerned that the recent M&A activities could potentially marginalize OOIL (which is ranked 9th largest globally) longer term notwithstanding its competitive cost structure and better revenue management. Stock is attractive at 0.5x P/B.

  • •    Container shipping revenue fell across all trade lanes: Container shipping revenue fell 17% y/y to US$1.11B in 1 QI6, below expectations, as revenue fell across all trade lanes with the largest decline on AE trade (-36% y/y), followed by IA Aus trade (-14%), TP trade (-13%) and TA trade (-at 3%). On a q/q basis, revenue fell 6%, mainly driven by lower average freight rate which fell 5% while volume fell 1% q/q in 1Q16. Revenue fell 9%/5%/4% q/q in the TP/TA/IA/Aus trades and was steady q/q on AE trade. 1Q16 revenue breakdown by route region: TP 36% (+2ppts y/y), I A/Aus 36% (+lppt), AE 16% (-5ppts),TA 12% (+2ppts).

  • •    Volume rose but load factors fell: Shipping volume rose 4% y/y but fell 1% q/q to 1.37MM TEUs, better than normal seasonality. AE volume fell 11% y/y but rose in other trades with the largest rise on TP trade (+12% y/y) followed by TA trade (+11%) and IA/Aus trade (+5%). OOIL’s loadable capacity rose 6.3% y/y in 1 QI 6 resulting in overall load factor 1.7ppts lower y/y.

  • •    Average revenue per TEL fell significantly: Average revenue per TEU fell 20% y/y, 5% q/q to US$811 in 1Q16, implying not much respite in 1 QI6 after a challenging 2015. Bunker fuel prices fell 48% y/y, 24% q/q in 1Q16. AE rates fell 28% y/y, 4% q/q, TP rates fell 22% y/y, 7% q/q, I A/Aus rates fell 19% y/y, 3% q/q while TA rates fell 12% y/y, 6% q/q.

  • •    Separately, the global containership fleet capacity rose 7.4% yly to 20.14MM TEUs or 5,173 ships as at 1 April, 2016. The total industry orderbook amounts to C.3.86MM TEUs or c. 19.2% of global existing fleet. This implies a sector capacity growth of c.5.9% this year, followed by c.6.6% in 2017E and c.5.1% in 2018E, assuming all vessels are delivered on schedule (source: Alphaliner). Scrapping rose 144% y/y to 136KTEUsin 1Q16 implying c.2.7% of current fleet annualized but still much below than the c.7.3% for dry bulk shipping. Idle fleet ratio rose to 7.4% in early April, slightly below the record high of 7.8% reached in eariy-March in terms of TEUs driven by low charter rates for vessels over 2K TEUs resulting in increased scrapping.
