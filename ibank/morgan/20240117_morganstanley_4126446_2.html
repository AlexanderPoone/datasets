  • 3.    Will carbon markets reflect the current pace of decarbonization? Major compliance markets and voluntary carbon markets (VCM) were oversupplied in 2023 and price action reflects both 1) Lower demand due to lower emissions in Europe and the UK and 2) a significant sentiment shift on the authenticity of carbon neutral claims in the VCM. Global emissions rose in 2023, however, and Limiting temperature to 1.5C is nearly scientifically unattainable. ALL else equal, higher emissions should translate to a higher cost of carbon, but the market is presently disconnected because of 1) where the emissions are currently being taxed and 2) a pullback from corporates to offset their residual emissions. In 2024, we think compliance markets will continue to reflect regional trends rather than global as we are still years from sufficient implementation of carbon pricing in markets in which emissions are rising and border taxes to combat Leakage. On the voluntary side, we think the market will continue to be oversupplied not because of a lack of emissions to offset but because of continued tepid desire to use carbon-reducing credits against real emissions. That said, we expect to see greatest interest in carbon removal technologies (e.g., DAC, CCS, BECCS, and afforestation/reforestation) and in programs such as the ICVCM, and are most constructive on price action in these markets.
