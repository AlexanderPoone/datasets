China rail infra E&C

Refocus on core amid resurge of rail infra capex to drive re-rating (H shares)

  • •    CRG and CRCC reported decent growth in earnings and new orders for FY15, along with a sharp surge in Op-CF and an improving B/S, the latter of which reflects easing credit conditions and benefits from recent restructuring since 2014, which brought focus back to cash construction, while deemphasizing property and mining investment. From a top-down perspective, we expect rail capex to stay firm and fiscal policy to stay proactive while monetary policy stays accommodative in China for 2016. Against this backdrop, we expect the new operational trends, along with favorable policy support for rail capex, to drive the sector’s re-rating.

  • •    Profitability to continue improving, revenue growth to recover. Steady GPM and deleveraging drove last year’s earnings growth despite flatfish revenue. With these drivers in place and revived revenue growth from 2016, we project earnings CAGRs of 18% for CRG and 13% for CRCC over FYI6-18. Our FYI6/17 estimates remain above the Street, by 13%/19% for CRG and by 8%/l 1% for CRCC.

  • •    New order flow inflected from mid-2015, reflecting notable pickup in new project starts, growth momentum sustained YTD. New orders for infra construction fell c20% in 1H15 before rebounding a strong 40% in 2H15 on average, driven by a pickup in new project starts made possible by policy intervention, easing funding and the gradual rollout of the new PPP model. New order growth remained robust in 1Q16, up c 15% Y/Y for both contractors.

  • •    CF generation and B/S strength improved sharply in 2015, expected to stay robust with wider application of PPP model. The cash conversion cycle improved while net gearing fell notably last year, helped by recapitalization and better Op-cash inflow. Although the terms of projects under the PPP model vary, in general we expect conversions into PPP from the past BT and BOT to help lessen capex burdens as funding sources are more diversified with more support from government under PPP.

  • •    Revise FY16E-17E earnings 1-2% for CRG and CRCC, reset Dec-16 DCFbased PTs due to revisions and PT rollover. Both stocks still trade at undemanding valuation multiples following the recent rebound, at an 8-9x P/E and 0.8-0.9x P/B on FY16E, despite solid growth prospects and ROE generation. Maintain OW on CRG-H and CRCC-H. The H-shares have risen c!0% on average YTD (HSCEI: -5%).
