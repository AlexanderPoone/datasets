We expect a moderate 0.9pt improvement in the July University of Michigan consumer sentiment reading between the preliminary and final reports, taking the headline index to 73.5. After the strong jump in the preliminary reading, we anticipate some further upside for the final report as labor markets remained resilient and equity prices improved further on the month. If realized, the final headline reading would move to a two-year high. Meanwhile, inflation expectations moved back up a tick across the preliminary July report, and future movements of those series will be noteworthy.

We forecast that real consumer spending rose 0.3% in June following a flat May reading, while nominal spending grew 0.5% on the month. Despite some softness in the June retail sales support (not counting upward revisions to prior months), we suspect spending on services continued to outpace spending on goods. Spending on goods excluding autos and building materials posted a solid 0.3% gain in June, while unit vehicle sales rose 4.2% last month.

We also look for personal income to have climbed 0.4% in June, broadly in line with its monthly pace since February. Wage and salary growth is expected to have risen a very strong 0.8%, after average weekly payrolls posted a similar jump in the June employment report. With spending expected to have increased slightly more than income, we anticipate the saving rate will tick down to 4.5% in June.
