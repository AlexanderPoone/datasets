Lifting margins on stronger demand and better supply discipline

We have lifted our assumptions on pricing and margins following the release of the NDP results (22 Sept). We now expect GPM to rise to 23.2% in FYI7 or an effective NP/T of HK$574. The 2.7% increase in FYI6 and the 8.4% increase in NP for FYI7 are due to GPM increases. Our revised EPS estimates are 7% and 12% higher than consensus estimates for FY16 and FYI 7, respectively. We lift our Dec-17 DCFbased PT to HK$ 10.1 (previously HK$9.3).

New tissue business is all about co-Iocation. LMP started a brand new business of making tissue paper in 2014 and 2016 would be the first year that tissues would be in a solidly profitable position. We expect LMP to grow to about a 5% market share (production) by the end of 2017 based on the company’s expansion plans. The key to success for LMP in this highly competitive business is its low cost manufacturing as well as its strategy of growing a niche brand with minimal marketing expenses. Currently, three of the top four leading tissue makers in China are companies with branded products. Most of these incumbents are dedicated tissue makers (some make diapers and other sanitary products) without large-scale paper production. LMP’s containerboard production volumes are about 5x larger than the volume of China’s largest tissue maker and the co-location of the new tissue business with LMP’s existing containerboard business allows for one of the lowest cost production of tissue paper in China. Tissue production costs such as power generation, environmental equipment, logistics and many factory overheads for tissue production are reduced by 50% to 100% by sharing with the existing containerboard facilities.

Branding through the internet. Initially LMP’s tissue sales were nearly all unbranded ‘big roll’ production for branded tissue makers for 2014 and 2015. LMP would sell these large rolls (>1 tonne) to branded producers who do not have their own production. Increasingly these customers are house brands of retailers. LMP can make money selling to these brands because it has very low costs of production. For 2016, the company plans to sell 20-30% of its sales through its own brands; Hanky for bleached tissues and Puz for unbleached bamboo pulp tissues. The company is focusing its marketing effort for sale in Sichuan Province only for products in stores and also for sale through the e-commerce for the rest of the country. The company does not intend to spend heavily to build a national brand and prefers to be a niche online brand. Due to a large number of consumers buying everyday consumables online and the relatively low cost of shipping (LMP estimates just 10% of the total end user price is shipping costs), this channel of selling tissues is gaining traction. Ultimately, the goal in a few years by LMP is to sell 50% of total production through branded sales (online and traditional) with the remaining sales in large rolls OEM for other brands.
