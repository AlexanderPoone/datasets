Beijing's endeavors toward a Blue Sky following announcement of investigation into fraudulent NEV sales

CHINA CONNECT


  • •    A concrete step forward on NEV subsidy policy: China’s Ministry of Finance (MoF) announced today the results of an investigation into fraudulent NEV sales that started earlier this year. Five companies were identified as being involved in fraudulent sales, with illegal subsidies received totaling Rmbl.Obn (Table 1). Penalties include not only the return of the subsidies, but also the revocation of vehicle manufacturing licenses, meaning a company is out of the car production business completely, e.g., Suzhou GMC Bus Manufacturing Ltd. (unlisted). The MoF also highlighted other penalties for companies whose NEVs sold were in question, e.g., not to actual end customers, but sitting idle as inventory.

  • •    What does Beijing want to achieve, and how? We believe China remains ambitious in its 2020 accumulated NEV sales target of 5.0mn units (vs. JPMe 4.6mn). To achieve this, curb further policy loopholes and reduce the financial burden of the subsidy program, we note a few major policy moves: 1) Carbon emissions trading scheme, starting with select pilot cities in 2017, followed by nationwide implementation in 2018 (here). The two main purposes of the scheme are: (1) to reduce the government’s financial burden while mitigating NEV makers’ profit shortfalls due to subsidy reduction (i.e., 20% in 2017); and (2) to force small inefficient NEV players to exit, as they won’t be eligible for the subsidy or have any carbon quota/credit for sales. While valuations and measures of the carbon quota are unknown at this stage, we believe the consensus 2017 forecast for BYD could see 10-15% upside through sales of carbon quota.

  • 2)    Raising the bar on entering the NEV business: MIIT recently revised China’s “Rule on NEV Production Admission and Administration,” specifying stricter criteria for entering the NEV business.

  • 3)    On the battery side, 57 suppliers are included in the national supplier list from which OEMs have to source batteries in order to receive a subsidy. Korean makers are likely to be included before year-end.

• Recommendation: Accumulate BYD-H (OW): Our view into 2017 is that investors should focus on two groups of companies as industry volume slows: (1) those with higher earnings visibility - Brilliance China and Geely Auto; and (2) policy beneficiaries - BYD. We raise our 2017/18 earnings forecasts for BYD by 9%/17% to reflect our positive stance; our revised estimates are 10-15% above the Street. Our new BYD-H PT is HK$75.
