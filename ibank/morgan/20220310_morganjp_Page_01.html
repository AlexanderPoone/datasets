Exiting the 4Q Print: Back to the Future; Top Picks Are RUN, NOVA, ARRY and SHLS

Stocks under coverage have outperformed over the past month as 4Q results/outlooks were generally better than expected and the energy situation in Europe reminds investors of the long-term trend towards renewables, now potentially accelerating further. Some near-term headwinds remain, particularly for utility-scale projects, though we remain generally positive on the overall space. Our preferred picks are RUN and NOVA within residential and ARRY and SHLS within utility-scale, though we acknowledge some patience is likely still required within utility-scale.

  • •    Solid demand. 4Q demand trends remained favorable with most product/solution companies reporting record bookings and/or backlog figures. The strength was broad-based with residential solar companies (ENPH, GNRC, SEDG, NOVA, RUN, SPWR), utility-scale solar (FSLR), storage (FLNC), biomass (EVA), smart meters (ITRI), and fuel cells (BE) all reporting record bookings and/or backlog. Wind blade manufacturer TPIC was one standout that did not report record demand. Within solar, we also note that BNEF increased its FY22 estimate of PV deployments to 245 GW, up 33% y/y, revised higher from a prior forecast of 24% y/y growth.

  • •    Supply chains remain a headwind, particularly for utility-scale. Generally, longer project cycles have had relatively more significant issues with supply chain headwinds, as project pricing and margins determined in the past (in some cases measured in years), are negatively impacted by current elevated input costs. As a result, upstream product companies with exposure to utility-scale have been generally more impacted, while residential projects, with timing measured in weeks/months, are generally more immune as elevated input costs are passed on to end-customers via recently signed contracts.

  • •    Russia/Ukraine conflict could accelerate renewable deployment. As the EU looks to reportedly increase its energy security and as citizens pay surging power prices, we believe residential solar companies (SEDG, ENPH) could see increased demand near-term owing to short project cycles. The European Commission has also proposed steps to significantly increase deployments of renewables and green hydrogen over the medium-term, which we believe could help buoy most companies under coverage. See page 5.

  • •    Trading multiples remain at a discount. Despite the recent rebound in our coverage, we note that the average multiple remains a discount to the one- and three-year averages on both a FY22E and FY23E basis. Stocks under coverage are trading approximately in-line with the five-year average, despite a significantly larger scale, better ability to compete with other sources of electricity on an economic-merit basis, as well as broader global government support.
