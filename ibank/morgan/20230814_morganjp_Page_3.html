our view on the creditheading into the PASO was constructive for the immediate term despite the sharp repricing of the last months and the wide confidence interval associated to Argentina’s medium term policy framework. As the market kept pricing a higher probability of a regime change (through the face of a Juntos por el Cambio government) and the fading a negative tail risk scenario in the short run, there was still room for further improvement as the political events reassured this direction of travel. However, considering La Libertad Avanza’s significant outperformance, and the disappointing results of Juntos por el Cambio (Patricia Bullrich) add a new layer of uncertainty considering the relative position of Union por la Patria (Sergio Massa) that will likely weigh on valuations. In this state of the world, where the disruptive policy path that Javier Milei entails becomes more likely amid a deteriorating macroeconomic stance, the bar for further overperformance seems high and expect significant volatility in the com ing months (subject to the events of the political arena). Nevertheless, the weakness of the ruling coalition vis a vis the aggregate performance of Juntos por el Cambio and La Libertad Av anza still pushes in the direction of a regime change coming December, narrative that should still offer support for valuations.
