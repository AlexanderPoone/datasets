Cheung Kong / Hutchison Whampoa

Setting the course for restructuring

Hutch’s reported results not only highlighted the strength of its European operation but also the strength of its balance sheet. Hutch is confident that they can drive further consolidation in Europe. After restructuring, CK Property is expected to have a balanced property portfolio with more rental income than Cheung Kong today, providing support for a progressive dividend policy, in our view. We maintain our OW on both companies, noting about 20% upside potential from current levels.

  • •    Setting the course for restructuring. Hutch and Cheung Kong results were higher than our expectation. However, we have seen more than usual one-off write downs or impairment charges recorded at Hutch. We believe this is setting the right course for the proposed restructuring exercise. We have also seen Hutch revalue up its investment properties quite substantially to further boost its shareholders’ equity. Similar upward property revaluation has also been seen at Cheung Kong.

Hutch’s earnings were slightly higher than expectations. The beat was mainly from the higher-than-expected contribution from 3 Group in Europe. Apart from Italy, which is still facing keen competition, all other markets are seeing improvement from better service margin and synergies from previous consolidation. Hutch management believes that they can drive consolidation in every market that they are operating in on the back of their balance sheet strength.

Cheung Kong’s earnings also beat our estimate. While 76% of the company’s turnover was from Hong Kong, China development business is slowing down both in terms of delivery and contracted sales. From achieved contracted sales of HK$39bn in 2014, we expect the company to acliieve about HK$30bn contracted sales in 2015. The company also wants to maintain a balanced mix of property development versus property investment for CK Property going forward in order to maintain strong investment-grade ratings. However, its gearing is likely to be at reasonably higher levels in order to support a healthy level of ROE.

Market focus is still on restructuring. While not much has been mentioned regarding the ongoing restructuring exercise, we believe the market will still be focusing on this issue rather titan the reported results. The Cheung Kong shareholder meeting has passed the first step of the restructuring. The next milestone will be the dispatch of the Hutch scheme document by the end of March. We believe there are still further re-rating upside for both stocks post restructuring.
