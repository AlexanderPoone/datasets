For associate and JCE ports, we estimate the profit yield (net profit/TEU in USS) for each port asset to come up with an estimate for the attributable net profit for CP’s non-consolidated terminal assets. We once again see that the profit yields of the newly consolidated CSPD ports will have a dilutive impact (partly driven by higher mix of less profitable domestic volumes). We have further made an assumption of a 5% drop in profit yield for most Chinese port assets on the back of Rmb depreciation (c4.8% depreciation in 1H16 while base should ease in 2H16) as well as potentially more empty boxes being moved on the back of slower trade volumes, which would both have a negative impact on the yield in USS terms.

In terms of volume assumptions, for FY16, we have made reference to the 1H volumes which have been reported which show c3.5% total throughput growth in the first half with organic growth being closer to 1% if we were to strip out the newly acquired ports in Turkey and Korea. While several operators are expecting a pick-up in 2H16 volume growth on the back of a lower base as well as sequential improvements coming through in the last few months, we have chosen to remain more conservative in our assumptions, expecting cl.9% total throughput growth (+1% organic) for FYI6 while factoring in a low-growth environment for the coming years expecting 2.6% organic growth (excluding Kumport and Busan) in FY17.
