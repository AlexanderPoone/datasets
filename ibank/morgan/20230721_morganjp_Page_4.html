We estimate that new orders for durable goods were unchanged in June while related shipments fell 1.3%, with both weakening appreciably after surging in May. Industry data point to very strong demand for non-defense aircraft, while additional strength in the defense sector offset weakness in orders elsewhere; indeed, we expect orders in the important core capital goods measure fell 1.4% last month. Shipments should be weaker across the board in June, declining 1.3%, with aircraft slipping materially after a jump in May. Core capital goods shipment should also be weak, declining 1.0% on the month.

We forecast that real GDP increased 2.0% saar in 2Q, similar to the pace of growth in 1Q. As has been the case for much of the pandemic recovery period, GDP growth continues to be buffeted by large swings in net exports and inventory accumulation. As a result, we estimate that growth in real domestic final sales should have run stronger than GDP last quarter.

The main components of real domestic final sales contributed positively to growth in 2Q, even as growth in real consumer spending likely stepped down to a 1.3% saar from a very strong 4.2% saar at the start of this year. By contrast, growth

in business fixed investment posted a rebound from a near stall in IQ as equipment spending jumped 5.3% saar after two consecutive quarters of contraction. We look for real investment in intellectual property products to have accelerated to 6.7% saar last quarter, while nonresidential structures investment slowed modestly to a still-strong 10.0% saar. We also anticipate a solid 3.6% saar rising in real government spending, which has been a significant contributor to overall growth for the past four quarters now.
