Third Time's a Charm

After having tried twice to get Long duration this year and failing, we tried again and the third time was a charm. We suggest staying Long and buying any dips that arise. We also bought the dip in the USD and see the case for EUR/USD at parity in 2024. The hard vs. soft Landing debate is back.

Global Macro Strategy

We discuss recent coincident indicators of the economy and consumer from the Philadelphia Fed and Conference Board. We also preview an upcoming note on Fed policy and the US general election. Finally, we discuss our call for EURAJSD to fall to parity in 1Q24.

Interest Rate Strategy

We switch Long 30y exposure in US rates, to Long UST 10y. We maintain Long SFRZ4 on Z3Z4Z5 fLy. We dose aLL our bullish trades and curve flatteners, i.e., EUR 10y10y swap, the EUR 2m10y 3.25%/3.OO% swaption receiver, the EUR 2s10s swap rec and the short 5y Bono vs 20y EU. We enter two tactical bearish trades for a two-month horizon: pay EUR 5y5y swap and a Long Feb 130.50/129 Bund put spread. In cash, we keep our structural short 10y BTP versus Bund, our 10s30s OAT ASW box and our Long 10y Austria versus Belgium. We maintain short EU 2034 ASW and EU 0.7% 2051/EU 2.5% 2052 micro steepeners. We keep receiving Feb'24-Aug'24 MPC spread, while we maintain our tactical paying 2s5s10s SONIA fly. We maintain receive 10y OIS vs. sell 30y JGBs (DV011.5 vs. 1), sell 30y JGB ASW, and TONA OIS 2s5s steepener (DV011.5 v.s 1). We close Long 5y JGB vs. pay 7y OIS (DV01 2 vs. 1), as 5y JGB yield went back to fair Level.

Currency & Foreign Exchange

We are bullish on the USD, particularly now that it appears oversold. We think the case for EUR/USD to fall to parity by spring still holds and markets are underestimating Eurozone economic weakness and ECB dovishness. In CHF, we Look at turnover data and identify the significance of US factors over EU factors for the currency. We discuss why USD/JPY has been traded rich vs. the Level suggested by policy differentials. We see downside risks to USD/ CAD into the BoC meeting. We recommend paid April BoC 2024 with received June BoC 2025 positions.

Inflation-Linked Bonds

In the US, we remain neutral on TIPS and BE.
