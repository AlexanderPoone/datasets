Long-term trends in Turkiye: Mean reversion or trend following? (I)

MSCI Turkiye has been on a long-term downtrend, both in index performance and in USD earnings-at least until last year. From a long-term perspective, MSCI Turkiye is a mean-reversion dream. Or a trap to avoid for investors who expect the current negative trends to continue.

The performance of MSCI Turkiye in USD terms from Jan ‘10, i.e. just after the end of the GFC, to now is -5% CAGR Losing 5% every year is a poor trend of equity investors. The trend line - and MSCI Turkiye is well above the trendline - shows a -10% CAGR.

When we look at earnings, we see that since Jan ‘10, USD earnings are up small, by 3% CAGR since Jan ‘ 10, but more than all of that has come in the last 12m. USD EPS from Jan ‘ 10 to the trough in Dec ‘21 was -68% overall or -9% CAGR. We wonder if the strong reversal in earnings is lasting. If the JPM TRY forecast of 25 by end-’23 is correct or we use a TRY of 26.9 to reverse the recent real appreciation, then we think USD earnings will be down.

Looking at Turkiye vs. EM as a relative trade, it has lost 5% CAGR since Jan ‘10 or 47% overall. And that includes Tiirkiye’s world-beating‘22 performance, +88%, miles ahead of Chile which rose+12% and finished in 2nd place in EM.

The poor USD performance of the stock market seems odd when we look at the >5% real GDP growth that Turkiye has compounded since 2010. However, the gap between the best-in-CEEMEA real GDP performance and below-average GDP growth in USD is one more lens to see the issues in Tiirkiye’s recent economic performance.
