ESG Improvers

Finding the jewels among polluting industries




Dara-Driven


Environmental, Social and Governance improvers are companies with positively trending ESG profiles but are currently underappreciated by investors and rating agencies. We have identified 9 stocks in Asia ex-Japan that could see a rerating as they announce emission reduction targets and articulate their ESG strategies. Our top picks include utility names HK Electric Investments, Tata Power and Global Power Synergy' for their promising transition towards renewable energy, and cement companies ACC, Ambuja Cements. UltraTech and Taiwan Cement for their science-based emission reduction plans. Hyundai Steel and Bharat Petroleu m are also on our list for their low carbon intensity vs peers and better protection against potential cost from carbon pricing.

  • •    Improving emissions yield higher returns. We created two market-weighted stock screens from five polluting industries and found that companies with improving greenhouse gas emission intensity would outperform those with a deteriorating intensity by 42% from Janl9-Mar22, yielding 10% annualized excess return vs the regional index. Hence, investing in future ESG improvers should generate excess returns, in our view.

  • •    ESG improvers. The criteria we used to identify our ESG improvers are 1) companies in polluting sectors such as utilities, materials, energy and mining; 2) improving caibon intensity and expected emission reduction targets; 3) relatively weak ESG ratings; and 4) strong fundamentals. The 9 companies we identified (see Table 1) have improving ESG profiles, which are not reflected in their valuation or ESG ratings (usually updated once a year based on annual data disclosures). We recommend accumulating these names ahead of emission targets announcements and potential ESG rating upgrades.

  • •    The most polluting sectors. Companies that produce electricity, oil & gas and construction materials are the three most polluting sectors in Asia ex-Japan, based on 2020 data. However, we found that 50-70% of electric utilities and construction material companies in the regional index reduced their emissions during that period. We believe this was driven by the increased use of renewable power and higher energy efficiency.

  • •    Companies ramping up net-zero targets, but lacking robustness. Sixty of the 500 largest companies by revenue in Asia ex Japan have announced net-zero emission targets vs only 5 before 2020. While this is encouraging, we recommend investors look beyond the targets and scrutinize the execution plan. One way is checking whether their emission reduction goals are validated by the Science Based Targets initiative. SBTi provides credibility to whether companies align their targets with the Paris Agreement to limit global warming to below 2°C. The companies with net-zero targets supported by SBTi are ACC Ltd (ACC IN, OW), CLP Holdings (2 HK, OW), Taiwan Cement (1101 TT, OW), Vedanta (VEDL IN, OW), Ambuja Cements (ACEM IN, N), HK Electric Investments (2638 HK, N), UtraTech Cement (UTCEM IN, N), and Adani Transmission (not covered).
