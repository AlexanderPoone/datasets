Reduced odds of US recession near-term = good for high beta FX

What does this mean for FX?

First, lower odds of near-term recession in the I S in combination stronger relative growth outside the I S moves high beta FX more concretely to bullish territory vs. LSD tactically. We have noted that historically, a growth lift outside the US is usually enough to deliver USD weakness. We had transitioned to this regime on our systematic models in November but resisted chasing outright USD shorts due to elevated US recession risks which are usually associated with USD strength. With odds of US recession in the near-term lower, the shelflife for USD weakness vs. high beta FX is likely to be longer than originally anticipated.

The caveat is that one must be vigilant on growth signals as the ultimate destination will still be USD-bullish if it involves a hard landing, so this should be viewed as a tactical setup.

China re-opening more durable than euro rebound

Not all high beta FX are equal. A rebound in currencies linked to China re-opening should have more durability' than the euro in our view as the Eurozone will grapple energy vulnerability in the medium-term as well. Activity data has been beating expectations in the region but the level of EASIs has fallen; this metric will remain key to determine the extent of positive (or negative) fundamental momentum in EUR.
