A temporary sweet spot

The past month has been about stabilization. US nominal yields, which had been at the center of investor attention given explosive moves higher in February, lost momentum in the past month. Importantly, US real yields have been stable since late-February which enabled other markets to breathe easier. This stability occurred in conjunction with PM Is/ activity data that reinforced strong global growth momentum and thus the tone in risk

In FX, this backdrop manifested into bifurcated performance of the dollar. In GIO where interest rate divergence has been the dominant driver of FX returns all year, most DM currencies weakened further vs. USD as US yield spreads widened additionally in favour of the dollar (exhibit 2). In EM. the stability in US real yields enabled several higher beta commodity FX to strengthen in the past month, but idiosyncratic events have been more dominant (in TRY, RUB and INR for example).
