Longs build on EUR crosses: The market may have cut Long EUR/USD positions rapidly on Friday on the back of the aforementioned catalysts, but we find investors1 confidence to be long EUR on crosses has increased, particularly against GBP. ECB's signal for another 50bp hike in March - though not enough to meet the market's high bar - still contrasted with BoE commentary of a pause and more rate cuts further out. EUR/GBP longs built in Line with our expectations and though positioning there is starting to look extreme versus history, it is still below Levels seen post the September 2022 mini-budget (Exhibit 3). JPY shorts (against both EUR and USD) may be added too, as reports suggesting Amamiya as the next BoJ Governor may reduce market expectations for a sudden hawkish BoJ tilt. In contrast, we don't see scope for more EUR strength against NOK and SEK. Both crosses stand at multi-year highs, and the market is already meaningfully Long (Exhibit 2). Stronger-than-expected European (and global) growth should be supportive of small open economies, and the increasing probability of a soft landing could keep equities supported as the current earnings season draws to a close. We prefer being short EUR/NOK given the also solid domestic picture and higher beta to oil prices, while hedging the possibility of a risk pullback via Long EUR/AUD positions.

Signals from FX options positioning: We have found that in the G10, short-term (weekly) contrarian strategies looking at the 1-year percentile of the options positioning score provide attractive risk-adjusted returns. For this week, the strategy's favours USD Longs against USD/CAD and USD/JPY (Exhibit 4). We agree more with the Long USD/JPY signal given pricing for Fed rate hikes may build (supporting US yields) amid strong data, contrasting BoJ pricing should a continuation candidate gets nominated as BoJ Governor.
