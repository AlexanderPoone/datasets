  • •    North American Utilities Outlook: Walk This Way: Green Leverage, Increasing Growth, Attractive Valuation Drive Positive Backdrop. We expect accelerating renewables deployment and associated transmission needs (possibly with DC support) combined with a continued focus on coal retirement pull-aheads to drive incrementally positive developments for the group. At the same time, in a world starved for yield, the 3.1% 2022 utility yield and mid-single digit dividend growth potential should appeal to income investors. Altogether, the convergence of these positive tailwinds, green leverage, and a resilient business model creates a compelling risk/reward profile, in our view.

  • •    Green Rules Everything around Me (G.R.E.A.M.): Electric Green Ranker IV the People. We refresh our Electric Green Ranker and highlight improvements in AEP, PEG, and CNP’s relative rank given the substantial green capex increase for AEP, emissions benefits from PEG’S fossil fleet sale, and new CNP emission goals. Overall, AES and D screen favorably under our “Green Rate of Change” analysis and remain top picks.

  • •    Natural Gas LDC Green Ranker II: Green and Bear It. As ESG themes continue to grow across the utilities sector, we update our natural gas local distribution company (LDC) green ranker. Among green ranker updates, NJR realized the most significant improvement on a “Green Rate of Change” basis, now screening as the #2 name. Across LDCs, we prefer ATO and NI into 2022.

  • •    Cost Structure Analysis: Examining FERC Form 1 O&M Trends; NEE, WEC, ALE Stand Out. Our cost structure analysis utilizes FERC Form 1 data to analyze electric utility cost trends over the past five years, examining how a company’s cost structure changes over time. While capex focused on expanding renewables and infrastructure supporting clean energy often takes center stage, we see expense management as a somewhat underappreciated key factor within the industry. Beyond regular operational execution, historical factors driving O&M changes include realized M&A impacts (NEE), rate case outcomes (AGR, ALE), and system safety needs (EIX, PCG), among other factors.

  • •    Regression Reveals Relative Rate Risk More Than Compensated for at Current Valuations. For the group, our 20-year regression versus the US 1 fl-year implies 12% utility upside in 2022, even with a +75bp interest rate increase, the forecast from JPM’s Interest Rates Strategy team. For individual utilities, our analysis shows that utilities with higher dividend growth typically outperform those with lower dividend growth during periods of rising rates. While NEE stands out amid diversified utilities, AEE, CMS, CNP, WEC, and XEL appear best positioned among regulated electrics to outperform in a rising rate environment, given top-tier 6-7%+ dividend growth.
