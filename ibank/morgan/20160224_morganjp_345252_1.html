New World Development

Towards the end of group restructuring

IH16 will continue to be eventfi.il for NWD as the proposed privatization remains in progress. Despite the more challenging market outlook, we believe the stock should be re-rated given that management has shown a clear direction for the company and its efforts in improving shareholders’ return.

  • •    Interim DPS increase reflecting management’s confidence. New World Development (NWD) reported IHFY16 underlying profit at HK$3.28 bn, down 26% Y/Y. Despite lower earnings, NWD raised interim DPS by 8% Y/Y to HK$0.13/sh, implying a payout ratio of 36% (vs. 24% for IHFY 15). Management is confident in full-year earnings and we believe this is a positive sign as they work on improving shareholders' return.

  • •    New developments in streamlining group structure remains the focus. NWD has been very active in the past 1 -2 years to restructure the group in older to streamline its operations. The recent disposal of its fixed-line business further reinforced the group's strategy to dispose of non-core assets and redeploy resources to core businesses. Given the substantial outperformance of NWCL since the announcement in Jan-16 amid the stock market correction, we believe the chance for success of the proposed privatization of NWCL is high.

  • •    Gearing to increase in interim period. Using the latest financials, we estimate the pro-forma net debt-to-total equity ratio after taking into account all the major acquisitions and disposals would be around 33%, compared to 31% as at Dec-15. That said, as NWCL will receive payments over the next 24 months for three of the projects, gearing will increase in the interim period. Upon successfill privatization and with part of the proceeds to be received from Evergrande, gearing could go up to 39% by Jun-16, as per our estimate.

  • •    Sales target remains unchanged despite low achieving ratio. NWD recorded some HK$3.1 bn of contracted sales in HK so far for FYI 6. Even though this is only 31% of the FYI6 target of HK$10 bn, the company will keep the original target and try to balance between margin and volume to achieve that. While we believe it is getting more challenging to reach the target as other developers are also eager to launch their projects, the potential miss would have a muted impact to FY16E earnings but a greater impact on cash flow. Yet, this is likely to be compensated by more selective landbanking in the meantime.
