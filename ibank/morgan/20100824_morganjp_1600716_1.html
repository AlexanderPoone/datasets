COSCO Pacific

1H10 core operating profits up 51% Y/Y, 56% Q/Q despite distortions related to Yantian asset and Piraeus project - ALERT

COSCO Pacific (1199 HK) reported the 1H10 results during the lunch break. We summarize below for our first impression:

  • •    Overall the numbers are ahead of our expectations despite a few distortions highlighted below: The group's operating profit came in at US$67 MM. up 51% Y/Y and 56% Y/Y. driven by the strong recovery in all three segments. At the net profit level, the result was up 29% Y/Y and 17% Q/Q on lower non-core one-off gains & other operating income. The net result of US$105MM (excluding discontinued businesses) in 1H10 was 5% ahead of our expectations. The results should come down well with the market in our view, given our FY10 net profit forecast appear ahead of consensus according to Bloomberg. Refer to Table 2 on the back for detailed results highlights.

  • •    Worth highlighting a few distortions which will make CP’s results more tilted towards the 2H, including:

  • 1)    Yantian acquisition, which we estimate to add a net profit of US$20MM on a 6-month basis this year, will only be included from July 1,2010;

  • 2)    Yantian port did not declare the dividend income during 1H10 (annual contribution is approx. US$18-19MM p.a. in the past, hence approx. US$1 OMM impact in 1H10), hence no earnings contribution during 1H10. This is because in 1H10 Yantian was still treated as a "subassociate investment" for CP's 7% stake before the acquisition of additional 14% stake from Mersk and hence only dividend income would be recorded as CP’s earnings. Starting from July 1. 2010. the Yantian asset has been reclassified as an "associate" and hence CP would start booking the related profit using the "equity pick-up" method.

  • 3)    Piraeus Port was only acquired from Oct-2009; during the 1H10 period, we estimate Piraeus Port contributed a net loss of US$1 OMM (vs. nil in 1H09). As we highlighted, starting from June 1 this year with the 8-month service agreement with PPA ended, the port has been turned around and losses have almost narrowed down to zero with the cost savings in payroll estimated at US$2~3MM per month.

  • •    Dividend payout was a positive surprise: Total dividend declared at US$86MM or US$4 cents per share, up cl()0% Y/Y. driven by special dividends of US$2 cents per share, ahead of our expectations.

  • •    Maintain OW: The stock remains attractively valued in our view, at 1 lx P/E on core earnings, 9.5x EV/EBITDA and lx P/B on FYI IE. More to follow post the analyst briefing at 4pm & our JPM Live Call at 8pm HK time.
