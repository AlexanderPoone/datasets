Macro: A 4.75-5.0% Fed Funds, rising unemployment rate, falling inflation, and flattish GDP growth forecasted in 2023

Our US economist Michael Feroli published 2023 US Economic Outlook: Bad Moon Rising;. They expect the FOMC to tighten another lOObp, including 50bp in December and 25bp in both February and March. The almost 500bp of expected cumulative hikes is already delivering a commensurate tightening of financial conditions, which we believe will tip the economy into mild recession later next year. Avoiding a near-term recession, J.P. Morgan’s economists forecast a mild US recession by 4Q23 amid a million job losses. In particular, forecasted US GDP growth is +1.0% in +1Q23, +0.8% in 2Q23, +0.5% in 3Q23, and -0.5% by 4Q23. This translates to q4/q4 US GDP growth of +0.4% in 2023 following +0.5% in 2022. They are effectively looking for a Category 1 economic hurricane. But to get inflation to behave, it seems hard to avoid a period of significant decline in labor market activity. Even as supply chain normalization should provide some relief to price inflation, that relief will be transitory unless wage inflation also moderates.

Even with a modest slowdown and with firms hoarding labor, we think the economy could shed over a million jobs. By most measures hourly wage growth is currently running around 5%. That likely needs to run closer to 3.5% before policymakers can feel more comfortable about returning to 2% inflation. And that sort of wage growth deceleration will likely require an unemployment rate between 4% and 5%, depending on how entrenched wage growth expectations have become. As such the US unemployment rate is forecasted to rise from 3.6% to 4.3% by YE23 and to 5.0% in 2024. Inflation should moderate with a softer labor market, stronger dollar, and unwinding of pandemic distortions. For the core CPI, we forecast deceleration from 6.6%oya in September to 5.7% in December and 3.4% next September. This implies roughly 0.3% gains on average for the monthly core CPI prints into the third quarter of 2023. They expect annualized quarterly core CPI rates to cool from 5.5% in 2H22 to 3.7% in 1H23 and 2.9% in 2H23. Core PCE prices (% q/q) are forecasted to fall from 4.5% in 3Q22 to 3.1% by 2Q23 and 2.2% by 4Q23. With falling inflation and an unemployment rate rising toward 5%, our economists look for the Fed to ease up on policy by early 2024. Under the assumption the economy slips into recession later next year and significant job losses ensue, our economists see the funds rate being reduced 50bp per quarter starting in 2Q24, leaving the funds rate at 3.5% by ’24 year-end. Calibrating monetary policy to maintain a soft landing will prove to be a very difficult task. But they don’t see the need for a big recession to bring the economy more into balance.
