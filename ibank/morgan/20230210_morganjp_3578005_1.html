The PBOC released the credit report for January, with both TSF data and bank loans coming in above expectations. Regarding aggregate credit conditions, TSF increased 5.98tn yuan in January (JPM: 5.35tn yuan; consensus: 5.4tn yuan), the second-highest on record (compared to the historical high of 6.18tn yuan in January 2022). The stock of outstanding TSF grew 9.4%oya (vs. 9.6%oya in December). Bank loans beat expectations notably, increasing 4.90tn yuan in January (JPM: 3.9tn yuan; consensus: 4.20tn yuan), registering the highest monthly increase on record, with the stock of outstanding loans rising 11.3%oya (vs. 11. l%oya in December). Regarding non-loan components of TSF, government bond issuance picked up by 414bn yuan (vs. 281 bn yuan in December), corporate bond issuance rose moderately, by 149bn yuan (vs. contraction of 489bn yuan in December), and the shadow credit components (trust loans, entrust loans and bank acceptance) expanded 349bnyuan(vs. contraction of 142bn yuan in December).
