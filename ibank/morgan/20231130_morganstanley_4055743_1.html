Industry View                               In-Line


Deposit rates were flat on average: In the past two weeks, the average highest rate paid was unchanged at 5.12% (cumulative +470 bps since the first rate hike), the average 13-36 month rate was unchanged at 3.85% (cumulative +355 bps) and the average 1-12 month rate increased by 1 bp to 4.92% (cumulative +471 bps). Using the average highest rate paid as a proxy, CD rates are now up 470 bps through the first 525 bps of the current rate hike cycle, implying a 90% beta versus 65% last rate hike cycle. Among the primarily online deposit gatherers (ALLY, AXP, COF, DFS, and SYF), the average highest CD rate increased by 11 bps to 5.33%, while online savings rates were unchanged at 4.58%.


Higher-for-longer rates should pressure deposit costs: While two weeks don't make a trend, it does stand out that this is the first two week period this rate cycle in which the average highest CD offer is flat. We will be watching for continued evidence of a deceleration in deposit pricing pressure as the Fed likely keeps rates on hold for the next several quarters. Our base case expectation is that CD offers trend higher across the group as banks continue to selectively raise their rates to attract new incremental deposit balances. Even if new CD offer rates remain flat from here, we expect deposit pressure from QT, mix shift from lower cost to higher cost deposits and older CDs maturing and repricing at current rates means that total deposit betas should continue rising over the next several quarters.
