Investment Thesis, Valuation and Risks

Weichai Power - H (Overweight; Price Target: HK$15.00)

Investment Thesis

YTD Weichai’s share has rebounded strongly, reversing last year’s underperformance as industry sales recovered while Weichai regained lost market share. In 2015, it saw core net profit drop c60% Y/Y in part because Beiqi Foton aggressively cut its Weichai orders. Beiqi’s sourcing ratio from Weichai slid to c25-30% in 2015 from 60-70%.

Looking ahead, we believe the company’s earnings quality and growth outlook are improving on the back of: (1) we think Foton will raise its Weichai procurement ratio in 2016 in order to protect its market share, while giving its JV brand more time to establish itself; (2) recent datapoints that suggest that industiy demand may have bottomed; (3) KION contributions becoming more visible in 2015 and continuing to be a more significant source of earnings for Weichai going forward; and (4) the hydraulic component segment, which could be a potential source of upside as Weichai aims to capture one-third of the midrange to high-end hydraulic components segment by 2020, while segment margins are also expected to rise toward the estimated steady-state level of 30-40% (<30% last year).

Valuation

Our Dec-17 PT of HK$15.0 is based on our DCF valuation. Key assumptions in our DCF valuation include a WACC of 10%, driven by a cost of equity of 10.7%, a cost of debt of 5.0%, a target debt-to-capital of 10% and terminal growth of 0%. Our PT translates into a target FY16E P/E of 24.3x, EV/EBITDA of 6.5x and P/BV of 1.6x.

Risks to Rating and Price Target

Downside risks to our rating and PT include: (1) a weaker-than-expected recovery in heavy-duty truck sales and construction machineiy sales; (2) worse-than-expected synergy from the Linde acquisition; and (3) successful vertical integration by end customers.
