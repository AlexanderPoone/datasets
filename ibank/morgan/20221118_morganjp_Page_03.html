Credit Strategy Update

High-yield bond spreads tightened lObp over the past week and yields are 83bp below October’s high amid easing inflation concerns, limited supply, better-than-feared earnings, and the largest stretch of retail inflows into the asset class on record aside from the stretch surrounding the pandemic. In particular, the past 4 weeks’ $11,3bn of inflows are exceeded only by the $48bn of inflows over the 12-week period between April and June 2020. Meanwhile, 3Q H Y Earnings in aggregate were better-than-feared (link), and guidance improved in aggregate from 2Q22 levels (25% negative in 2Q22, down to 21% negative in 3Q22). On the other hand, dispersion among sectors is rapidly rising. Negative outliers me lude Media, Reta.il, and Chemicals with 40-50% of companies providing negative guidance.

Positive outliers include Aerospace, Food, Services, and Energy with 0-8% of companies providing negative guidance. High-yield bond yields and spreads decreased 7bp and lObp over the past week to 9.07% and 500bp, respectively, which are down 31bp and 2bp in November. Yields are now 83bp below 10/13’s high since April 2020, whereas spreads are 136bp below their year-to-date high in early July. There was also mild outperformance down in quality after experiencing significant decompression among ratings over the previous two weeks. As well, the BB/BBB relationship has widened 40%+ off the multi-year extremes reached in late October.



By rating, BB yields are now 7.19% (-6bp w/w), B yields are 9.18% (-5bp w/w), and CCC yields are 15.31% (-25bp w/w). And BB, B and CCC spreads of 319bp (-7bp w/w), 514bp (-8bp w/w), and 1127bp (-27bp w/w) are 4% below, 1% below and 7% above their long-term non-recessionary averages. HY/IG spreads of 350bp (Obp w/w) compares to a low in April and high in July of 267bp and 473bp, respectively.

Meanwhile, BBB/BB spreads of 135bp (+5bp w/w) are now 16bp inside the 12Ms average and 42bp above 10/28’s 3-year low of 93bp and are at a high since mid-October. The HY index is up +0.67% in November with BBs (+0.90%) outperforming Single Bs (+0.88%) and CCC rated bonds (-0.71%). The US HY index is now down -10.77% year-to-date with Single B bonds (-9.56%) outperforming BB bonds by 50bp (-10.06%) and CCC bonds by 943bp (-18.99%). There is a lot of dispersion by sector year-to-date with Diversified Media (-3.49%) and Energy (-3.61%) outperforming and Retail (-18.48%) and Broadcasting (-18.26%) underperforming. Meanwhile, only one deal for $400mn has priced this week following the most active week for high-yield capital markets ($6.2bn prior)
