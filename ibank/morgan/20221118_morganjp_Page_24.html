Week in Review

High yield and leveraged loan summary

  • •    Spreads and yields - High-yield bond yields decreased 7bp to 9.07%, while spreads decreased lObp to 500bp. Five-year Treasury yields were unchanged at 3.94% and 10-year Treasury yields decreased 6bp to 3.77%. Leveraged loan yields (to 3-year takeout) were unchanged at 10.66%, while spreads decreased 2bp to 636bp.

  • •    Performance - High-yield bonds and leveraged loans returned +0.54% and +0.24% for the week, while equities (S&P 500) returned -0.17%, investmentgrade bonds returned +1.29% and emerging market bonds returned +1.72%. Year-to-date, high yield bonds and loans have provided losses of -10.77% and -0.57%, while 10-year Treasuries, investment-grade bonds, emerging market bonds, and the S&P 500 have returned -16.53%, -16.37%, -18.49%, and -16.02%, respectively.

  • •    New-issue activity - One new high-yield bond priced this week totaling $400mn, compared with five bonds priced last week totaling $6.2bn and the 5-week average of $2.2bn. YTD volume is now up to $103.2bn ($53.6bn ex-refi) versus $469.0bn ($183. Ibn) over the same period last year. As for the loan primary market, five new leveraged loans priced this week totaling $8.0bn. YTD, 313 new leveraged loans have priced totaling $226.3bn ($155.3bn ex-refi/reprice), compared to $766.2bn ($361,8bn) over the same period last year.

  • •    CLO issue activity - Five new US CLOs priced this week totaling $2.2bn, compared with the 5-week average of $2.4bn. Year-to-date 301 US CLOs have priced totaling $140.2bn, as compared to 749 US CLOs totaling $343.4bn for the same time period last year.

  • •    Demand - High-yield bond funds reported inflows totaling +$2.9BN for the week ending November 16th (1.3% of AUM). Notably, the past four weeks’ $11,3bn of inflows represents the largest 4-week total on record aside from the post pandemic stretch which saw $48bn of inflows over a 12-week period (Apr-20 - Jun-20). For the week, the HY ETFs reported inflows totaling +$2.0BN, whereas actively managed HY funds reported inflows totaling +$889mn, the largest inflow since the week ended August 3rd. Outflows for H Y bond funds YTD total a record -$43.3bn (-$7.4bn ETF) following outflows totaling -$13.2bn in FY21. Leveraged loan funds reported outflows totaling -$180mn for the week ending November 16th. For the week, the Loan ETFs reported inflows totaling +$328mn, whereas actively managed HY funds reported outflows totaling +$508mn. Net inflows for loan funds since the beginning of 2021 are down to $40.8bn, whereas dedicated Loan Fund AUM is down to $109bn from as much as $142bn in May. Outflows for Loan funds YTD total -$5.6BN (-$2.6bn ETF) which follow +$46.5bn of inflows in FY21.

  • •    Default activity - The par-weighted high-yield default rate is currently 0.84% (1.59% including distressed exchanges), while for loans the par-weighted default rate is 1.12% (1.58% including distressed exchanges). Most recent defaults include Isagenix International ($227mn, 9/30/22), K&N Engineering ($330mn, 9/30/22), Phoenix Sendees International ($445mn, 09/27/2022), Cineworld ($3.8bn, 09/08/2022), Lumileds ($1.6bn, 08/29/22), Carestream Health ($945mn, 08/23/222), Output Sendees Group ($767mn, 08/06/22), Altera Infrastructure ($975mn, 07/15/22), and Endo International ($7.9bn, 06/30/22).
