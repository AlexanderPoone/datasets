Taking stock

Following the slide of SVB and Signature Bank into receivership, and the swift policy responses by the Fed, FDIC, and Treasuiy, markets have been left wondering whether these actions will be enough. Despite the liquidity backstop provided by the Fed in the form of its Bank Term Funding Program (BTFP), fears of large outflows of uninsured deposits from other regional banks swept the industry, with First Republic Bank coming into focus, given it too held a high share of uninsured deposits relative to other regionals (see First Republic, Steven Alexopoulos, 3/17/23). Focus also shifted to European lenders, and Credit Suisse in particular, given its liquidity position had already been in the crosshairs prior to last weekend’s events (see The Financials Statement, Roberto Henriques, 3/17/23). Tumbling share prices of First Republic Bank and Credit Suisse drove a continued risk-off tone throughout the week. The liquidity support measures announced by the SNB on Thursday will buy more time for Credit Suisse, but our bank analysts think that ultimately the company will require more drastic restructuring of its overall operations. While they have no fundamental concerns for European banks, risk premiums should remain higher due to the sentiment contagion effect (see A Central Bank Bazooka, Roberto Henriques, 3/16/23).
