Equity Strategy

China continues to fade - reiterate the peaking in Eurozone vs the US trade


  • •    After a sharp -60% rally during Nov-Jan, on the back of the reopening drive, MSCI China has lost 20% since. Our China strategy team believes MSCI China can trade within the team’s base and downside case target (54-66) between now and a good part of 2H. Chinese economic momentum continued to accelerate for a bit longer than the equity indices this year, with a strong 12% qoq sa GDP print for QI, but has softened since.

  • •    The latest set of China economic numbers, for April, has underwhelmed, including IP, retail sales and FAI. This is joining peaking PMIs, 20% lower iron ore price from highs, as well as a big slowdown in China CESI. The concern is weak confidence, which has restricted the path to broadened recovery. Our economic team does not anticipate meaningful new stimulus measures for the rest of the year. In the near term, key drivers of growth appear to be softening. The consumer might not get stronger, FAI and property are both structurally challenged, the external backdrop might not help and geopolitics could stay tough.

  • •    Internal consumer mobility metrics have fully recovered, such as subway ridership and domestic flights, but granted there is more to go on the external front. Unlike in the US and Europe, though, there might not be much pent up demand, as Chinese consumers didn’t enjoy significant cash transfers during Covid lockdowns, and there are reports of wage cuts in certain industries. More than half of the increase in the Chinese savings rate seen last year has already unwound.

  • •    The property market has stabilized, post the sharp fall in activity - where new builds are 30-40% below last 3 year ranges. The weaker levels of activity look set to stay, as new land sales are lower 30% ytd. The house prices are at risk of incremental weakening on rising secondary market listings. Tier 1 house prices in China are not dissimilar to what was observed in Japan in 1990, in terms of affordability.

  • •    FAI and infrastructure spend could continue to lag, absent a fresh policy stimulus. FAI shares of GDP remain very high, compared to other countries. Fiscal stimulus has been frontloaded in QI, and even though the M2 is moving up, there might not be much further credit easing in the pipeline.

  • •    We believe that China exposure baskets might keep disappointing. Our China basket has rolled over - JPGLCHRO Index, and is losing money this year, post the strong start. We think this could continue, and more broadly we remain UW Value vs Growth style this year.

  • •    China reopening was one of the big supports for Europe trade, where Eurozone beat the US by 30%+ since last September to most recently, in USD terms. The likely China rollover is one of tire reasons why we decided to cut this trade earlier in tire month. With this closure, we have tire OWs on tire following regions globally: Japan - upgraded to OW in December, UK -record cheap with the highest dividend yield and lower than 1 beta to global equities, and Switzerland - with a clear Defensive allocation and also lower tiran 1 beta.
