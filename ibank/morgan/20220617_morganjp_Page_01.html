JPM/CSIA System Integrator Survey: Demand Cresting at High Level

Our latest proprietary system integrator survey reflects demand that is “cresting” at a high level with significantly higher impact from supply chain disruptions, not a surprise. Most integrators (83%) anticipate an increase in demand over the next 12 months, which is slightly lower than the Oct’21 survey (88%) but is a meaningful step up from the 2019/2020 survey of 64%/67%. Respondents saw -7% average revenue growth in the last 12 months and they expect -9% growth in the coming 12 months. Responses in this survey indicate -4% capex growth in ’22 as compared to 5% in the Oct’21 survey, while for ’23 capex, the growth expectation is stable at -4%. On April trends specifically, approximately 38% noted stronger demand than in Jan-Mar vs -19% indicating weaker demand (~51%/7% Apr’21), which is slightly worse than normal seasonality. Backlog commentary continues to be strong in a tight supply chain environment, not unusual, with 48% of respondents seeing stronger than normal backlog vs -50% in the Oct’21 survey. Specifically on supply chain disturbances, -98% of respondents indicated that these have a negative impact on their business (vs 94% in the Oct’21 survey) with 74% seeing a material impact, which is a significant step up from 57% in the Oct'21 survey. On pricing, respondents are seeing a -11% price increase on the products that they buy and they are passing on a ~7% price hike to their customers on average. Of the respondents, -77% highlighted that they are being charged by vendors with prices set on the day of order and -75% of respondents indicated that price increases are a limiting factor to growth in terms of customer budget. Approximately 74% of respondents said they are seeing project modifications due to inflationary pressures while -32% are seeing cancellations. Turning to software, MES adoption appears to be relatively slow as -31% of respondents indicated that MES adoption is minimal vs -20% in the Oct’21 survey.

System integrators are third-party firms focusing on delivering technically advanced capital projects, a proxy for upward of 70% of sales for automation suppliers. Responses are slanted toward North America, similar to the overall SI industry, but with some global representation as well.
