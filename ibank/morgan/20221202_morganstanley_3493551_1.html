Tech Bytes I Asia Pacific

2023 vs. 2019

We draw parallels with past upturns and consider risk-reward to be especially attractive now with the consensus downgrade cycle similar to the 2018 trough. Good news is hard to come by near the bottom, and on the balance of probabilities we view that 2023 is the start of a recovery year.

Navigating tech cycles - experience required. The tech industry often repeats previous cycles, but they rarely run on schedule, and trying to figure out the exact start and end dates of cycle inflections is unrealistic. But, we should build portfolios that are durable enough against down- and up-cycles with the understanding that they are a natural extension of markets even if we don't know when they will occur. In the current market, we are reminded that cycles often rhyme and by studying what drives an upturn, one can Look for similarities to spot new up cycles. From this, we believe that: (1) it is better to be conservative than generous on valuations; (2) it is better to be cautious than optimistic on earnings; and (3) it is better to be contrarian than unhappy near the bottom when Looking for entry points.

Conservative rather than generous on valuation. Putting the current sector correction in the context of previous tech cycle inflections, we are at a very late stage of the down-cycle for market expectations. While it seems reasonable to assume that a lot of bad news has been discounted, with the -45% average correction in tech stocks from the peak and the majority of investors we've spoked to anticipating recession coming in 2023, some simple 'what's in the price' analysis suggests that markets could have Limited downside absent any deterioration in geopolitical news flow. A cyclical downturn would require the tech sector to fall -10% from here to previous troughs and rise 107% to peak. Based on the average earnings reductions for Asian tech stocks of -20% this year vs. our Leading earnings model points to a -16% drop over the coming months, it should be clear that consensus is now erring on the side of a caution' vis-a-vis our top-down base case assumption, which is an upturn driven by YoY improvements from 1Q23.

Incrementally less cautious on earnings - what happens next?
