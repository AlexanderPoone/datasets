Investment Thesis, Valuation and Risks

Prada S.P.A (Neutral: Price Target: HK$43.00)

Investment Thesis

Prada is a key luxury goods player in Fashion & Leather Goods. Its portfolio of brands includes Prada, Miumiu, Church’s and Car Shoe. In FYI4, the Prada brand represented 83% of Sales and 92% of EBIT. The group experienced a sharp drop in LFL in FY14 in particular in its high margin leather goods product category. This, combined with elevated fixed costs linked to the recent aggressive store roll-out plan, led to a sharp squeeze in margin in FY14. Management aims to renew momentum in its leather goods sales by rebalancing its offer with a wider price range and improved merchandise mix. The effects of these initiatives remain muted so far. On a more positive note, we expect FX to help sales and margins meaningfully in FYI5 and FY16 (some delayed impact owing to hedging into FYI6) and management appears committed to control costs more actively with a clear curbing of store expansion plans into FYI 5. We remain Neutral on the stock.

Valuation

Our Jan-17 DCF target price of HK$43 is based on 2015-2019 explicit forecasts, medium-term growth of 6.0%, terminal growth of 3.0%, and a WACC of 8.3%.

Risks to Rating and Price Target

Key downside risks include any abrupt changes in euro exposure and A&P budget, sustained pressure on its core leather goods offer (vs our expected bottoming). Key upside risks include higher-than-expected sales growth at Miu Miu, significant opex cuts (vs our expected cost control), and marked improvement in the macro environment notably in Asia.
