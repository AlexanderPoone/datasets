October’s bond issuer downgrades exceeded upgrades for the first time since 2020

The historic upgrade wave for high-yield bond issuers has waned. In particular, October witnessed the first-time downgraded bond issuers exceeded upgrades since December 2020. As well, October included the most downgraded issuers (26) issuers since November 2020 and the fewest # of upgraded issuers (14) since October 2020. That said Centene’s $16bn upgrade to investment-grade did boost 2022’s US Rising Star volume to a record S105.6bn (25 issuers) as that trend continues apace. In the details, October saw 14 upgrades totaling $42.2bn and 26 downgrades totaling $26.3bn. Note the 2022 bond upgrade-to-downgrade ratio stands at a favorable 1.69:1, which compares to 2.31:1 in 2021 and 0.34:1 in 2020. And the LTM upgrade-to-downgrade ratio stands at 1.74:1, which is below March’s highest level on record (2.47:1). Year-to-date there are 271 companies upgraded totaling $532bn, which compares to 162 companies downgraded totaling $259bn. Note 53% of bond issuers were downgraded in 2020 which compares to a high of 45% in FY2009. And since the beginning of 2021 100% of 2020’s 523 downgrades have transitioned to upgrades.
