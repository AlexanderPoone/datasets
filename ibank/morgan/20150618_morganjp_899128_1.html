Aluminum Corporation of China

-H

A-share placement helps ease high debt burden

Chalco’s A-share placement offers some relief from its high debt burden and reduces its loss per share to existing shareholders. However, with aluminium prices back near 12-month lows in an oversupplied domestic market, we believe the company’s high-cost assets will continue to struggle in losses. Given the cl7% YTD H-share rally (versus HSCEI’s 12%), we believe much of Chalco’s restructuring story is now priced in. Reiterate Underweight.

  • •    Completion of A-share placement. Following several unsuccessful attempts since early 2012, Chalco announced that it had completed a non-public A-share placement to eight investors. The company will issue 1,379m new A shares, representing 14.4% of A-shares (or 10% of total issued shares) at Rmb5.80/share, a 42% discount to its June 15 close. Net proceeds of Rmb7.9bn raised from the issue will be used to finance 1) an alumina project in Xing county (Rmb4.7bn), 2) the Zhongzhou mining plant (Rmbl.3bn), and 3) working capital (Rmb2.0bn).

  • •    Reduced debt burden. The placement results in: a) reduced loss per share (-15%) from lower interest costs and increased shares (+10%); b) increased BVPS (+20%); and c) lowered net gearing to 221% (-75ppt). Chinalco, Chalco’s parent, will see its holding reduced to 34.7% (from 38.2%).
