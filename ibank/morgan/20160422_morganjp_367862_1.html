Zhuzhou CRRC Times Electric Co Ltd

1Q16 beat above-consensus JPMe by >10% on higher margins despite negative mix change

  • •    1Q16 results beat expectations by >10%. ZZCRRC reported 1Q16 NPAT of Rmb567M (+42% Y/Y), achieving 17% of FYI6 JPMe (vs. the historical achieving ratio of 14%), while JPMe was 4% ahead of the Street. The earnings beat was driven by better profitability, as net margin expanded 3.6ppts Y Y. Revenue rose 20% Y/Y, driven by a c50% surge in revenue from Mus, to cRmbl.2B, offsetting the decline in locomotives. GPM expanded Ippt oya, to 39.1%, despite the much lower contribution from locomotives, while the opex ratio declined to 18.4% (down 1.2ppts Y/Y). Maintain OW.

  • •    Full-year guidance unchanged. Management reiterated its FY16 guidance at the analyst call, expecting some growth for MUs (JPMe +7% Y/Y revenue growth), while RTVs should see solid growth, with revenue guidance of Rmb 2-2.5B vs. Rmbl.9B in FY15 (JPMe cRmb2.2B). However, locomotives will continue to see pressure this year (JPMe flat growth).

  • •    Healthy orders on hand for MUs and RTVs, locomotives the weak spot. As of end-2015, ZZCRRC had orders for c200 MU trainsets on hand, close to the amount of orders it received from CRC last year, which is enough to cover this year’s deliveries until early 4Q (assuming no new procurement from CRC this year), while RTV backlog stood at cRmb5B. Locomotives, on the other hand, have less visibility and will depend on CRC’s procurement this year.

  • •    Sales to CNR to rise more meaningfully from 2017. Management noted that it has been in the process of obtaining certification for various products, which could translate into more CNR revenue from FYI7 (ZZCRRC’s systems that were previously installed only on ex-CSR equipment need to be certified before they can be installed on CNR trains, a process that generally takes 1-3 years). Further, the revenue synergies from the CSR/CNR merger may come through more quickly if the rollout of China Standaid MUs accelerates.

  • •    Update on new businesses - IGBT and SMD, turnaround on track. ZZCRRC’s 1700V and 3 300V IGBT products are already being sold and used in certain subway trains and locomotives, while it is still testing its 6500V module, which can be used on MUs. For SMD, ZZCRRC refinanced most of the expensive shareholder loan in 2015, while it is working to boost local sales as a way to turn the business around more quickly.
