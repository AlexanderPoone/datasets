The number of loan issuers downgraded in October were the most since May-20 and outpaced upgrades for the sixth consecutive month

Ratings trends for loans are not as favorable as for bond issuers. In fact, October’s # of loan issuers downgraded (34) exceeded upgrades (13) for a sixth consecutive month. October also included the most downgraded issuers May 2020. For context. May, June, July, August, September and October are the first-time downgraded loan issuers exceeded upgrades since November 2020. Notably, October saw the least number of issuers and amount of upgrades and the most number of issuers and amount of downgrades since May 2020. The 2022 upgrade-to-downgrade ratio stands at 0.77:1, which compares to 2.00:1 in 2021 and 0.29:1 in 2020. This also sits in contrast to rating agency trends in high-yield bonds where upgrades easily surpass downgrades in 2022 with a ratio of 1.69:1. Since the beginning of 2021 there are 583 companies upgraded totaling $530bn, which compares to 449 companies downgraded totaling $405bn. Note the 640 loan issuers downgraded in 2020 indicated 47% of 13 51 issuers were downgraded. In other words, more than 90% of 2020’s downgrades have transitioned to upgrades since the beginning of 2021.
