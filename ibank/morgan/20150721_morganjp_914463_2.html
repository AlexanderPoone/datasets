Key catalysts for the stock price:

  • 1)    Rising intra-Asia trade and logistics demand plus E-commerce penetration.

  • 2)    More companies looking to outsource their logistics services to improve cost efficiency. 3) Trade liberalization, 4) China’s One-Belt-One-Road strategy, driving increased cargo flow between China and ASEAN/rest of the world. 5) Value-accretive acquisitions.


Upside risks to our view:

1) Value-accretive acquisitions that boost earnings growth. 2) Unlocking of value via the disposal of less optimal HK warehouse properties to reinvest in higher-yielding assets. 3) Margin improvement in logistics and freight forwarding segments. 4) Fair value gains of investment properties. 5) Industry consolidation.


Downside risks to our view:

1) 43.17%-owned Kerry TJ’s earnings dsappoint as it contributes 26% of Kerry Logistics Network’s core profit. 2) International freight forwarding expansion may result in stronger revenue growth but lower profit margins. 3) Reduction in fair value of investment properties. 4) Excessive competition among the logistics service providers, particularly in China. 5) Infrastructure constraints, especially in Emerging Markets. 6) Customers shifting their manufacturing bases from China to onshore or nearby countries with lower production costs where KLN has limited access. 7) Weakening currencies of overseas markets. 8) Value-dilutive acquisitions and integration challenges post acquisition.

Our June-16 price target for Kerry Logistics Network is HK$14. This is based on 18x P/E, in line with its historical average valuation since listing. This implies an EV/EBITDA of 11x, which is in line with the Asia Pacific and the global logistics sector's average valuation. Our price target is also well supported by our sum-of-the-parts (SOTP) fair valuation of HKS14.3 per share and DCF-based fair valuation of HK$15.5 per share, assuming a WACC of 6.3% and stable growth of 2.0%.
