Assessing Valuation and Positioning in Turkey

How much near-term upside is left?

The pattern of long-term TRY weakness/MSCI Turkey sell-offs has been reversed in the short-term by CBRT hikes is well-established. MSCI Turkey index rallied 30% MTD yet still down 19% YTD, underperformed EM index by c.30%. JPM strategy team maintain Turkey as N in CEEMEA allocation, highlighting a near term 20-25% upside potential in the index.

Figure 1: MSCI Turkey vs MSCI EM (in USD)

Rebased to 100 on 26 Nov'10


This rally together with an almost 2% downgrade in earnings post Q3 reporting has brought the market P/E to 6.5x, up from 5.6x- yet the discount to EM on 12M forward P/E remains at 54%, close to its highest levels of the past 10 years. In terms of blended valuations, MSCI Turkey Financials is on 3.6x 12m fwd P/E, a 46% discount to its 10-year average while the non-financials are 9.6x 12m fwd PE, only a 7% discount. That valuation discrepancy clearly supports the call for the banks over the rest of the market, which is now a consensus call.
