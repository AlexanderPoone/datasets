November’s rally in leveraged loan prices slowed over the past week amid a focus on earnings and the most active primary market since early June. Leveraged loan prices increased +$0.05 over the past week to $93.22 and are now $1.20 above early July’s year-to-date low with the average price for BB loans increasing $0.13 to $97.58, while Single B loan prices increased $0.17 to $93.16 and Split B/CCC loan prices decreased $0.61 to $75.64. Meanwhile, leveraged loan yields decreased Ibp and spreads to maturity remained unchanged over the past week to 9.79% and 575bp, which are down 49bp and 22bp month-to-date and are now 10.66% and 636bp to a 3yr takeout. The Leveraged Loan index is providing a +1.17% gain in November with B (+1.53%) outperforming BB (+1.13%) and CCC (-0.34%) performance. Notably, loans are providing their 3rd best monthly return since Jan 2021 and is on track to be the 4th best November on record. Loans are also outperforming HY bonds by 50bp month-to-date. Bl, B2, B3, and Caal loans are providing returns month-to-date of+1.30%, +1.56%, +1.46%, and -0.88%, respectively. For context, Bl, B2, B3, and Caal loans are providing returns year-to-date of +0.60%, -1.45%, -3.26%, and -11.69%, respectively. Using Moody’s, meanwhile, BB, B1/B2, B3, and CCC loans have a spread to maturity of 340bp, 551bp, 793bp, and 1772bp, respectively. A couple of relationships stand out.

One, the B1ZB2 spread versus BB loans of 210bp is comfortably above the past 12Ms and non-recessionary averages of 152bp and 126bp, respectively. Second, CCC loans, which trade 979bp wide of B3 loans are pricing in a recession (see table). Given we expect a downgrade wave and build in the CCC loan universe, the current cohort of B3 loans should underperform amid hindered demand conditions. Notably, CCC loan spreads are already at a high since June 2020. Year-to-date, the Leveraged Loan index is providing a -0.57% loss with BB (+2.23%) and B loans (-1.20%) outperforming CCC loans (-13.01%). There is a lot of dispersion by sector year-to-date with Energy (+5.61%) and Utility (+3.89%) outperforming and Consumer Products (-5.10%) and Metals/Mining (-4.05%) underperforming. For context, this compares to YTD losses for HY (10.77%), IG (-16.37%), and the S&P 500 (-16.02%). Meanwhile, the CLO primary has priced $4.8bn month-to-date following $8.7bn of ex-re fi/resets in October; year-to-date activity of $118.4bn is down -16% vs a year ago. Lastly, 5 deals priced this week for $8.0bn, the highest weekly issuance since the week ending 6/10 and boosting November’s loan issuance to $14.8bn, which is the highest monthly issuance since June.
