A partial IP recovery in February

Industrial production remains volatile and has not consistently exceeded pre-pandemic levels. After a series of declines, IP expanded 2.6% m/m sa in December followed by a 2.2% drop in January. IP increased 0.7% in February, a tenth more than we forecast and two-tenths above the consensus, only partially recovering the losses from January to stand back at the August 2021 level (Figure 2). Industrial production is now rising 5% 3m/3m saar, up from 2.2% in January. The February expansion was widespread with capital goods production increasing 1.9%, intermediate goods production rising 1.6%, and consumer goods production up 0.3%.

The nowcaster and the March PMI are sending positive signals thus far. The IP expansion lifted our IQ nowcaster to 2.1%, from 1.8% last week, and above our call for a 0.6% q/q saar GDP expansion in IQ (Figure 3). Also, the manufacturing PMI firmed significantly from 49.6 to 52.3 in March, the highest level in six months. However, the FGV industrial confidence continued falling through last month.
