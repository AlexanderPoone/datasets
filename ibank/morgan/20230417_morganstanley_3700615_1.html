They Think It's All Over

I’m not talking about Arsenal’s title challenge, which still has Legs despite recent performance. Yet, EM currencies are more likely to suffer a setback as US growth concerns mount through 2Q, raising spillover risks. We turn bullish on USD and bearish on EM FX, but stay neutral on rates and credit.

FX & EM Strategy: The recent weakness in USD, rebound in EM FX and strong equity market performance are at odds with a rising recession probability. We expect a tactical change in direction and turn bullish on USD and bearish on EM FX. We like long USD versus KRW, CLP, ZAP and PHP and add long BR L/M XN. In G10 we are long USD versus EUR, AUD and CAD.

Sovereign Credit Strategy: This week we look at all the upcoming maturities in EM HY sovereign credit. After low maturities in 2023, the first half of 2024 is when maturities increase materially, with 4Q24-2Q25 also very high. By end-2025, we see 11% at higher risk, defined as sovereigns where average yields are significantly above 10%, and 15% at moderate risk, defined as sovereigns where yields are around or slightly above 10%. Turkey and Egypt have the largest maturities, yet with focus Likely on Tunisia, Pakistan and Kenya instead, given lower available liquidity. This week we move down to a neutral view on Qatar and Abu Dhabi while adding Saudi Arabia and South Africa like stances. We also add an Oman like stance and suggest buying OMAN 32 versus MOROC 33. We close our Jordan dislike stance following eurobond issuance.

LatAm Macro Strategy: We find that MXN and CLP (the two most expensive currencies in the region) would be particularly vulnerable to risks that the market could price in a US-Led hard Landing, while BRL would be least exposed. We add Long USD/CLP and Long BRL/MXN. We stay long PeruGB 20 29 (FX-hedged) and maintain our 9m CLPxCAM payer.

Asia Macro Strategy: We have been on the road, visiting clients in Singapore and discussing the top 10 questions in Asia/China. We add Long USD/KR W to our Long USD/PHP recommendation to increase our Long USD exposure. We also add a short USD/TWD strangle as we expect Cross-Strait tension to reduce. We keep short SGD/CNH and short TWD/INR. In rates, we keep all three of our trades: receive 1-year CNY NDIRS, pay 1y1y KRW NDIRS and receive 2-year SGD versus pay 2-year USD.

CEEMEA Macro Strategy: Currencies in the CEE region continue to appreciate while the correlation between duration and core yields has increased. We still think that markets are pricing too many cuts in some countries, such as the Czech Republic. In FX, we go long USD/ZAR following the change in our EM FX stance to bearish.
