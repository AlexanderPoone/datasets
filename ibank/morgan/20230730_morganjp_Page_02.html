Improving outlook for outbound flight supply

If we compare monthly data in 2023 vs. the same period in 2019, outbound passenger volume recovered from 9% of 2019 level in Jan to 44% in Jun. Earlier this month, CAAC said it expects outbound travel capacity to recover to 60-65% of pre-COVID level in 2H23. As the recent Politburo meeting urged an increase in outbound flights, we see potential upside risk to this estimate.

Revenue analysis from a geographical perspective

Revenue

We forecast 2023 revenue to be 21% above 2019 level, driven mainly by domestic travel which we estimate will be 35% above 2019 level. While outbound flight supply has not recovered back to 2019 levels and is on a gradual and progressive recovery trajectory, we note Trip.com is quickly gaining share in the outbound market. We therefore lift our 2023 outbound revenue estimate to 75% of 2019 level (previously 70%) and 2024 outbound revenue estimate to 120% of 2019 level (previously 100%). We forecast 19% YoY total revenue growth in 2024, thanks to a 60% YoY increase in outbound revenue, assuming a full normalization of outbound flights. We expect domestic travel growth will slow to 10% YoY in 2024 due to the high base.

Margins

We expect 2023 non-GAAP OPM to increase by 7ppt YoY to 26% in 2023, thanks to strong domestic revenue recovery as well as the significant cost optimization in the past few years. Outbound travel OPM should remain relatively weak due to limited revenue recovery. We expect outbound OPM to reach 36% in 2024 (4ppt higher than 2019 level), driven by revenue recovery (20% above 2019 level) and eased industry competition. On the other hand, we expect domestic OPM to retreat slightly in 2024, as the company invests in user experience and service capacity. Overall, we forecast a mild Ippt group OPM expansion in 2024 to 27%.
