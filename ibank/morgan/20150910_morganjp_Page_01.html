Xinyi Glass

Lowering PT to HK$5.2 - We see little downside before a recovery


Overweight

0868.HK, 868 HK

Price: HK$3.95


▼ Price Target: HK$5.20 Previous: HK$5.50


CHINA CONNECT;


Xinyi Glass (XYG) is one of China’s largest and most diversified glass producers. Despite some drags from Yuan depreciation, we still expect earnings growth of c.15% in 2016 driven by modest sales and earnings growth in all key product segments. However, a meaningful re-rating depends on a stronger recovery in pricing and margins for float glass which remains elusive. Maintain OW with lower PT of HK$5.2 (from HK$5.5). However, given what could be a lengthy wait before a recovery in construction activity, we currently prefer subsidiary Xinyi Solar.

  • •    Factoring in currency changes. Our estimated impact on earnings for XYG is quite minor as both revenues and costs are denominated in the local currency. We do not expect a large FX loss on translation of HK dollar debt as the functional currency of XYS is the HK dollar. XYG shares have fallen significantly (down 7.1% in the past 3 months, outperforming the HSCEI by 10.9% over this period) and factor in quite a negative scenario in terms of demand and pricing for glass. We see little downside at the current forward PR and PB multiples.

  • •    Lowering FY16 earnings and PT to HKS5.2. XYG’s functional and reported currency is the HK dollar. Therefore the currency’s recent depreciation will hurt the company. Aside from FX issues, we also lower our estimated price increase of float glass in 2016 from 10% to 5% after seeing the disappointing margins for float glass.

The key downside risks to our PT are falling demand for construction glass that could result from a cooling property market in China, and further weakness in PV glass demand from Europe.
