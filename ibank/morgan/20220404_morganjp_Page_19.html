Our concern is that Ba.nR.ep has underemphasized the expectations channel. By taking a longer time to reach a neutral stance in terms of the real policy rate (estimated at 1.8-1.9% by the research staff) the board is opening the door for a rapid rise in inflation expectations. Thus, more hikes will be needed in order to re-anchor expectations. Moreover, by implicitly assuming a policy continuity regardless of the election outcome, we think the Board has eschewed a risk management approach to monetary policy. In the event that the policies of the current frontrunner (Petro) lead to less policy continuity than some of his advisors currently suggest, we see the neutral policy rate rising structurally (on a higher credit premium), and thus a more restrictive stance would be needed to reanchor expectations compared to a policy continuity scenario.

We adjust our call for BanRep to keep moving in lOObp clips in the next two voting meetings (April and June, the latter occurring after a likely second round vote), with a final 50bp hike in July to take the policy rate to a terminal rate of 7.5%. Before we expected BanRep to pause at 6.5%.

Recognizing that the terms of trade improvement can provide some breathing room, we still have a hard time seeing inflation expectations heading back toward the target. This would keep the pressure on BanRep to act in order to bolster its credibility. On the flip side, confirmation of a policy continuity scenario should help to guide inflation expectations back toward the target. We think at this moment it is too close to call which scenario will prevail.
