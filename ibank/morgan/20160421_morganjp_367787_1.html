China State Construction

Lifting PT to HK$18 - strong orders in 1Q

China State Construction (CSC) is one of the largest contractors for affordable housing (AH) in China and one of the largest builders in HK/Macau. We recently visited a large combined affordable housing and infrastructure project in Jiaxing and detail in this note the growth potential of affordable housing for CSC and other construction related companies. In addition, we have lifted our assumption for 2016 new contracts due to strong tender wins in 1 QI6.

  • •    Jiaxing site visit reveals a robust pipeline. Our visit to a combined affordable housing and infrastructure project in the city of Jiaxing, near Shanghai, revealed a robust construction market and a government policy that promotes the healthy development of homeowner equity by allowing low income occupants of affordable housing (AH) units to sell their units after 5 years. We see the use of AH as a good tool by the government to mitigate soaring home prices as well as lifting low income people into the middle class. Although CSC has over HK$7bn in projects in Jiaxing, it is still a small portion of the affordable housing that the city is building. During our visit, we also learned that construction activity has picked up in 1Q16, pushing up the price of some building materials by 30% since 4Q15.

  • •    Lifting new orders and sales. We have adjusted our new order assumption from HK$76bn to HK$80bn to reflect the strong orders signed in 1Q16 totaling HK$21bn and a general improvement in China’s construction and credit markets. We have lifted earnings slightly to reflect higher order levels and our new EPS estimate for CSC is 2% and 11% higher than consensus for 2016 and 2017, respectively. We have lifted our Dec 16 PT to HK$18 (prev. HK$17) to reflect the earnings changes.

  • •    CSC trades at 8.7 x FY16E P/E. Our DCF-based PT (Dec-16) of HK$18.0 implies FY17E P/E of 10.8x and FY16E P/B of 2.4x. Key risks are falling construction activity in China and lower-than-expected returns on long-term projects in China. Another risk would be a shortage of workers or a hike in raw materials that could crimp margin.
