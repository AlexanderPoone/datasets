Power Assets Holdings Ltd 1st Take: No Special DPS, but one more project under bidding

Industry View                Stock Rating                 PriceTarget

In-Line                   Equal-weight             HK$75.00

After its board meeting on May 12, PAH announced that it will not pay a special dividend. We are disappointed with the decision, but we believe the stock will not react significantly negatively, as management said it is pursuing further acquisitions.

What happened: On May 12, the PAH board announced no special dividend, due to three reasons:

  • 1)    PAH participated on Husky midstream assets on April 26 (cash investment of HK$5.3b).

  • 2)    PAH is participating in the final stage of another acquisition, which is of larger size than Husky - we believe this may be the 50.4% stake in Ausgrid N SW, with a reported estimate price of >A$10bn (enterprise value), based on a Bloomberg report on Feb 26,2016. The final bidding results are expected to be out in the middle of 2016, according to the report.

  • 3)    PAH has begun bidding for another project of similar size to Ausgrid.

On the one hand, PAH's active participation in acquisitions is in line with our view that PAH, as the preferred acquisition platform for power and gas projects, still has acquisition growth potential; on the other hand, given its HK$68b cash by end 2015, and the fact that the Husky transaction used only HK$5.3banda 2H15 dividend of only HK$4.3b, there should be at least H K$ 58b on hand. With the surprise decision of no special dividend today, we would not rule out the possibility that, should the two following acquisitions fail, CKI might pursue another attempt to merge with PAH by the end this year, in order for CKH/CKI togain access to the HK$58b cash pile.To be clear, though, CKI has not expressed any intention to carry out any such an attempt.
