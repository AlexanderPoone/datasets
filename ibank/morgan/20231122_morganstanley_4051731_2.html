How is inflation tracking in Asia?

The return of low-flation: We have been of the view that Asia is transitioning back towards the pre-pandemic Low-flation environment (see From inflation to low-flation, Oct 15 & Rapid Disinflation Underway, Jun 18). A combination of factors has exerted disinflationary pressures on the region. These include deflation in China amid deleveraging headwinds, lower commodity prices, and weaker core good prices. The GDP deflator-the broadest measure of price pressures in the economy - remained in deflation in China at -1.4%Y in 3Q23, while Asia ex China ex Japan's GDP deflator is tracking at a relatively weak 1.8%Y. Aggregate headline and core inflation for the region had reached 24- and19-month Lows in September and are tracking at an even weaker 1.8%Y and 2.0%Y in October, respectively. About 78% (by PPP weight) of economies in the region already have inflation within their respective central banks' comfort zones.
