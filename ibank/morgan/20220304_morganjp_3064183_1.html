US High Grade Strategy: Uncertainties come in threes

High Grade Strategy

  • •    HG bond spreads continue to widen, driven by both the broader geopolitical developments and heavy bond supply. HG bonds are underperforming HY, CDX and other comparable markets.

  • •    HG bond markets are dealing with three large uncertainties: the Fed, bond supply and Ukraine (which is most directly impacting commodity prices). The Fed's path near term became less uncertain this week after Chairman Powell’s congressional testimony, but the other two remain significant headwinds for HG bond spreads.

  • •    Uncertainty on the impact of higher commodity prices on economic growth is the next phase of the discussion, as investors try to assess how significantly growth will be impacted and what impact this may have on coiporate earnings and creditworthiness.

  • •    HG bond supply in Jan-Feb was the 2nd most active start to a year, and March supply is continuing this trend. This shows that there is a meeting of the minds between issuers and investors at the current wider spread levels (plus new issue concessions). This is not supportive for tightening of secondary market spreads, but it does suggest we may be approaching a turning point. Investors in new issues have been generally monetizing recent new issue concessions.

  • •    The front end of the spread curve is underperforming, as higher yields are a relatively more important positive factor in the long end than front end. This is leading to more negative CDS-Bond basis.
