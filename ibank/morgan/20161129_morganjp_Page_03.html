Investment Thesis, Valuation and Risks

Tingyi (Cayman Islands) Holding Corp (Underweight; Price Target: HK$7.00)

Investment Thesis

We continue to maintain our UW rating for Tingyi. Going into 2017 we argue that: i) There is no major improvement in beverage product as far as we can see. While low base for beverages and fixing water pricing is likely to keep beverage momentum positive for three more quarters, we do not see any real momentum in the product that could lead to sustainable growth, ii) Noodles industry volumes are declining and Tingyi has not gained back market share. Tingyi’s 3Q noodles market share continued to weaken and has not delivered innovation needed by the market so far. hi) Sales momentum is improving but raw material costs are moving up; thus, it is difficult to see sharp improvement in margins. The stock trades at 27x 17E P/E with c8% recurring earnings CAGR in local currency post 17E. Given stretched valuations and little conviction on sustainable growth that should come from consumer pull, we maintain our UW rating with a revised Dec-17 PT of HK$7.

Valuation

Our Dec-17 PT is HK$7. We value Tingyi on a 21 x one-year forward P/E, which is 1 standard deviation below the historical mean.

Risks to Rating and Price Target

Upside risks include: 1) a sudden sharp improvement in beverages sales via strong product development and market share gains and similarly better-than-expected sales in noodles on the back of products upgrade; 2) a further sharp decline in input costs leading to notable gross margin expansion; and 3) Pepsi margins coming through better than expected.
