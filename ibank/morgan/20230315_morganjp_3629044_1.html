China’s January-February macro indicators show a solid growth rebound upon post-Covid economic reopening. On the production side, IP came in marginally below expectations to rise 2.4%oya in Jan-Feb (consensus: 2.6%; JPM: 2.5%). This translates into solid average monthly growth of 1.3%m/m sa in Jan-Feb, on our estimates. On the demand side, retail sales growth rebounded to 3.5%oya (consensus: 3.5%; JPM: 2.5%), withaverage monthly growth of 3.5% m/msa. On the investment side, FAI growth beat expectations to grow 5.5%oya in Jan-Feb (consensus: 4.5%; JPM: 4.0%), with ongoing solid growth in infra-FAI and manufacturing FAI, along with a much narrower decline in real estate FAI. In addition, recall that the Jan-Feb trade report released last week beat expectations and showed solid sequential improvement.
