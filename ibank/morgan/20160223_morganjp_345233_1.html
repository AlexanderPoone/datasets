Hong Kong: January7 CPI rises 2.7%oya driven by food prices

Hong Kong’s January headline CPI inflation came in slightly above consensus, but in line with our expectations at 2.7%oya (JPMorgan: 2.7%oya; consensus: 2.5%oya). On a seasonally adjusted basis, the index rose 0. l%m/m sa in January, which was the same pace as December. Netting out the effects of the one-off relief measures, CPI inflation rose 0.3%m/m sa, or up 2.6%oya in January (vs. 2.4%oya in December).

Seasonal rise in food prices drives inflation. With Lunar New Year falling on February 8 (eleven days ahead of last year), the seasonal rise in food prices ahead of the holidays was pushed into January. As expected, food prices, which rose 0.4%m/m sa in January, was the main driver of inflation in the month while other major categories remained benign. In particular, housing price inflation has remained quite soft, rising just 0. l%m/m sa in January.

Focus on tomorrow’s budget. Whether the government will continue to roll out relief measures during the FY2016/17 will shape the inflation trajectory for the rest of the year. Given the weight of housing (31.66%) in the CPI, housing relief measures will have the most impact on inflation. Last year property rates were waived for the first 2 quarters in the fiscal year (with a ceiling of $2,500 per quarter for each property), along with the waiving of one month’s rent for lower income public housing tenants. While there has been debate on the benefit of these short-term measures, it is one of the more direct ways to boost consumption in what is likely to be another difficult year for Hong Kong’s economy.
