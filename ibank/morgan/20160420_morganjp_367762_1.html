China Gas Utilities

Our thoughts on Zhejiang's gas price cut

Shares of'China gas utilities fell by -10% today, vs HSCEI -2%, due to concerns on the potential reduction of end-user gas prices in Zhejiang. Based on our estimates, the EPS impact on gas utilities should be <2%. Hence, current shares have priced in a broader implementation of this policy to other provinces. However, there’s no evidence to suggest other regions will follow suit. In the past, Anhui and Hebei provinces have issued opinions on controlling gas utilities’ returns at ~8%, but actual implementation has not taken place. Today’s selloff has priced in a >10% reduction in gas utilities’ gas sales margin, on our estimates. We believe this is overdone and would use the opportunity to buy ENN, CR Gas and China Gas on back of stronger gas demand this year.

  • •    Reduction of gas prices in Zhejiang. The Zhejiang province has issued a notice to reduce gas transmission fees by -20% as a way to reduce operating costs for businesses, capping non-residential end-user prices at Rmb3.4/m3. Users of coal-to-gas boilers will pay a lower price of <Rmb2.79/m3. Meanwhile, city-gate prices for non-residential users (input cost for gas utilities) will be reduced by RmbO. l/m3, or 4%, to Rmb2.19/m3. See tables below. (Source: Zhejiang government, 6 Apr 2016, link)

  • •    EPS impact from Zhejiang price cut. Of the gas utilities under our coverage, ENN is most exposed to Zhejiang, with 8% of total gas sales to that province, followed by Towngas China (4%), CR Gas (2%) and China Gas (1%). The proposed policy would result in a Rmb().l-O.3/m3 reduction in current dollar margin of ~Rmbl.()/m3. Overall impact on EPS should be minimal, at 1-2%. (See Table 2 below)

  • •    Broader impact. Gas utilities earn a dollar margin of ~Rmb0.7 for every unit of natural gas they sell. In the past, they have been able to maintain a stable dollar margin and passed on any price increases to end-users. The policy in Zhejiang is the first to specifically reduce gas utilities’ dollar margin. One reason may be because the dollar margin in that province could be as high as Rmbl.2/m3, vs national average of *Rmb().7/m3. Among the gas utilities under our coverage, gas sales make up 40-50% of total gross profit. A 10% reduction in dollar margin would have a ~5% impact on gross profit.

  • •    Other proposed gas policies: Anhui. According to the 2016 annual working plan of Anhui Price Bureau, the government will implement relevant policies this year to help enterprises reduce costs on energy, financing, logistics, etc., and will conduct a reform on the prices of natural gas, electricity, and water. City transmission tariff will be lowered, while gas is recommended to be traded directly.

  • •    Other proposed gas policies: Hebei: According to the [2015-119] document issued by Hebei Price Bureau in Jun 2015, transmission tariff for city gas distributors should be set with the reference to IRR or ROA, which should not be higher than 8% and 7%, respectively. This policy has not been implemented in Hebei.
