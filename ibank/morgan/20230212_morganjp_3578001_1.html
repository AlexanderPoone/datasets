China

2022 current account surplus at 2.3% of GDP; expect notable narrowing this year

The State Administration of Foreign Exchange (SAFE) has released China’s preliminary balance of payments (BoP) data for 2022. The current account surplus widened to US$417.5 billion in 2022 (2.3% of GDP), compared to US$317.3 billion in 2021 (1.8% of GDP).

The further widening of China’s current account surplus in 2022 laigely reflects external trade performance, as the goods sector surplus expanded to $685.6 billion in 2022 (vs $562.7 billion in 2021). Looking back, following remarkable growth in 2020-21, Chinese exporters started to face headwinds in 2022, including supply-chain distortions related to the Shanghai lockdown in 2Q, followed by growing concerns about slowing external demand. A breakdown of shipments to major markets highlights broad-based softening in DM demand in recent months, with exports to the US leading the downturn, along with decline inexports to theEU and Japan. It is also worth noting that, having expanded notably through the pandemic, China’s tech exports appear to have peaked, falling 2.8%yoy in 2022. For the year as a whole, total goods exports under the current account grew moderately by 4.6%yoy (slowing from 28. l%yoy in2021). Meanwhile, the growth rate in goods imports slowed even further, with modest growth of 1.0%yoy in 202 2 (vs 3 2.7%yoy in 2021), as sluggish domestic demand growth amid Covid drags and a notable housing market slowdown restrained China’s import demand.
