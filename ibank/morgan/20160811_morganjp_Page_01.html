The Wharf (Holdings) Limited

Retail sales outlook improving


Overweight

A Price Target: HK$59.00

Previous: HK$56.60


Despite the 1H16 results beat, we do not think the market will be too excited about the shares in the short term given that the run-up of the share price appears to factor in most of the improvement. However, the results do not contain much new negative evidence to dislike the company. We believe the medium-term positive is the improving trend of retail sales in June and July and the numerous mall openings in 2017. Upcoming news about the potential restructuring of its telecom assets will be the next catalyst, in our view. We maintain our OW rating.

  • •    Wharf 1H16 results were stronger than expected. Wharfs reported 1H16 underlying earnings were 15% above our estimate. The beat was mainly from 1) stronger-than-expected retail rental in Hong Kong, and 2) higher booking and better margin for China development properties. We were expecting a 4% H/H decline in Hong Kong retail rentals, but Wharf delivered a 5% H/H improvement. We believe that was due to the sharp improvement in the tenant retail sales decline in 2Q16. Wharf increased its dividend by 5.5% to HK$0.58, mainly pegging it to the growth outlook of its rental income.

  • •    Retail sales outlook improving. The retail sales decline narrowed from -20% inlQ16 to -10% in 2Q16 in Times Square and -19% in IQ to -10% in 2Q in Haibor City. The company also noted that the June and July performance for some high-end brands was even better, with some back into positive territoiy. With the strengthening of the Yen, we believe this is likely to support the outlook in 2H16. However, occupancy costs in HC and TS reached 20% and 25% in 1H16 respectively which could potentially lead to negative rental reversion in 2017 unless retail sales can improve. In view of the recent trend, we believe there is a high chance of further improvement in 2H16.

We believe this will help to cushion some slight pressure in the Hong Kong rental income. Wharf is not eager to replenish its land bank in China, but with the capex on the China IFS soon to be running out, we expect the company’s leverage to lower sharply after 2017.
