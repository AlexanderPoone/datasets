SWOT Analysis

Strengths

Weaknesses

  • •    It is the largest pharmaceutical distributor in China

  • •    Owned by the largest state owned pharmaceutical group, CNPGC and Shanghai Fosun Pharmaceuticals, a major private drug maker. This enhances credibility and stature of Sinopharm and can lead to greater access to hospitals

  • •    The company has the largest supply chain network covering 31 provinces and 241 logistics centers. Also, it owns the largest number of retail drug stores in major cities in China

  • •    The country’s largest portfolio to meet demand from customers and widest network coverage to satisfy the need of manufacturers to grow business nationwide

  • •    The nature of the business results in very low net margins (1.66%in 2015)

  • •    Limited presence in other Asian or Global markets

  • •    Healthcare expenditure as a % of GDP is low as compared to other developed markets

  • •    A large chunk of revenue comes from hospitals (62%). So any reform in hospital policies can damage revenue

  • •    The company is dependent on a number of key suppliers, all of whom may opt to use smaller distributors if they offer better terms

Opportunities

Threats

  • •    E-commerce will pose as a new growth driver for traditional pharma firms with strong platforms and resources

  • •    Public hospital reform will lead to separation of treatment and drug sales, which in turn will bring enormous opportunities for drug retail business

  • •    China Sinopharm International Corporation will provide opportunities to expand the trade globally

  • •    Heightened interest in expanding drugstore business should lead to higher margins and cash flow in the longer term

  • •    Numerous M&A opportunities in distribution industry with many small distributors that may be available at reasonable valuations

  • •    Huge competition from Chinese pharmaceutical retailers as a number of these local players are now listed

  • •    Global established players entering the market will further increase pressure

  • •    Pharmaceutical sales is a highly regulated industry with government imposed caps on drug prices

  • •    Though sector growth is more than Chinese GDP growth rate, it has slowed in recent years

  • •    Implementation risk of moving from developed Tier I cities to smaller cities and towns is quite significant

  • •    Rising interest rates will drive up finance costs

Source: J.P. Morgan
