Prada S.P.A

The good and the bad

Prada reported Hl 15 results today post HK close. There was good and bad news in this release and conf call, in our view. The good: GM was significantly higher than expected; up 280bps yoy in Q2 leading to a 9% beat in Hl EBIT estimates. We would expect the stock to see a bounce on this: after underperforming the sector for two years, Prada now trades on 17.5x CY16e or a convergence towards the European sector average but on significantly more depressed margins than peers and these Hl numbers mark an inflection in GM. The bad: Prada stated that Aug. and beg. of Sept, trading has been ‘impacted’ by the recent stock market collapse, in particular HK and US. This is the most cautious we have heard so far on current trading, not to mention that, for Prada, compares are very easy in Q3.

  • •    The good: Gross Margins much higher than expected. GM in Q2 came in up 280bps yoy vs JPMe flat, and vs QI down. This was despite negative product (leather) and geographic (HK) mix. The key drivers of the stronger GM were forex and supply chain efficiencies at 50/50, we believe. We note that stronger GM has overall been a recurring theme in luxury reporting in Hl 15, helped by forex and stronger sales at full price, and despite a well flagged negative geo mix. This is surprisingly not yet putting to rest the bears’ argument that GM dynamics for the industry are over. In our view, the sector has enough challenges to face as is that it is neither needed nor fair to overlook one of its key positives.

  • •    The bad: Current trading appears to be particularly soft. Prada management stated that Aug. and beg of Sept, trading has been ‘impacted’ by the recent stock market collapse, flagging HK and US as deteriorating (we understand that EU and Japan remained strong). Note that Q3 faces significantly easier compares for Prada, cl000bps easier. A sequential deceleration on these compares would be no short of a shocker, in our view. Is there a readthrough for the sector? Other luxury goods companies when commenting on current trading appear to have been less dramatic. We think that Prada saw an accentuation of the impact as its product launches do not yet tease consumers’ appetite. Still, we think we ought to be prudent on Sept-Dec 15 trading. It can’t help a consumer sentiment that was already not that buoyant and Sept-Dec are months with lower tourists weight.

  • •    We leave our FY15E estimates unchanged with higher GM expectations compensated by lower Ifl, lower fx contribution and higher opex.
