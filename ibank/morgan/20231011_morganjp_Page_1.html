Bitcoin Mining: Expanding

Coverage

Initiating Coverage on CIFR, CLSK, RIOT and MARA

The bitcoin mining industry is at a crucible moment as management teams (and investors) weigh the prospects of a bitcoin ETF, which may catalyze a rally, against record hashrate increases and the looming block reward halving that threaten industry revenues and profitability. With that as a back drop, we think discipline and timing are paramount and favor operators that offer the best relative value in light of their existing hashrate, operational efficiency, power contracts, funded growth plans and liquidity. We are initiating coverage on CleanSpark (CLSK, Overweight), .Marathon Digital (MARA. Underweight). Riot Platforms (RIOT, Underweight) and Cipher Mining. (CIFR. Neutral). We also upgraded IREN this morning to Overweight (from Neutral), details found here.

  • •    -20% of network hashrate at risk from halving. More hashrate has been added over the past 9 months (-160 EH/s) than in bitcoin’s first 12 years of existence. Barring a doubling of bitcoin, we think the pace of hashrate grow th slows to -15 EH per quarter, which corresponds to -S200 - S300 million in quarterly global hardw are investment. We estimate as much as 80 EH/s (or -20% of the network hashrate) could be removed at the next halving (Apr ‘24) as less-efficient hardware is decomissioned.

  • •    Not all miners created equal. Miners vary by scale, operating efficiency, access to capital and growth prospects. We believe CLSK. our top pick, offers the best balance of scale, growth potential, power costs, and relative value. MARA is the largest operator but has the highest energy costs and lowest margins. RIOT has relatively low power costs and liquidity and is nearing completion of a large facility, but is by far the most expensive name in our coverage universe. CIFR has the lowest power costs but is growth constrained.
