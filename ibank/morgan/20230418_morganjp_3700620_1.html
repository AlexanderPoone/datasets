Asia Pacific Equity Derivatives Highlights

Long Japan equity volatility, Volatility positioning and dynamics

  • •    Japan equity volatility: an asymmetrical opportunity for long investors: The recent decline in Japan equity volatility presents an opportunity for long volatility investors. Our analysis suggests that there is an asymmetrical risk in favor of long volatility investors at this juncture. The post-pandemic shock liistory suggests that the Nikkei’s realized volatility has a minimum level of ~15v when the Nikkei spot trades above 29000. While we recognize the deltahedging flows of dealers’ long gamma positions is a major concern for going long volatility, our gamma positioning proxy indicates that dealers are no longer providing significant volatility dampening flows. With the moderation of concerns surrounding the tightening of global financial conditions and US banking stress, we anticipate domestic factors, including TSE reforms for sub-Ix P/B firms, wage and inflation dynamics and Bo J monetary policies, to have a more prominent role in driving share price and volatility movements in Japan’s equity market. Japan currently offers the most attractive equity risk premium among developed markets, and there are signs that foreign interest in Japan’s equity markets is evolving. If the Nikkei continues to gain momentum, there is a potential opportunity for long volatility investors to benefit from the increase in volatility resulting in foreign buying and derivatives hedging flows. For investors agreeing with the view, we recommend 1) buyNKY Dec23 100% upvar, 2) buyNKY vs SPX outperformance and dual-digital options, and 3) buy NKYDec24 120% call.

  • •    Structured prod net issuance trend in Korea: In March. Koreanautocallable issuance showed signs of recovery with total notional of US$2. Ibn of products issued during the month. The majority of issuance can be attributed to reinvestment demand following early redemptions of US$3.2 bn of products. Considering the redemption outlook and relative attractiveness of autocallables vs bonds, we think recovery in autocallable issuance will continue in the coming months but would likely be limited to below historical averages due to retail investors preference for cash equities.

  • •    Structured product issuance trend in Japan: The issuance of NKY-1 inked structured products continued to be restrained in March in the absence of revised policy guidelines for the sales of structured products in Japan. At the current spot, risk of hitting knock-out barriers appears relatively high, which could lead to early redemptions and hence reinvestment demand. The reinvestment demand could fuel some recovery in Japan structured product issuance; however the recovety would likely be limited until the new guidelines for the sale of structured products come into effect.
