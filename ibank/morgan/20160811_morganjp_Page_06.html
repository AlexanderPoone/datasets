China DP - No longer a drag

Strong sales should relieve concerns about China DP operation

China development revenue increased by 58% YoY and its contracted sales of Rmbl6.3bn reached 68% of its full year target at June 2016. The reported margin of the China DP was 16% in 1H16, showing an improvement from 10% in 2H15. That said, we expect a similar margin trend as in FYI5 for full-year 2016, while we may see some improvement in FYI7 with the strong ASP growth so far this year.

Impairment provision recognized in 1H16

Despite better sales and margin improvement, Wharf made provisions of HK$496mn for China DP projects in 1H16. This is the second time in three years Wharf has made provisions for this division. According to management, this is mainly in relation to the car parks in its residential projects in China which are in an oversupply situation.

While the better contracted sales should have mitigated concerns about its China DP business, this would not become a key share price driver for Wharf, in our view.

With the lessons learned from the aggression expansion in China, we believe Wharf will stay prudent in land banking in China by focusing on fewer target cities.
