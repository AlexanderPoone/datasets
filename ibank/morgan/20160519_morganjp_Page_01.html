CHINA CONNECT.


We initiate coverage on IMAX China with an OW rating and a Dec-17 PT of HK$52. The company is the exclusive IMAX theatre system provider to film exhibitors, licensee of the IMAX brand, and the sole commercial platform for the release of IMAX format films in Greater China. It is a direct beneficiary of the fast-growing box office and aggressive theatre rollout in China, by taking an asset-light model and without any exposure to the rising content production costs. We believe it offers more attractive valuations than film-related A share companies and deserves a scarcity value for being one of the few investable HK-listed China box office plays.

  • •    Investment positives: 1) Theatre companies’ aggressive expansion, strong consumer demand for high-quality and immersive watching experience, and the premium brand name of IMAX bode well for continued robust orders for IMAX screens, i.e., 109 installations on average per year for 2016E-2018E; 2) Expanding IMAX theatre network and favorable market reception to IMAX format films should drive IMAX box office to grow at a CAGR of 31% for 2016E-2018E in PRC; and 3) We expect gross margin to further expand from 65% in 2015 to 74% in 2018E on the back of the high scalability of its film business and revenue shift mix toward box office sharing in its theatre business.

  • •    Investment risks: 1) Dolby Cinema’s entry into China may reduce the demand for IMAX screens; 2) Heavy reliance on the partnership with Wanda presents potential risk to growth if Wanda decelerates rollout or manages to get more favorable revenue-sharing terms; 3) Currency risk; and 4) PE shareholders may sell down stakes upon lock-up period expiry in Jul-16.

  • •    Initiate with OW and PT of HKS52: Our DCF-based Dec-17 PT of HK$52 presents 22% potential share price upside. It implies a 2017E P/E of 29x, compared to IMAX parentco of 23x and the global average of 2 lx. We think the valuation premium is justified by: 1) IMAX China’s faster growth at a CAGR of 38% for 2015-2017E, vs 14% of IMAX US and global average of 21%; and 2) the scarcity value for being a China box office play listed in HK.
