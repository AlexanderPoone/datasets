VTech Holdings

New PT of HK$105 - First half was a most challenging period

VTech Holdings (VTech) is one of the world’s largest producers of cordless phones and electronic learning products. Vtech reported 1HFY17 sales growth of 6% Y/Y and a net profit decline of 29%. Excluding acquisitions, sales were down 1% and net profits down 3%. Losses related to recently acquired Leapfrog were higher than our expectations, but most of the difficult challenges related to the integration of this acquisition were completed in 1HFY17. We note that consensus estimates have come down by 19% over the past six months and have factored in many of the challenges in the first half. We set a new Dec-17 PT of HK$105.

  • •    Most challenging first half. 1HFY17 sales were 7% lower than our estimate (up 6% Y/Y or down 1% Y/Y excluding LF). The disappointment was mostly related to a 12% decrease Y/Y in sales of telephone products (to US$278m). GPM for 1HFY17 improved by 1.1% to 31.9%; this was more or less in line with our expectations, however, the loss at Leapfrog (approx. $25m) is higher than our expectations. On the positive side, management did mention most of the expenses related to restructuring were completed in 1HFY17.

  • •    Lowering estimates and setting new PT of HKS105. We have lowered our sales expectations for FYI7 and FY18 by 4% to factor in the weakness we have seen in telephone products in 1HFY17 (although we do factor in some improvement in 2HFY17 telephony sales down 4% YY instead of down 12% Y/Y in 1HFY17). We have left our DLP sales roughly unchanged. Overall, we have lowered our FY17 net profit by 21.9% and our new FY17 EPS estimate is 5% higher than consensus. We set a new Dec-17 DCF-based PT of HK$105 (previously Dec-16 PT of HK$94); the key reason for the higher PT is the rollover of the PT timeframe.
