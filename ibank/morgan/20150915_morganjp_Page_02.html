H1 15 Results in focus

Hl 15 preliminary net revenues had already been released in August, +4% reported to 61,824m, implying Q2 net revenues +2% to €996m. New today was more precise detail on organic sales progression; Hl Sales fell 6% in local currencies, implying Q2 -6% ex-FX.


HI retail sales were +8% reported, -3% organic to €1,552m, implying Q2 retail sales +8% reported, -2% organic to €803m. We calculate LFL in Q2 of -5%, better than that of Q1 (-9%) albeit on easier compares.

HI 15 wholesale sales were down 14% reported, -20% ex-FX, to €248m, implying Q2 -17% reported, -23% organic, to €182m.

European retail sales again posted double-digit growth +12% organic, sustained by tourist flows and local consumption.

Retail sales in the US were muted -4% organic, with management commenting on the strong USD reducing tourism flows as well as shifting local consumption elsewhere (likely Europe.) Asia Pac’s trend in Q2 was in line with that of Q1, down 18% ex-FX as Hong Kong and Macau continue to drag sales.

Japan accelerated helped by easy compares +13% ex-FX in Q2 and boosted by Chinese tourists.

GM sole handedly drives 9% EBIT beat

Q2 GM expanded 280bps yoy to 72.8% (JPMe: 70.0%), driven by forex and supply chain efficiencies (better planning, higher production efficiencies, better sales at full price). We understand that both contributed 50/50 to the GM expansion in Hl with a likely over-proportional impact of forex in Q2 (hedging gains in Q2 14 must have been lower than we had anticipated). These positive effects markedly offset both negative product (Leather Goods -6% ex-FX in Q2) and geographical (weak HK) mix. We would point that some of the supply chain efficiencies may be encompassed in inventories (+48% yoy) and might hence not fully recur moving forward. Still, Prada GM had and will likely have even more support than we had initially factored in.
