China Property

Sales in May were disappointing

Thanks to a low base last year, top 100 developers’ aggregate sales in May still managed to see a positive 3% Y/Y growth (fig. 1). However, if compared to the 4-year average, sales were down 45% (worse than -27% in April). In other words, the strong sales in February and March were very short-lived, and the sales level is now back to where we were in 2H22 when sales were typically 40-50% below 4-year average. As the base will get higher in June (pent-up demand from lockdowns was released in June 2022), we believe the Y/Y growth will turn negative again. Against this backdrop (together with weak macro data like PMI), we reiterate our view that the sector will stay weak and underperform MSCI China until signs of sales recovery or major easing. To buy on dips, we like SOEs as, although their sales are also slowing down, their sales in May are still roughly similar to the 4-year average with market share gains. We prefer CR Land. COLL Poly PS. COPH & CR Mixc. We stay cautious on all distressed names (e.g. Sunac China, Sunac Services, Shi mao Services).

5M23 developers’ contracted sales (CRIC)

  • •    Developers’ sales weakened in May: Despite a low base effect due to lockdowns in May 2022, the marginal 3% y/y improvement in May is much weaker than the 31% and 32% y/y growth in March and April, respectively. Compared to the 4-year average, sales were further down by 45% (similar to 2H22). For June, while we expect some M/M growth as developers usually push for more launches in June (June is in fact the month with the highest sales contribution - Table 2). we believe the Y/Y growth will turn negative in June as the base is higher (pent-up demand from lockdowns was released back then).

  • •    Introducing seasonality-adjusted (s.a.) Y/Y data (fig. 2): Sales data in 2022 was severely disrupted by lockdowns. Therefore, during lockdowns (e.g. April & May), the Y/Y base would be particularly low, while for the short period after lockdowns (e.g. June), the base would be unusually high due to the release of pent-up demand. To smooth out the impact, we adjust the monthly base in 2022 by multiplying the average monthly sales contribution in 2016-2021 (Table 2) with the full-year sales in 2022, as an alternative way to gauge the sales trend. Using the seasonality-adjusted figures, the top 100 developers’ sales were down by 14% in May, which is worse than what the non-seasonality adjusted y/y growth suggests.

  • •    SOEs continued to lead: SOEs saw an average 30% y/y growth in May (nondistressed POEs -23% y/y; distressed names -42% y/y). Compared to the 4-year average, SOEs’ sales still managed to hold up with a +2% growth (nondistressed POEs -51%; distressed POEs -75% - fig. 5). Only SOEs and defensive names saw a positive y/y growth in May (fig. 8). with China Merchants Shekou (+67%) seeing the strongest sales growth, followed by C&D (60%), Jinmao (58%), CR Land (55%), Yuexiu (49%), Greentown (23%), COLI (11%), Longfor (9%).
