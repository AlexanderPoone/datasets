Weekly Monitor: More Supportive Policy Coming?

Sentiment improves given greater expectations on stimulus policies.

Metal: Shanghai copper prices were up 0.5% WoW and inventories up 15.6% WoW. Shanghai aluminum prices were flat WoW while inventories were down 4.5% WoW. Key news of the week: Yunnan's power supply was effective and strong in October, with thermal power generation up 38.8% YoY, and hydropower up 12.7% YoY.

Gold: Price was up 0.6% WoW to US$1,993/oz. Key news of the week: Shandong Gold plans to increase its stake in Yintai Gold by Rmb1.288bn - Rmb2.576bn, at prices not higher than Rmb19.04/share.

Steel: Shanghai HRC was flat WoW and CRC prices were flat WoW. Shanghai rebar was up 1.9% WoW while Tangshan billet prices were flat WoW. Long steel inventories at traders were down 3.0% WoW and flat steel was down 2.6% WoW. Key news of the week: Bloomberg reported that China is drafting a list of 50 property firms eligible for funding.

Cement: Prices were down 1.1% WoW to Rmb367/t as of November 24. Key news of the week: China Resource Cement's 3mntpa aggregate project completed construction and commissioned.

Coal: Prices (5,5OOkcal) were down 0.4% WoW to Rmb753/t as of November 24. Qinhuangdao inventory was flat WoW at 6.33mnt. BSPI was down 0.1% WoW at Rmb733/t. Key news of the week: Most coal mines in Lishi District, Lvliang City resumed production during the afternoon of November 17.

Paper: Linerboard prices were flat WoW at Rmb3823/t and corrugated medium prices were up 0.1% WoW at Rmb3229/t as of November 22. Domestic OCC prices were flat WoW at Rmb1734/t. Corrugated paper inventory at 38 sample producers came to 359.0kt, -3.8% WoW as of November 23. Linerboard paper inventory was 947.2kt. -2.4% WoW.
