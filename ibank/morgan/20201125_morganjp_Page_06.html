What has happened when the CBRT hikes?

We are in Stage 4 of the cycle: 1) low TRY rates —> 2) TRY/MSCI Turkey weakness —* 3) CBRT hikes enough —> 4) TRY/MSCI Turkey rebound. Each cycle typically takes 18-36 months. We have seen similar rebounds in 2014,2016 and 2018 where the rebounds in MSCI Turkey in USD terms were 48%, 53% and 46% trough-to-peak, respectively

Positioning is light, supportive for the rally

Turkey has experienced a significant shift in stock ownership, with international participation in the free float declining to an unprecedented low of 49% vs 60-65% seen two years ago and above 70% in 2007.

We identify 3 pillars underpinning a reversal in fund flows:

  • 1)    Supportive rule-based market policies centered on free-market practices, the positive effects of which are already being felt;

  • 2)    Growth remaining firm, shifting from domestic consumption to exports. JPM expects 2021 GDP growth at 3.6% after a small decline of 0.6% in 2020, while the C/A deficit is likely to narrow from an expected 4.2% in 2020 to 1.2% of GDP in 2021. More prudent policies along with weaker domestic demand, increased price competitiveness caused by the lira weakening sharply and an expected partial recovery in tourism are likely lead to a significant improvement in Turkey’s external balances. Further, a more meaningful recovery in tourism could even result in a current account surplus.
