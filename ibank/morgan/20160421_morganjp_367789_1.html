Value Partners Group Limited

Looking to resurface


▼ Neutral

Previous: Overweight 0806.HK, 80 6 HK Price: HK$7.95


CHINA 'connect'


▼ Price Target: HKS8.20

Previous: HK$9.20


We assume coverage with a Neutral rating and Dec-16 PT of HKS8.2. We remain constructive about the L-T growth outlook at VPG but think the share price is fairly valued at the current level after a rebound from a recent trough, as upside potential for operating performance in the coming 12 months is limited.

  • •    Beating high watermark could be challenging in FY16. We expect lower net fiinds inflow and thus slower AUM growth in FY16E, compared to a CAGR of 22% in the past 3 years, resulting in a slowdown of management fee growth. In addition, operating profits are more leveraged to performance fees, but the 50% of AUMs entitled to performance fees is currently more than 10% below the high watermark based on our calculation. This limits upside for performance fees in FY16E in our view, and a YoY decline in 1H16E could be meaningful given the high base in 1H15.

  • •    Potential earnings drag at Vision Credit. Earnings contribution plummeted more than 60% YoY in FY 15 and the operation suffered losses in 2H15, largely due to almost tripled provision expenses as the impaired loan ratio rose significantly to near 19% in 2H15; we anticipate another 30% profit contraction YoY in FYI 6 and would not be surprised if operations turn to red for the whole year if asset quality deterioration continues; the operation is too small to trigger capital adequacy issues at group level but could still be a meaningful earnings overhang in a worst case scenario.

  • •    Long-term growth prospects remain blight. Looking beyond the recent tighter capital controls and currency intervention, we believe HK remains the major beneficiary of China’s capital account deregulation while VPG’s strong brand image and the development of QDLP/MRF schemes should facilitate better AUM growth in the mid-to-long term, in addition to its continued expansion of product distribution outside the greater China region.

  • •    Awaiting better timing or entry points. We see limited valuation upside after a +30% share price rebound from the recent trough. We think material consensus earnings revisions - an important share price driver - will be limited over the coming 12 months. We suggest investors wait for a better timing, when flagship fiinds are less than 5% below the high watermark, or more attractive valuation, if share price pulls back to HK$7 or below (equivalent to -1 std of L-T P/AUM).
