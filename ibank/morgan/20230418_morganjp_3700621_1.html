China Ecommerce

March 2023 online sales: Better than expected recovery; Divergence among categories continues

  • •    According to the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS), China online retail sales in 1Q23 increased 8.6% YoY to RMB 3,286b, vs. 4% in 4Q22. On a monthly basis, online retail sales in March grew 13%, vs. 6% YoY in 2M23, confirming a continuous recovery which is better than what many investors feared, though the acceleration can be partially explained by the low base last March (when lockdown measures started in Shanghai). Online physical goods GMV in 1Q23 grew 7.3% YoY to RMB 2,784b, slightly* better than 6.4%/7.1% in 4Q22 3Q22. On a monthly basis, online physical goods GMV7 increased 11% YoY in March vs. 5% in 2M23. Overall retail sales (online and offline) grew 5.8% YoY in 1Q23 and 10.6% YoY in March alone. Online physical goods transaction value in 1Q23 contributed 24.2% of total retail sales, up Ippt YoY/1.5ppt vs 2M23.

  • •    We think ecommerce stocks may see upside for their share prices if the online retail sales can show more meaningfully recovery into 2Q23, against the low expectation by investors now. We expect online retail sales growth to improve more meaningfully in AprilMay against the very low base last April May when Shanghai was under a hill lockdown, and momentum may continue as ecommerce platforms/merchants are prepared for more aggressive promotion into 6/18 event this year. Our sector’s pecking order remains Alibaba > PDD > JD, given the divergence of sales by category’ (apparel strong vs. elect ionics/ho me appliance weak), and continuing preference for value-for-money products by consumers amid the lukewarm economic outlook.
