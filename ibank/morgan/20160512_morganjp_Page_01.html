Chow Tai Fook Jewellery Company Ltd.

Profit warning following weak 4Q sales print

Chow Tai Fook issued a profit warning saying its FYI6 (year end in March 2016) earnings may fall 40-50% y/y vs JPM estimate of 33% decline. The announcement notes the decline can be attributable to 1) the worsening SSS in China (down cl5% in 2HFY16 vs flat in 1HFY16), 2) reduced GPM due to a combination of rising exposure to gold products (55% in FYI6 vs 53% in FYI5) and the unrealized hedging loss in FYI6 vs unrealized gains in FYI5 which helping GPM expand by 50bps. If we exclude the impact of hedging and FX for both FYI5 and FYI6, the recurring earnings decline in FYI6 is expected to be 20-30%. This print is largely in line with 1H16 results (42% headline earnings decline; 22% recurring earnings decline) indicating 2H16 remained subdued, which is not a surprise to the market after the company recorded a weak 4QFY16 operational update on 12 April. We cut our FYI6 earnings by 14% to build in its weak sales trend in 4Q as well as the FX loss, and revise the price target to HK$3.5. We do not see any sustainable pick-up in the near term especially after CTF posted weak China sales for 4QFY16. We are now incrementally worried about China business. Maintain UW.

  • •    We expect FY17 sales outlook to remain weak. We are forecasting CTF to post flat sales in FY17 with flat SSS in HK (this is partly helped by a lower base of tourist arrivals given the one-trip-per-week policy was introduced in Apr-15) and 2% SSSG in China. We expect flat adjusted GPM and OPM in FYI7 given higher-priced items are difficult to push, and recurring earnings to decline slightly at LSD.

  • •    China becomes a swing factor to CTF. CTF’s share price has been weak over the past two years (fell 53% vs HSI down 10%) largely because of weak HK while China is relatively resilient. A sudden deterioration in China’s business in 4Q may fuel the market concern if it would further pressure weak HK business. We don’t see any positive catalysts in the near term.
