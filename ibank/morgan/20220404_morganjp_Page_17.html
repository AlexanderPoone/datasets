We also fine-tune our imports projection for the year. We now expect energy imports to reach almost US$12bn (US$4.6bn above our previous forecast) due to higher energy prices, with the energy deficit increasing to near $6bn, from $0.3bn in 2021. That said, we flag that risks would be skewed to the downside if LNG supply is disrupted. We also expect the government to limit growth of non-energy imports so as to comply with the IMF reserves accumulation target for the year at the expense of lower growth and higher inflation. In all, we see 2022 imports at US$65bn, with the trade balance at US$18.7 (FOB-FOB), in line with the 2021 outcome.

If the terms of trade impulse turns negative, the probability of technical recession would increase above 50%. For now, we maintain our 3.0%y/y 2022 GDP growth projection (0.6%4Q/4Q), though we see downside risk.
