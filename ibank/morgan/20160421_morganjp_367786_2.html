Findings from recent visits to other construction related companies:

Construction activity may be heating up. Our recent visit to a construction site by China State Construction (3311 HK -OW) in the city of Jiaxing in Eastern China revealed that there has been a significant increase in the cost of some construction materials since 4Q15 as construction activity heated up after the CNY holiday. In addition, wage growth for construction is also higher than 10 percent Y Y and in some cases much higher. This indicates a still tight market for certain types of labour. It is encouraging to see a faster pace of both private and public construction in the Yangtze delta region. We heard from CSC management that construction is improving in the area around Jiaxing; this is consistent with some favourable figures on new home construction released by the government (see below). General pick-up in sentiment from factories. Our recent visit to Haitian Inti, the largest plastic injection molding machinery maker globally, suggests a widespread recovery in demand that is causing many plastic parts makers to order new machinery. Incidentally, Lesso is one of the largest customers of Haitian. Haitian is seeing a more robust order flow after the CNY holidays and is running near capacity now to deliver machinery from strong March order flow. The company cited a general improvement in end user demand for plastic pails as well as government policies (such as tax rebates and subsidies) that encourage factories to update their equipment.

Price, valuation and risks. Our PT (Dec-16. DCF-derived) of HK$9.0 implies a fwd P/BV of 1.69x and P/E of 8.46x (CY17E). The key downside risks include lower non-PVC prices, higher wages and lower margins on non-PVC pipes and non-pipe products, significant increase in R&D costs and a sharp fall in housing construction starts.
