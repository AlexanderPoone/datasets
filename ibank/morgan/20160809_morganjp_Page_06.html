Investment Thesis, Valuation and Risks

SJM Holdings (Underweight; Price Target: HK$4.60)

Investment Thesis

Despite its continued underperformance over the past couple years, SJM is still our least preferred stock, as we cannot see a good reason that would make it outperform its peers over the next 12-18 months. The stock may rebound technically as a ‘laggard play’ if and when sector sentiment meaningfully improves; this, however, is not our base case.

We are UW on SJM for the following reasons. First, the pace of its market share decline is accelerating amid the supply of new casinos. Second, its EBITDA is falling more steeply than that of peers, and we think its ‘EBITDA share’ will continue to hit record lows. Third, its balance sheet is rapidly deteriorating from the major Cotai capex, and we forecast that the company will turn into a net debt position in 2017E, for the first time in its history.

Lastly, the stock is not even cheap anymore. The stock’s valuation merit - which we saw as the only virtue of this stock - is fading very fast, from the double impact of rising leverage and falling EBITDA. Of note, SJM trades at 1 Ix/lOx EV/EBITDA for FY17E/18E, which is more expensive than Galaxy or MPEL.
