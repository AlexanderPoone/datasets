ESG - Environmental, Social & Governance Investing

Creating an ‘Innovation Culture Score’, using Human Capital Factor data.

In this note, we create an "Innovation Culture Score' by exploring the Social pillar of ESG, and utilise inputs from within the ‘Human Capital Factor’ data. We analyse and explore this unique data-set which captures employee intentions and behaviour, rather than simple disclosures. The results suggest a focus on Innovation & Culture has a strong ability to consistently outperform the market, match the profile of thematic definitions of innovation but without the historical drawdowns & generally centre sector tilts away from a dominance on technology.

  • •    The "Innovation Culture Score’ - What is it?

The Innovation Culture Score (ICS) is a metric closely related to the Human Capital Factor (HCF), but is tilted to capture the portion of Human Capital that is related to innovation. In essence, the aim of the score is to quantify how well an organization fosters a culture of innovation as well as providing its employees with the encouragement and support to pursue new projects and ideas beyond their typical remits.

  • •    "Innovation Culture Score’ - Results.

The Innovation Culture Score (ICS) is built using US private data sources, which utilize HR employee survey responses from corporates. The back-test for the long ICS has yielded an excess return of +3.6%, with a t-statof 1.7. The risk adjusted performance, given by the Shaipe Ratio, comes in at 0.6. The Long-Short performance returns come in at +5.3%, the Shaipe Ratio is 0.7 and the maximum drawdown of 19.1%.

  • •    Innovation Culture Score - Characteristics.

In contrast to the HCF, the ICS largest sector tilt is towards financials and companies with a high ICS tend to have far more moderate valuations than the overall HCF, which has historically been tilted towards the technology sector. Similarly, many innovation stocks defined by thematics are also heavily tilted towards Technology stocks (ARKK) and can be volatile and suffer large drawdowns. Our analysis suggests that the Innovation Culture score is better at distributing sector tilts across all sectors, yet has offered returns similar too many popular innovation stock indices.
