L’Occitane International SA

Longer Term Top Down Story Attractive, Share Price Catalysts Limited in Short Term

We assume coverage of L’Occitane with Neutral recommendation and HK$ 18.40 Mar-15 PT. Top down drivers are attractive. However, we are cautious about short-term sales growth and longer-tenn EBIT margin upside.

Top down drivers attractive. Worldwide cosmetics sales have been both resilient and strong, with growth averaging 4.3% over the past -20 years. There are a number of drivers of future growth including the emerging middle class and the aging population. L’Oreal estimates the worldwide cosmetics market could double by 2025. Within the broader market, the natural personal care market has taken market share, growing double digits over recent years.

EBIT margin upside likely to be capped. L’Occitane EBIT margins have contracted over recent years with margins expected to hit lows for FY14. Nevertheless, any upside in EBIT margins will likely be capped for a number of reasons: (1) Competition is rising, implying need for further reinvestment, (2) L’Occitane does face significant brand concentration risk and diversification will likely negatively impact margins; (3) High number of store refurbishments planned for short term; (4) E-commerce growth likely requiring upfront investment. Although global industiy leaders are currently reporting mid-teens EBIT margins and L’Occitane is reporting low teens, there is significant divergence in margins amongst cosmetics companies, with smaller vertical organic cosmetics players reporting mid-to-high single digit EBIT margins.

Short-term catalysts relatively limited. With EBIT margin upside likely to be capped, we believe any share price catalysts will rely on sales growth. In the short term, we expect sales growth to be relatively muted due to: (1) Unfavourable FX movements; (2) Increase in Japanese consumption tax in April 2014; (3) Significant number of refurbishments planned for FYI5; (4) Continued patchy trading in Mainland China and HK. Although we expect offsetting factors, we believe upcoming sales releases will be mixed at best.

Retain Neutral. Our FYI5 forecasts are -14% below consensus, implying P/E of 22.5x. The share price has increased -24% over the past month on improving 3Q14 sales. Given the lack of short term earnings catalysts and valuation in line with peers, we believe near term share price outperformance will be unlikely.
