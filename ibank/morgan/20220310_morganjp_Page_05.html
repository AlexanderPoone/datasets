Europe in Focus

The European Commission released a plan to reduce the EU’s reliance on imports of natural gas from Russia (the EU imports ~40% of its natural gas from Russia). The communication builds on the existing Fit for 55 decarbonization plan, seeking to increase the resilience of the EU-wide energy system based on two pillars: 1) Diversifying gas supplies, via higher LNG imports and pipeline imports from non-Russian suppliers, and higher levels of biomethane and hydrogen. 2) Reducing dependence on fossil fuels through energy efficiency, mcreasing the share of renewables and addressing infrastructure bottlenecks. The Commission anticipates developing a formal “REPowerEU” plan, in cooperation with Member States, by the summer. We believe most companies under coverage should benefit from accelerated demand over the medium-term, and view rooftop solar companies as the best near-term beneficiaries owing to shorter project cycles (SEDG and ENPH).

Accelerated deployments of renewables. The Commission calls for a “frontloading” of the EU’s Fit for 55 plan, which targets 480 GW of wind and 420 GW of solar by 2030. This frontloading could increase deployment rates by ~20%. The plan also calls for 80 GW of renewables by 2030 to accommodate higher production of green hydrogen.

European Solar Rooftops Initiative. The Commission calls for a plan that reduces barriers (e.g. permitting) and provides incentives to generate an incremental 15 TWh of energy within one year from rooftop solar (JPMe: 7-8 GW). The Commission will publish a recommendation on rapid permitting for renewable energy projects in May.

Green hydrogen accelerator. The Commission calls for a frontloading of the Innovation Fund to enhance the EU’s domestic manufacturing of hydrogen electrolyzers, next-gen solar and wind, and other technologies.
