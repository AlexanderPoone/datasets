  • 1.    New Year’s Day holiday tourism

China had the three-day New Year’s holiday from December 30, 2023 - January 1, 2024.

  • •    Domestic passenger traffic: The Ministry of Transport estimated that daily average domestic passenger flow during the holiday reached 42.7mn person-time, rising 78.4%oya on a low base. In growth terms, passenger flow via railway and waterway outperformed, rising 177.5% and 72.9% compared to the 202 3 New Year’s Day holiday period.

  • •    Cross-border passenger traffic: The National Immigration Administration reported that 5.2 million entry and exit trips were made over the three-day holiday, 4.7 times that of the 2023 New Year’s Day holiday, largely back to the 2019 level.

  • •    Tourism spending: The number of tourists reached 135mnperson-time(vs. 52.7mn for the 2023 New Year’s Day holiday, or 826mn for the 2023 National Day holiday), 109.4% of the 2 019 level. Tourism revenue recorded 79.7bn yuan (vs. 26.5bn yuan for the 202 3 New Year’s Day holiday or 753.4bn yuan for the 2023 National Day holiday), 105.6% of the 2019 level. Tourism expenditure per capita ticked lower to 97% of the 2019 level on a comparable basis (vs. 98% for the 2023 National Day holiday).

  • •    According Maoyam China’s box office revenue generated during the three-day holiday reached 1.5 billion yuan, higher than recent years.

  • •    Home sales: Beijing led the new home sales recovery after the latest policy efforts to lower mortgage rates and downpayment ratios, but other cities remained generally soft. According to CREIS, 40 major cities’ new home sales fell 26% during the three-day New Year’s Day holiday, with a 3% modest fall in Tier 1 cities supported by the 26% growth in Beijing, a 17% drop in Tier 2 cities and a 50% decline in Tier 3/4 cities.
