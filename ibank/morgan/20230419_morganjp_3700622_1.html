Not much excitement from the 1Q23 real estate data

We think the 1Q23 real estate data from NBS may not yet drive up investors’ expectations. Residential sales value in March continued to see positive growth on both a Y/Y basis (+11%) and a 4-year average basis (+3%), but the trend is similar to that of top 100 developers’ sales published earlier (fig. 3). New starts were still disappointing with the Y/Y decline widening to 27% (2M23: -9%), while completions remained the outperformer with a +32% Y/Y growth (2M23: +8%). Among the three major metrics, we believe completions will see the strongest growth, followed by sales and lastly new starts. For 60-city weekly sales, while there was still a positive Y/Y growth (+29%), we think this is not meaningfill as the base is low' (due to lockdowms last year). If compared to the 4-year average, the decline w'ould be 32%, which is slightly w'orse than the wreek before (-28%). Last wreek, the sector mildly rose 1% (driven by SOEs only), in line with HSCEI (+1%). In the near term, as wre do not expect a meaningful pickup in sales (on a 4-year average basis) or strong easing (as sales/home price are also not substantially deteriorating), wre reiterate our view' that the sector may stay range-bound in 2Q23. Top picks: COLI / CR Land / Poly PS / COPH / CR Mixc. For pair trade, wre continue to recommend long COLI & short Vanke-H (link) on grow'th differential (month-to-date: COLI +13% vs. Vanke-H +5%).
