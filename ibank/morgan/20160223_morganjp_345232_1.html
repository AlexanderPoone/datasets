HSBC Holdings plc

Tougher operating environment and higher impairment costs driving lower EPS

Following HSBC’s 4Q15 results, which were below company consensus PBT by 46%, we cut our clean PBT estimates by 6-7% in 2016-ISE. The key drivers of 4Q weakness were similar to those experienced by global peers: weak revenue (8% below adj. cons) and higher provisions ($1.6bn/70bps vs $1.0bn cons) partly driven by Oil & Gas. Lower RWAs helped CT1 improve to 11.9% and final DPS to 21c (both in line with consensus). HSBC is trading at 13.4x/10.8x 2017/18E P/E and 0.7x ’ 17E P/B for 5.5% RoE in’ 17E, underperforming the HSI by 940bps. Our revised Dec-16 PT is HK$50 (was HK$55); we retain our Neutral rating.

  • •    Q415 results: Clean PBT of $1.9bn was 46% below company cons and 39% below JPMe. Relative to expectations: i) Revenues of $13bn were 8% below cons and 2% below JPMe; ii) Costs of $ lO.Obn were 1% below cons and JPMe; and hi) impairments of $1.6bn (70bps) were significantly above cons of $1 .Obn and JPMe of $0.7bn. TNAV was $7.48 down 3% q/q.

  • •    Tough industry revenue environment weighs: Europe and North America were the drivers of weakness in Q4 revenue relative to JPMe (both 3% below). After strong growth in HT15 (+4.5%), group adjusted revenue fell in H2’15 (-2.7%), and we assume that revenue pressures likely persist. Management indicated a mixed start to 2016, with pressures in Global Markets and Asia WM, but strength in CMB and resilient Global banking and RBWM. We now forecast group core revenues (ex Brazil, CML and legacy credit) to be -0.5% ex FX in FYI6 (-4.5% in USD terms).

  • •    Impairment rise partly driven by Oil & Gas: Q4’15 impairments of $1.6bn (70bps) included $0.4bn provisions on Oil & Gas, specific impairment charges across a number of countries and sectors in wholesale, higher provisions in Brazil retail (exited), and lower releases from the US CML book. We now forecast FY16 impairments of 39bps, FY17 impairments of 50bps and FY18 impairments of 56bps; still below the long-term average (20yr) of 104bps.

  • •    Asia business snap shot: Asia contributed 84% of FYI 5 PBT. 1H’15 revenue +8% y/y, then fell 5% y/y in 2H’ 15. Strong 1H’ 15 revenue was partly driven by strong WM sales in HK (ie. China market rally), which is difficult to repeat in 2016. We expect growth deceleration and continued asset quality deterioration in Asia, particularly Greater China and Indonesia.

  • •    Changes to estimates: We cut our FY16-18E clean PBT estimates by 6-7%, mainly due to lower revenues and higher provisions. As a result of our PBT cuts, our PT falls by 9% to HK$50.
