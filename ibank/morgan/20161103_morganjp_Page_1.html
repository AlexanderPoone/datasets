Limited earnings recovery visibility

Despite a slight market recovery in China, we remain cautious on SSC due to its structural risk to Sinopec’s upstream business and less impressive cost management track record, relative to peers. We expect continuous heavy losses in the foreseeable future. We maintain our UW rating and PT.

  • •    Exposure to Sinopec a structural risk. SSC’s exposure to Sinopec is a key weakness on a relative basis because: (1) Sinopec’s upstream all-in costs for oil production is c.20% higher than peers’, so it’s least likely to increase E&P capex unless oil prices see a meaningful recovery; and (2) Sinopec has a strong refining/chemicals business, so when expectations are low of a rapid oil price rebound, it tends to allocate more resources to downstream, in our view. This has been reflected in Sinopec’s most aggressive capex reductions in the upstream business YTD, at 50% yoy versus peers’ 20-30% reductions.

  • •    Loose cost management and inflated gearing. Compared with peers (like China Oilfield), we view the company’s cost cutting as disappointing. Its 3Q16 COGS fell only 3% yoy (up 4% qoq) and SG&A expanded 28% yoy (up 2% qoq). Total costs remained unchanged yoy and rose 4% qoq. The impact of one-off items like asset impairments was limited, at Rmb89mn, or 1% of total costs. This reflects limited cost-control efforts and sets up more risk for the future, in our view. On the balance sheet, net gearing soared to 100% at end-3Q16 versus 50-60% in 2013-2015. We forecast continuous heavy losses in 2017-2018E, with net margin at negative 10-20%. Its net margin collapsed to -48%, as revenue was down 32% yoy, while costs stayed flat.
