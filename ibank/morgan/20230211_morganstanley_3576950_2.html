A Headline to Drive Markets, Policy?

The January US employment report has led investors to reassess the peak rate for Fed policy and the path of policy thereafter. It also introduced catch phrases like "no Landing" into the investor debate. And finally, it has heightened tensions ahead of the January US CPI report.

Our economists forecast core CPI increasing 0.43% M/M, which would round down to the Bloomberg consensus at 0.4%. Updated seasonal factors caused them to revise higher the original 0.38% M/M forecast, and also made the deceleration in 4Q22 Look less dramatic (see Exhibit 1).

Importantly, they added, "While the CPI core services ex-ho using aggregate was revised up based on the new seasonals, note that the effects on the same aggregate for the PCE index should be smaller given that it sources air travel and medical inflation differently than the CPI."
