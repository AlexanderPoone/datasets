Global Technical Analysis Research 02 June 2023


US

A steady trend of mostly better than expected economic data as well as the debt ceiling resolution have kept Treasuries under pressure, led to an S&P 500 Index breakout from the multi-month trading range, and put our medium-term outlooks under considerable pressure. Time will tell whether the recent price action is a false hope late in an economic cycle or a more lasting dynamic that completely upends our favored outlooks into the second half of the year. For now, we need to respect the US front end’s late-May release through Mar-May range support and more recent rejection of that area as new resistance. Furthermore, the US equity market saw a transition in leadership as the S&P 5 00 Index pushed through the 4200 area with cyclically sensitive sectors driving the charge, and industrial metals as well as correlated FX pairs staged short-term bullish trend reversals. While we think these moves will likely whipsaw, we can’t have high conviction until we see high frequency price action that points in that direction.

The post-payrolls 2-year note backup paused the rebound from the 4.655% May 26 yield high and importantly kept the market from retaking a key chart area. The 4.26-4.30% yield highs defined the cheap end of the Mar-May trading range prior to the recent breakdown (Figure 1). Furthermore, the 50-day, 100-day, and 200-day moving averages are all clustered at 4.09-4.25%. The late-May break and this week’s rejection of that key area puts a material dent in our outlook, which had favored a release to lower yields into the summer. For now, we are left watching for a yield top pattern to form near support that includes the 4.59% Mar 13 bull gap and 4.76% Mar 78.6% retrace. Despite the near-term setback, the 4Q22/1H23 price action can still prove to be a bullish reversal pattern if that short-term pattern develops in the days weeks ahead. A sustaiend move through 4.25% would start to put our view back on solid ground again, and bulls would gain further traction with a break through the 4.07% May 10 yield high. Medium-term pattern resistance rmains at the 3.55-3.655%Mar-May yield lows.
