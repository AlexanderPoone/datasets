Turkey

How to position for an equity rally?

Here we assess the value opportunities in Turkish equities in the aftermath of an improved macro outlook stemming from simplified governmental policies and an expected recovery in global demand. Banks and Conglomerates are a consensus call, with both sectors expected to benefit from the first tranche of a potential fund flow driven rally. In the Consumer segment, we still see select names as attractively priced to reap the benefits of economies opening up once more. Our pecking order is as follows: Garanti, Yapi Kredi, Koc, Sabanci, Arcelik, Coca Cola Icecek, Anadolu Efes (u/g to OW) and Migros. Stocks that may lag the near-term rally are: BIM, Tofas, Turkish Airlines and Ulker (d/g to N).

  • •    Three pillars supporting Turkey's return to the spotlight are:

1) Supportive rule-based market policies centered on free-market practices, which is already materializing; 2) Economic growth being intact, with GDP expected to rebound to 3.6% in 2021 after a 0.6% decline this year; and 3) De-dollarization, which could take longer to materialise, depending on confidence in TL returning post implementation of prudent economic policies.

  • •    Banks - Value and growth offering: We expect Turkish banks to differentiate themselves by ROE build, with RoTE expected to increase by 5% in 2019-22 vs a 3% decline in CEEMEA banks. Despite a 40% rally in USD MTD, future earnings momentum together with a deep discount on 21E P/BV implies 90% upside potential.

  • •    Conglomerates attractive as value plays given the diversified nature of their NAVs. Koc and Sabanci trade at rare large discounts to their listed NAV, which is 22% for Koc and much deeper at 36% (CRFSA adj) for Sabanci. A stable macro economy should deliver an opportunity for Koc to trade at a premium to its listed NAV, while the discount on Sabanci narrows to 18-20%.

  • •    Staples over Retailers - room to sustain the rally: Increasing mobility combined with a recovery in Turkish tourism should spur interest in Consumer Staples, while the value + growth combination is mixed for retailers. Icecek and Efes offer attractive entry levels at a >40% discount vs global peers, and Migros remains a top pick in the Retail space as it is likely to be a key beneficiary of a post pandemic recovery in shopping malls and tourist destinations which is 15% of its revenues.
