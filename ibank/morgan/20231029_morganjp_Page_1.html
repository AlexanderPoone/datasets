Colombia

A resounding vote against Petro® administration

Merely fourteen months post-inauguration. President Petro's political coalition encountered a stark rejection in the regional elections. As analyzed previously (see note), despite the diverse political landscapes across regions and cities, the elections were transformed into a referendum on Petro Aational governance and policy agenda, leading to unequivocal electoral repercussions.

The Pacto Histórico, Petro Apolitical alliance, faced defeats in the majority of significant urban and regional territories. A notable instance was Bogota, where Gustavo Bolivar, the Pacto candidate, not only was vanquished in the first round but relegated to a third-place finish. Amere 20% of Petro's runoff supporters rallied behind Bolivar, and Carlos Galan won with 49% of the votes.

Additionally, key cities manifested support for opposition candidates: Medellin overwhelmingly backed Federico Gutierrez (gamering about 70% of the votes), while Cali and Bucaramanga also elected opponent candidates to the incumbent national president.

The remarks from Pacto's Bogota candidate, Gustavo Bolivar, aptly encapsulate the political setback. He alluded to the task of "regathering the fragments" of the leftist coalition, and acknowledged "the protest vote directed at the national government".

In essence, the government coalition alongside its allies endured a substantial political setback, potentially heralding significant political ramifications. The prevailing scenario will likely render the passage of social reforms a more arduous endeavor sans modifications. The likelihood of securing Congress approval for the social reforms has been revised downwards to below 50%. The prospects of a "national pact" remain dim, given the hurdles concerning both political backing and the eroding credibility of Petro Administration. Conversely, the potential for a radical shift in administrative stance is perceived as a low-probability outcome, given the president Aand the administration Alow approval rates.
