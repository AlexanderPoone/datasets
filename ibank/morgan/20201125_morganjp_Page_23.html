Investment Thesis, Valuation and Risks

Arcelik AS (Overweight; Price Target: TL34.50)

Investment Thesis

  • •    Shares offer a strong investment case in the short term, supported by firmer demand than expected across the industry both in Turkey and international markets.

  • •    Positive pricing trends, higher economies of scale together with fx moves are driving margins higher.

  • •    In the medium term, we see growth headwinds dominating the sector with higher raw material costs, an unfavorable base from 2H 2020 and rising interest costs. Yet, the company is still immune to these headwinds until end 1H 21 on cost side and is likely to benefit from expected rate cut cycle in Turkey by 2Q21 on interest costs.

  • •    High working capital has been a drag on capital returns and its comparable valuation to peers. Arcelik has been successfully cutting its working capital need in the past two years, paving the way for capital returns to improve in the medium term.

Valuation

We are raising Dec-21 PT to TL34.5 on the back of lower WACC which we now apply a blended WACC due to different regional exposure.

It is a DCF driven PT based on 10Y cash flow valuation that incorporates a 14.8% WACC (12% risk free rate, 12% cost of debt after tax and 8% terminal growth). We previously moved to a more optimistic model reflecting stronger EBITDA margin as well as stronger demand. Our PT implies 11.6x P/E and 6.0 x EV/EBITDA based on our 2021 estimates. To compare with global peers, PT implies 0.6x 21E EV/Sales.

Risks to Rating and Price Target

  • •    Turkey is a key market for Arcelik if it is to sustain its high margins. Therefore, any change in Turkish demand would negatively affect the company’s margins and cash burn.
