Nonetheless, the front end of the rates curve has remained remarkably well anchored: OIS forwards are pricing in approximately a 50% probability of a 25bp ease in March, less dovish than two weeks ago, but are still pricing in more than 130bp of easing this year. The front end has been relatively insulated from the sell off, in our minds, because both the December FOMC meeting, as well as the minutesof this meeting, have indicated that the Fed has made clear progress toward its inflation target, and that the next move is likely to be an ease. Meanwhile, stronger economic data havebiased longer-maturity Treasuries higher, with the broad curve reaching its steepest levels since early summer 2022 (Figure 2). Looking ahead, we recommend adding 5-year duration longs, given the considerable backup in yields from their late 2023 lows, an expectation that TIPS breakevens will reverse lower, and a more favorable technical backdrop. According to our Treasury Client Survey, net long duration positions have been reduced, while the share of neutrals has risen to its highest level since May 2023. Additionally, despite the steepening observed in recent weeks, we maintain 5s/30s curve steepeners, as the curve still appears too flat after adjusting for its drivers, and the curve tends to steepen as markets transition from Fed tightening to easing (see Treasuries).

Turning to inflation markets, 10-year TIPS breakevens have widened nearly 1 Ibp, net of carry, since reaching local lows late last year, moving back to their widest levels since early November. This move has occurred even as our broad commodities index has declined, leaving 10-year breakevens appearing nearly 20bp too wide versus our fair value model.
