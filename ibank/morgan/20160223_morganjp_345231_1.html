Anta Sports Products Ltd.

Sales pick up in Jan + Feb; potential for more deals

Anta’s share price initially reacted positively to its FYI5 result and then closed down -6% (vs. HSI at -0.25%). We think this may have been due to increasing short interest, given that Anta reported incrementally weaker sales in 4Q and a relatively underwhelming JV deal with Descente.

  • •    FY15 result above JPMf, sales decelerate in 4Q, but strong in Jan + Feb. Anta reported FYI5 EBIT -4% above JPMf and -6% above consensus. It also provided an operational update for 4Q15, with SSSG decelerating to midsingle-digit growth (vs. high-single-dig it growth for the past five quarters). We think the incremental slowdown was driven by weather, with management indicating SSSG at historical high levels in Jan+ Feb (with weather also turning colder in Southern China).

  • •    JV with Descente, with more deals likely. Anta has entered into a term sheet with Descente (8114 JP) and Itochu Corporation (8001 JP) with respect to a JV for the Descente brand in Mainland China. Anta will have a 60% stake in the JV and inject Rmbl50mn of initial capital. Management left open the possibility of more deals. We don’t think the Descente brand alone will be transformational for Anta, although further brand acquisitions could be.

  • •    Other key takeaways: (1) The company believes store productivity for Fila is #1 in high-end sportswear in China; (2) 4Q15 SSSG was negatively impacted by warm weather, with cold weather helping Jan + Feb sales reach historical record levels; (3) Anta proactively reduced 3Q16 trade fair orders somewhat to manage channel inventory; (4) 3Q16 trade fair volume growth was strong, but negatively impacted somewhat by a mix shift away from winterwear; (5) there is potential for more deals with Descente Ltd.

  • •    Remain Neutral. Anta is benefiting from improving momentum, and its short-and medium-term performance is being supported by its other brands business. However, consensus forecasts already factor in high operating margins in perpetuity and strong earnings growth over the coming years. With consensus expectations so high, we see limited risk of substantial upside to the current share price. We believe consensus forecasts factor in limited downside risk associated with Anta’s future earnings.
