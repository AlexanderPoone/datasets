SJM Holdings

Solid 2Q16, better than feared

SJM reported better-than-expected 2Q16, with EBITDA beating JPMe/consensus by 5%. Overall the quality of results also turned out to be solid (e.g. resilient mass performance, decent margin improvement), while management’s tone was quite upbeat, so the initial reaction to the stock will likely be positive. That said. SJM remains our least preferred in the sector, with an UW rating, as we still expect its share of revenues/profits to deteriorate materially over the next 12-18 months, and we think it’s too early to give it the benefit of the doubt on its Cotai execution yet.

  • •    2Q16 in a nutshell. Adjusted EBITDA fell 5% qoq and 22% yoy to HK$796m, 5% above JPMe and consensus, with the margin improving 50bp qoq to 8.0%. Interestingly, we note that its sequential profit momentum of -5% qoq is in line with the industry’s -5% qoq - i.e., unlike what we feared, SJM did not lose its “share of EBITDA” this quarter, for the first time in a long wliile.

  • •    Specifics behind 2Q. SJM’s GGR fell 10% qoq, worse than the industry’s 8% decrease qoq, dragged down by a 13% qoq drop in satellite GGR. By segment, its VIP GGR declined sharply by 19% qoq (vs. industry’s -13% qoq), wliile mass fared better and fell by only 2% qoq (the same as the industry’s -2% qoq). Although from a low base, SJM’s flagsliip Grand Lisboa (GL) outperformed the industry in both VIP (-7% qoq) and mass (flat qoq), wliich is quite respectable (and even somewhat surprising). See Table 1 for details on quarterly results.

  • •    The delta vs estimates came from: (1) better-than-expected mass perfonnance (-2% qoq, vs. JPMe -6%); (2) and resultant margin expansion at both GL (+40bp qoq to 12.6%) and other self-run casinos (+1 lObp qoq to 10.5%).

  • •    Other takeaways: (1) In-line dividend. SJM declared interim DPS of HK$0.06 in 1H16, implying a 31% payout (same as in 1H15) and 1.2% yield. Final DPS should come in liigher than interim (both in payout ratio and absolute $), as guidance is the same at “no less than 50% payout” for the full year (JPMe: 60% for FY16, vs. 57% in FY15). (2) GGR was “so far so good”, with July GGR growing MoM, in line with the seasonal trend; (3) management believes margins can be sustained at this level given mix shift toward mass; and (4) no change to Cotai project (Grand Lisboa Palace), with target opening at end-2017 and project budget of HK$32bn (or US$4. lb) including land premium.
