Euro area: Soft core pressures late last year

17 January 2024

Looking at the composition of core, fading inflationary pressures have been skewed toward core goods la st year (due to improved supply sideconditions and lower gas elec tricity prices). Meanwhile, the fading of inflationary pressures in the sendees basket has ben more modest due to strong wage growth. Interestingly, the December data hint at some turn for core core goods prices. Core goods increased for the first time since the summer. As we enter 2024, shipping issues in the Red Sea may lead to higher price pressures for core goods.

Headline price dynamics at the start of the year will also be impacted by the unwinding of measures that offset the rise in gas and electricity prices across countries. In January, taxes are set to rise across countries (VAT rates or excise duties). This will be the case in Germany, France, Italy and Spain. Thus, contrary to markets prices signalling a decline in consumer gas and electricity prices, utility prices are going to show some stickiness (and even increase on a one-off basis on some countries). Overall, we expect headline inflation to decline towards 2.5%oya in 1H24 and 2.0%oya in 2H24. For core, we expect prices to print on the firm side at the start of the year compared to recent readings due to some VAT increases (e.g. restaurant prices in Germany). We then expect core prices to increase at a near 0.2%m/m sa monthly pace next year, which would bring core inflation to2.5%oya before the summer and 2.4%oya by 4Q24. The softness in inflationary corepressures of late creates downside risks around our forecast.
