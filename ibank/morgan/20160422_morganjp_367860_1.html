China Resources Beer

SG&A cuts help 1Q, we raise our FY16 EBIT estimate by 6%

CRB announced its IQ 16 operational update with IQ 16 EBITDA and EBIT up 55% and 178%, respectively on the back of c2% revenue growth. As the company also notes in its release significant improvement in these numbers were mainly due to i) GPM expansion thanks to product mix improvement and low input costs and ii) cut in SG&A spending in 1Q16 given that it is a slow season. Nevertheless, these numbers lead to a c6% increase in Rmb terms in our FYI6 earnings estimates even after we model a hike in SG&A spending which is likely be back loaded this year during peak season. Post IQ numbers we are now looking for 5% y/y growth in revenue, 17% y/y growth in EBIT and 9% y/y growth in EBITDA in Rmb terms for FYI6 which builds in conservative margins for the remainder of the year. We are now changing the reporting currency to RMB in our model as the company has announced this change.

  • •    We reiterate our OW rating with an unchanged PT of HK$14 with current structure. While we do not model the deal in our estimates yet as we wait for the approval and details of the financing, based on our scenario analysis we estimate that purchase of the minority stake at US$1.6bn could lead to 60-75% EPS accretion and hence would translate into c40-50% potential upside to our PT as we value CRB at 20% premium to global peers. We maintain our OW rating given: i) potential upside from the deal in the near term and ii) that CRB is a beneficiary of the consolidation trends in China beer sector which we believe will continue to lead to margin upside in the long term.

  • •    CRB also published a circular which did not include details on financing terms and timing. The circular mentions the completion of minority stake purchase will take place as soon as practicable on or within 24 hours after the closing of the two international beer names’ acquisition. Therefore, CRB will cooperate with and assist them in seeking MOFCOM’s approval of the acquisition. The circular adds that CRB is still considering all options with regard to the funding.
