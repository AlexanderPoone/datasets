China re-opening trackers chartpack (Series 15)

Mapping high-frequency trackers to official monthly activity indicators

We make our first attempt to map the high-frequency trackers to official monthly activity indicators in this edition. We also add operating rates forsemi- and all-steel tires to track auto production, as well as a deadweight tonnage breakdown for container and bulk ships. We update the latest tracking of where China stands in terms of individual mobility, supply-chain logistics and major economic activity since our last update on March 2. Key takeaways are highlighted below.

  • •    The deviation of su bway traffici ndex from its 2019 average tracks retail sales. Operating rates for semi-steel and full-steel tires track the auto production component of industrial production. Operating rates for steel plants can also give some color on steel production. Deadweight tonnage of departing arrived ships is a timely tracker to gauge near-term trade momentum.

  • •    Subway traffic index stabilized at nearly 30% higher than the 2019 average.

  • •    Deadweight tonnage of both departing and arrived ships at 20 major ports generally turned up further last week, especially for containerships though bulk ship departures remained weak. Total deadweight tonnage of departing ships at 20 major ports grew 40%oya last week, partly due to Shanghai lockdown related port operation disruption in March 2022.

  • •    Scheduled outbound flights are set to surge to Italy (58.6%), Korea (29.2%), ANZ (41.5%) and ASEAN (34%) by the end of March.

  • •    Housing transactions trended down on seasonality last week, but are still 24.8% higher than 2022.

  • •    Auto sales rebounded into the month-end as expected. For February as a whole, auto wholesales grew 1.7%ova or fell 3.4%m/m nsa, while auto retail sales grew 3.14%ova or eased 0.6%in/in nsa.

  • •    Operating rates for both semi- and all- steel tires picked up forcefully in the past month, pointing to solid auto production recovery.

  • •    Special LGBs issued reached 11.4bnyuanin thefirstweek, with another 127bn yuan scheduled before March 15. The issuancecalendar targets 365bn yuan for March (vs. actual issuance of 421 bn for March 2022).
