Travel Recovery Tracker -Oct/Nov Data Shows Recovery Still Underway

Data tracked through Late November, including Asia/Oceania passenger traffic after HK/Japan reopenings, US citizen departures by region, and airline commentary suggest 1) the int'L travel recovery continues, though at a slower pace postsummer & 2) there's still volume opp Left to recover for V/MA.

Cross bordertravel recovery continued in Oct/Nov, albeit at slightly slower pace with more still to go. Data we track across airports, airlines, hotels, and from other third-party travel data sources suggest demand and trends for international travel remain healthy. Visa's most recent intra-quarter data shows that card-present cross-border travel-related volume was at 117% of 2019's Level in November (up from -115% in September), supported by improving traffic in the US and Asia-Pacific travel corridors. Although the pace of recovery has slowed in recent months, we think that was somewhat expected coming off of a surge in summer travel. Data available for November shows hotel occupancy rates as compared to 2019 generally stable across Europe and the US, while US TSA data for November shows passenger throughput as a % of 2019 Levels remains steady vs. Late summer. Additionally, despite strong travel recoveries in Asia and Oceania in recent months, there's still meaningful passenger traffic Left to recapture in those regions. IATA revenue passenger kilometers to and from Asia-Pac was at 42% of 2019 Levels in September, while for Europe it was at 78%.

Asia-pac reopenings driving clear acceleration in traffic while we continue to await China updates. Data we track shows a clear acceleration in tourism traffic once border restrictions are Lifted. For example, we're encouraged by Hong Kong's recent easing of quarantine requirements for travelers, which appears to be driving an uptick in airport traffic in that region in October, and by Japan's recent reopening which helped double the foreign traveler arrivals to the country in October vs. September. Airports in Singapore and Australia began recovering rapidly earlier this year when those governments reopened, and are seeing passenger traffic at -65/55% of 2019 levels, up from 11/13% in January. More notably, China, which remains the Last major economy with strict restrictions in place, recently began easing travel requirements, though rising Covid-19 cases in recent weeks may stall further loosening. Though Ch in a-specific cross-border spend is a relatively small part of total V/MA volumes, we think the full reopening of China will be key to driving a full travel demand/volume recovery in the Asia-Pac region.
