Hong Kong & China Gas

Good time to consider our UW call amid rising yields

HK & China Gas outperformed the market and peers by 5-10% the past month. We believe this was partly due to investors’ search for a safer play in HK-based assets amid regulatory risks in the China gas sector and Rmb depreciation. HKCG shares are richly valued at 26x P/E and 2.5% dividend yield on the back of slowing operating profit growth of c5% pa during 2016-18. We reiterate our UW call on HKCG and recommend OW on ENN and Power Assets instead.

  • •    More HKD protection from other names. Amid a depreciating Rmb vs HKD and USD, we believe investors have been attracted to HKCG due to its Hong Kong gas business. However, we believe CLP (2HK) and HK Electric Investments (263 8HK) would be better alternatives for that purpose because they respectively have c70% and 100% of their earnings from HK, unlike HKCG which has only c40%.


  • •    Regulatory overhang in China. The government has been pushing for lower retail gas prices, which could potentially squeeze the margins for gas utilities. This has resulted in the sector’s recent underperformance. However, HKCG outperformed its China gas peers by 10%, which we think is unjustified because c60% of its business would be impacted if margins were indeed compressed.

  • •    Rich valuation for mid single-digit growth. Shares of HKCG are trading at 26x P/E, 3x P/B and 2.5% yield. This is a >30% premium vs its HK utilities’ peers, which we find unjustified as HKCG’s single-digit earnings growth is in line with growth from other HK utilities. Our top pick in HK utilities is Power Assets, which has a 3.8% yield.

  • •    Risks to our call. 1) Higher oil prices could improve earnings at the New Energy segment and indirectly result in higher gas demand in China; 2) Higher-than-expected gas consumption in HK and China; 3) Share buybacks by the company.
