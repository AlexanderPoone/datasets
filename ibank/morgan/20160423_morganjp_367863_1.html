Great Wall Motor - H

Better than feared, but not enough to warrant a reversal

Great Wall Motor’s 1Q16 result was better than feared, at -6% yoy, but with a sustained margin vs. 2015. We tweak our forecast and now expect GWM’s earnings to start to fall in 2017 (vs. 2016 previously). The stock could see a relief rebound, but we believe it would be short-lived, as our cautious thesis is unchanged: (1) competition and margin pressure; and (2) rising investment to meet China’s fuel economy target. Our top picks among China OEMs are Guangzhou Auto-11, BYD-H and Geely.

  • •    Recap of our thesis: We downgraded GWM in Jun-15 (click here) on expectations that: (1) rising competition in the SUV space, including foreign JVs and Chinese brands, would collectively pressure GWM to either cut prices or raise marketing expenses. The most formidable rivals at the - Rmbl00-12Ok price point, and hence threats to GWM’s mainstream H6 model, in our view, include Geely's recent launch "Bo Yue" SUV and Volkswagen’s budget SUV (by SAIC-VW) from late 2016, besides existing contenders such as Guangzhou Auto's GS4 and Changan Auto's CS75; and (2) mounting investment would be required to develop GWM’s own new energy vehicle models to meet China's fuel consumption target (click here for our analysis on this topic).

  • •    1Q16 results highlights: PAT slipped 6% yoy as margins contracted: GPM to 25.3% vs. 26.6% in 1Q15, despite 6% revenue growth. The expense ratio (11.3% of sales) was lower than expected, but we believe this will edge up along with new launches in the remainder of the year.
