Asia Pacific Equity Research 05 August 2022


Alibaba Group Holding Limited (9988.HK and BABA US)

Expecting earnings revision to turn positive

Despite macro uncertainties, which in our view have already been priced into investor expectations, we think Alibaba5s cost optimization initiatives will continue to drive profit upside in the coming quarters. We believe the bottom-line growth rate bottomed out in June quarter and will accelerate to 35%+ YoY in March quarter 2023, driven by 1) a recovery of highly profitable domestic econnnerce revenue (CMR) and 2) cost optimization across a large number of operations. We expect earnings revision to turn positive post June quarter and to serve as a key catalyst in the next few quarters. Maintaining OW.

  • •    Expecting a gradual recovery of CMR We expect Alibaba5s domestic GMV growth rate to return to positive territory (JPMe 0.4%) in Sep quarter 2022, followed by a gradual recovery to 3% in Dec quarter 2022, due to post COVID consumption recovery and low base effect. As a result, CMR growth rate will also return positive in Sep quarter and accelerate in the subsequent quarters.

  • •    ... Coupled with rapid cost reduction. We attribute the supermajority of the earnings beat in June quarter to Alibaba5s cost optimization initiatives and we expect these initiatives to drive more earnings upside in the coming quarters. From a group development perspective, Alibaba accomplished the user acquisition stage in the domestic market after achieving lb annual active customers in China. The next stage of development for the group is to retain these users and expand Alibaba5s wallet share across different user groups. This stage usually generates higher returns than the user acquisition stage, in our view. From a business line perspective, we think Alibaba has changed business strategy from market share gain to quality growth in a number of deeply loss-making operations (i.e. food delivery, Taocaicai, Taobao deal). We estimate losses from these 3 operations peaked at RMB 18b in Dec quarter 2021 but will rapidly narrow in the coming quarters (JPMe RMB 8b in Dec quarter 2022)
