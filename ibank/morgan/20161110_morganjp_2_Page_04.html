1HFY17 results review

We note that our estimates were last changed in May 2016 and consensus FYI7 EPS had decreased by 19% since that time (presumably factoring in more restructuring costs related to LF). Therefore, while 1HFY17 sales and net profits disappointed our estimates, we note that our revised FYI7 EPS estimate (factoring in weak 1HFY17) is still actually 5% higher than consensus. Therefore, the 1HFY17 results were probably within the expectations of consensus already and we do not expect a very negative reaction post the interim earnings release.

1HFY17 sales were 7% lower than our estimate (up 6% Y/Y or down 1% Y/Y excluding LF). The disappointment was mostly related to a 12% decrease Y/Y in sales of telephone products (to US$278m). Management did mention that the weakness (especially in Europe) was due to exceptional political uncertainties and many telephone companies adjusting down their inventory levels. While management expects 2HFY17 sales to be an improvement on 1HFY16, we take a more conservative stance and expect a small single digit decrease in telephone sales for 2HFY17. GPM for 1HFY17 improved by 1.1% to 31.9%, and this was more or less in line with our expectations (lower raw materials and lower Rmb should lift margins). However, the losses at Leapfrog (approx.. $25m) are higher than our expectations of $12-13m and mclude inventory adjustments, severance, and other one-off restructuring costs. The loss of $25m is surprising given sales of only about $70m related to the leapfrog acquisition over this period. On the positive side, management did mention most of the expenses related to restructuring are completed in 1HFY17 but we still factor in an additional $10m loss in 2HFY17 related to LF in our revised estimates.
