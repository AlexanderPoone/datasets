The Last Set of US Government Data for Now?

The past week saw a set of revisions to activity measures that changed some perspectives more than others. Our economists flagged a meaningful downward revision to real GDP, and a more modest upward revision to real GDI. We had thought this might be able to change the Fed's perspective on growth.

Instead, the outcome showed only a marginal downward revision to real GDP, and a Larger upward revision to real GDI than our economists projected. The changes Left real GDP+ (the average of GDP and GDI) at 1.3% Y/Y and at 1.4% Q/Q SAAR for 2Q23 - both below most estimates of potential growth.

Nevertheless, FOMC participants do not appear focused on GDP+ or GDI. Rather, they appear concerned about strength in GDP alone. In that regard, we note that the monthly PCE report raised our economists' 3Q real GDP tracking from 2.3% to 3.7%, driven mostly by inventories and trade.

As for personal consumption expenditure (PCE), after a downwardly revised 0.8% annualized growth rate in 2Q23, our economists track 3Q23 at 2.4%, driven entirely by consumption strength in July, likely related to "Barbenheimer", Taylor Swift's "The Eras" tour, and Beyonce's "Renaissance" tour.
