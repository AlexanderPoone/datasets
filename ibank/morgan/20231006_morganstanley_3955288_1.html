Global Macro Strategist

A Phoenix from the Ashes?

Continuing bearish price momentum was a key reason why we recently reevaluated our call on UST duration. Extremely robust US labor market data reinforces this thesis. We stay neutral on US rates, after have been stopped out, but maintain a bullish stance on the USD.

Global Macro Strategy

We discuss how volatility of US economic data muddies the investment waters. We also discuss the recent IMF release of its quarterly COFER data on FX reserve holdings, and how it points to a $100bn increase in USD reserves, once we factor in valuation effects on fixed income and FX.

Interest Rate Strategy

We stay neutral on UST duration. We maintain Long SFRZ4 on SFRU3Z4Z5 fly. We open a Dec 129.5/131 Bund call spread. We maintain our EUR 10y10y rec and our Dec 23 mid-curve 96.75/97.OO/97.25/97.5O call condor. On the curve, we keep our flatteners on EUR 2s10s and 30s50s swap. On ASW, we maintain the 10s30s OAT ASW box (short 30y OAT) and our conditional Bund ASW widener. We maintain GBP/EUR 2s/10s box (pay GBP) and Long IL28. We close our short 5y ASW position as valuations moved towards our target Level. We maintain TONA OIS 2s5s steepener (DV011.5 vs 1) and Long JGB 5y vs pay 7y OIS (DV01 2 vs 1). We close Long 20y JGB ASW vs ESTR. We enter receive 10y OIS vs sell 30y JGB (DV011.5 vs 1).

Currency & Foreign Exchange

Further USD gains in a falling rates world Likely requires softer risk assets as well. Stay Long USD for now. We preview FX implications of next week's US CPI print and caution that FX investors should not forget about CPI as a currency driver going forward. We think investors are overly concerned that the USD will Lose its dominant-currency crown. We discuss the potential impact in MoF's clarification for "excess volatility". We discuss why the Norges bank has consistently sold NOK.

Inflation-Linked Bonds

In the US, we remain neutral on duration and inflation.

Short-Duration Strategy

Maintain short 2y SOFR swap spread and Long SFRZ4 on U3Z4Z5 fLy. We discuss the recent RRP decline and what "sticky" reserves could mean.
