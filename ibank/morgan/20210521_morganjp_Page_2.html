Where crypto meets FRA/OIS

Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies have once again dominated headlines as many tokens lost 20-30% (or more) of their value in only a few days. Though a large and increasingly impactful asset class in its own right, these moves have been mostly confined to that market, with little evidence of spillover let alone contagion (see also The J.P. Morgan Viewx, J. Normand et al., 5/21/21). Even price action in gold, for which Bitcoin in particular is often considered a competitor as a store of value, has been roughly uncorrelated on a daily basis over the past two weeks. That said, there are areas in which we see and expect more interconnectedness between the crypto and traditional markets. Though it may be surprising, one area is money markets and funding spreads though the growth of stablecoins and the investment of their reserve assets.

First, a brief primer. Stablecoins are digital tokens that, as the name implies, are designed to maintain a stable exchange rate into U.S. dollars and other currencies (for an overview, see Can stablecoins achieve global scale?, J. Younger et al., 12/3/19). The most common form is asset-backed, where the tokens in principle represent a claim on a pool of fiat currency securities (the reserve account) which are typically focused on short-term, highly liquid instruments. Another is algorithmic, for which creation (minting) and destruction (burning) is automated to maintain their peg. The former is by far more common, making up -95% of total market capitalization at present.



Though always an interesting diversion, the exposure growth of this segment of the market has made their activity potentially much more impactful. More than $60bn such tokens have been minted since the beginning of the year, a roughly three fold increase (Exhibit 1). That makes them collectively among the largest prime fund-style liquidity portfolios globally. This likely relates primarily to increased turnover in the ciyptocurrency market more generally, where they are a key piece of market infrastructure.
