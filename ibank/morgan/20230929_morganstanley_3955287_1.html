September 29

JGB sell-off prompts unscheduled BoJ rinban; Softer Euro Area core inflation supports lower EGB yields; CAD weakens broadly after indications of further stagnant growth; Ban Rep keeps rates at 13.25%; USTs bull-steepen slightly on quarter-end; US 10y at 4.571% (-0.4bp).

Please refer to our recent publications and collaborations (Global Macro Strategy, Public Policy, Ej US Economics: A Missing CPI Print; Mexico Economics Ej Strategy: Banxico Review; Government Bond Auction Pipeline: The Month Ahead; EM Strategy: The GBI-EM Investors' Lowdown; EM Sovereign Credit Strategy: EM Technical Watch; EM Fixed Income Flows Update: Where Supply Meets Demand; Podcast I The Global Macro Guide: September 29).


  • •    JGB sell-off prompts unscheduled BoJ JGB purchase operations for the 5-10y sector (|PY300bn), but the sell-off persists into the Tokyo close.

  • •    Softer Euro Area core inflation data support a sharp decline in European government bond yields; 10y Bunds rally 9.1bp to 2.84%.

  • •    CAD exhibits broad-based weakness after Canadian growth is flat m/ m in July (C: 0.1%), with indications for only 0.1% m/m growth in August; USD/CAD rises 0.7% to 1.358.

  • •    BanRep Leaves its policy rate at 13.25% in a split decision (5:2). Our economists expect BanRep to begin gradual rate cuts at its October meeting, markets price 20bp of cuts for this meeting.

  • •    USTs trade in a roundabout manner but eventually a bull-steepening persists on quarter-end, 5-10y UMich inflation expectations of 2.8% is the Lowest since September 2022.
