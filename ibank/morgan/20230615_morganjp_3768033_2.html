Executive summary

  • B Growth target “around 5%” at the NPC

  • ■ Post-reopening recovery is bumpy and uneven

  • ■    Solid recovery in 1Q at 11.9%q/q saar, but loss of momentum since April

  • ■    Full-year growth forecast: 5.5%y/y (real term); 4.8%y/y (nominal term)

  • ■    Quarterly profile: 11.9% /1.2% / 4.9% / 5.5%q/q saar in the four quarters, respectively

  • ■    Growth drivers: consumption and investment to contribute 4.1%pts and 1.7%, net exports to drag 0.3%pt

  • ■    Uneven recovery and near-term headwinds

  • ■    Uneven recovery between services and production, between catering / jewelry sales and other consumption, between policy tailwind sectors (e.g. NEV) and other sectors, between public and private investment

  • ■    Expect weak-form stabilization for the housing market, but the risk of double-dip is increasing especially weak land sales and new home starts, spillover to LGFV stress

  • ■    External sector headwinds

  • ■    Growth stabilization measures in action

  • ■    PBOC rate cut In June; NDRC measures for favorable tax/fee policies

  • ■    Supplementary measures: special local government issuance; RRR cut; city-level housing policy relaxation; product-specific consumption stimulus, ...

  • ■    Further stimulus is data-dependent: policy bank support, special Treasury bond, further rate cut, nationwide housing policy relaxation

  • ■    Tolerance for flexible CNY movements
