Hospital capital spending survey shows 2024 spending levels will be consistent y/y, driven by increasing patient volume expectations paired against rising costs. Our survey (here) of over 100 hospital executives shows '24 capital spending is expected to advance 3.8% y/y, largely in Line with expectations for '23 (+4%). Most hospitals anticipate increased purchasing across all surveyed categories, though there are apparent shifting spend priorities. Soft-tissue robotics purchasing looks well protected, and there's an uptick in expected orthopedic robotic spend, though dialysis machines appear to be less of a '24 spending priority. Hospitals continue to experience pent-up demand, though the backlog appears to be improving with 60% of respondents saying Less than 50% of the overall pent-up demand remain. Furthermore, only12% of respondents indicated their hospital's current elective backlog is trending above Levels experienced three-months ago. Also, worth noting, two-thirds of hospitals expect to increase investments in ambulatory surgery centers (ASCs), while another 34% will keep spending unchanged. Roughly 60% of respondents agree that working with a single ASC vendor is important.
