Ping An Insurance Group - H

Tech Yield Update + Investor Day

In addition to the conventional investment thesis for Ping An (outstanding insurance fundamentals, improving balance sheet on the banking business and deleveraging potential on the trust/P2P lending platform), we view the utilization of technology as a new share price/earnings driver because it will enable the company to differentiate its improving underwriting efficiency and extra client/scale growth momentum. Big Data analysis for life, wearable devices for health, and claims control techniques using telematics/drones for P&C are examples. Ping An is developing its internet business, but, compared to group business scale, it looks too small in terms of earnings/ value contribution and we would like more clarity regarding Internet business opportunities and the “marginal value” contribution at the Corporate Day. With near-term events (IAIS global seminar and stakeholder dialogue in Macau on 18-19 June, Corporate Day on 26 June) and strong 1H results expected in August, we believe the recent price weakness presents a buying opportunity. Ping An remains our top pick, with 35% upside potential from current levels.

Putting technologies into perspective. We believe Ping An has a good opportunity to utilize technology as a new share price differentiator over the next couple of years: (1) We expect further improvement in the combined ratio of Ping An P&C. The overall cost structure is expected to be slim (i.e., lower expense ratio) following the customer migration from off-line to online. Considering the high car accident ratio in the country and less efficient claims adjustment structure, telematics (auto )/d rones (non-auto) provide a case for structural claims improvement. (2) Ping An Life should benefit from higher NBV growth/scale momentum utilizing Big Data. (3) Ping An Health is expected to learn scientific medical claims control techniques, adopting “Vitality” used by its JV partner Discovery in South Africa (ownership -25%).
