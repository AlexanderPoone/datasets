FX & EM Strategy: Adding Long USD Exposure

James Lord

Time to add some long USD exposure: As volatility in global bank stocks picked up in March, we removed any remaining bullish EM exposure. At the time, we felt that it was a prudent decision given the high uncertainty over the outlook for the financial sector in the US and Europe. Over the past month of course EM has powered on, with USD weakening, and we would have been better off sticking to our constructive view.

Yet, we believe that the increasingly benign message sent by financial assets, whether global equities or EM currencies, over the past month is at odds with the growing risk of a US recession. From one perspective, the weakness of USD is understandable if we consider the US in isolation. A downturn in the US will, all else equal, push interest rate differentials and returns on capital more generally in favour of other parts of the world.

This same perspective would also argue that all is not equal either, with growth prospects in the rest of the world enhanced by the outlook for the Chinese economy, where we expect the recovery in domestic demand to continue in the coming months.

However, our perspective is that this Line of thinking has become consensus while also risking complacency. The US slowdown could spill over to the rest of the world while the monetary tightening we've seen in other parts of the world could also begin to bite. Both channels could then cause investors to reassess the outlook.

Should investors reassess, then they would be doing so with investor FX positioning leaning short USD. Our FX options positioning tracker shows how investors have added Long positions across G3 and EM currencies.
