Fed Weekly: Sharp Drop In C&l Loans Last Week;

C&l Still Up Strongly qtd; Total Loans Up 7.2% yoy

C&I loans dropped sharply last week, down 0.8%, partly reversing some of the increase seen during the first six weeks of the quarter. This was partly offset by moderate growth in CRE and residential mortgage loans (which can be volatile). Last week saw' continued seasonal decline in credit cards and continued runoff in home equity. Other loans were flattish. As a result, total loans were down 0.1% on reported and -0.3% on core basis last week. However, total loans are still up solidly in IQ to date, up 0.3% qoq and 7.2% yoy - note that IQ is generally seasonally a weak quarter. Securities portfolio dropped sizably led by Treasuries and agencies plus Agency MBS. Deposits were flattish while borrowings rose moderately. Deposits are still down qtd but banks are issuing debt at a much faster pace qtd. Loan loss reserves were flat last week in amount and as percentage of loans.
