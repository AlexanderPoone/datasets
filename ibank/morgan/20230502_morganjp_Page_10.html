Foreign holdings at low ebb; domestic ownership at new highs

Foreign ownership of the stock market has fallen sharply in the last the four years from >60% to only 29%. Local ownership versus foreign ownership is a teeter-totter; we could just as easily say local ownership rose from 40% to 71%. We cannot remember a sharper, fester decline in foreign ownership in a stock market that was not removed from a maj or index. Our Index research team estimates roughly 12% of the big caps are held by index trackers; that means active holdings are down from c. 50% to 15% in those four years. The sharp drop in foreign ownership is - no surprise here - matched by sharp foreign outflows with total outflows of $ llbn since ‘20. Should foreign ownership return to 50%, then in flows into Turkiye would be $ 15 -20bn, or more if foreigners bought in at higher prices.

We see both push (foreign selling) and pull factors (domestic buying) in this decline. On the foreign selling, we think the longterm underperformance was the key driver of foreign selling. It is worth noting that corporate governance standards remain high and disclosure by the Turkish corporates remain among the best in the region. On the domestic buying side of the teeter-totter, we think the sharp rise in inflation and significant drop in real yields has pushed locals out of bonds into stocks.
