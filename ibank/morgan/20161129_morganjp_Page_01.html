Tingyi (Cayman Islands) Holding Corp

Like to see consumer pull rather than distribution push; maintain UW

Following 3Q results, we maintain our UW rating for Tingyi. Positives were: i) Sudden pick-up in beverages revenue (up 9% y/y vs 16% y/y decline in 2Q16), and ii) Noodles opex flat on q/q basis despite weak sales. Going into 2017, we argue that: i) There is no major improvement in beverage product in our view. While a low base and fixing water pricing is likely to keep momentum positive for three more quarters, we do not see any real momentum in the product that could lead to sustainable growth, ii) Noodles industry volumes are declining and Tingyi has not gained back market share. We need a big uplift in terms of innovation and taste in noodles. Tingyi is not delivering on this front yet. iii) Sales momentum is improving but input costs are moving up; thus, difficult to see a sharp improvement in margins. After incorporating better-than-expected 3Q results, our estimates are now in line with Bloomberg consensus. The stock trades at 27x 17E P/E with c8% recurring earnings CAGR in local currency post 17E. Given stretched valuations and little conviction on sustainable growth that should come from consumer pull, we maintain our UW rating with a revised Dec-17 PT of HK$7.

  • •    No major improvement in beverage: In our view, the company pushed RMB 1 bottle water effectively in 3Q and during the analyst meeting mentioned distributors’ inventory being a bit higher than normal at the end of September. This could signal channel inventory issues if not handled quickly. While a low base for beverages and fixing water pricing is likely to keep beverage sales momentum positive for three more quarters, we do not see any real momentum in the product.

  • •    Noodles industry volumes declining; Tingyi’s 3Q noodles performance was below industry and market share continued to weaken slightly (3Q16 market share was 42.5% in terms of value down from 43% in 1H16). Management notes it is difficult to do upgrades quickly but at the same time we see Nissin and Nongshim delivering 8% and 25% sales growth in China during 3Q16. While it is true these are off of a small base, it still shows that the category is not entirely dead in China but needs a big uplift in terms of innovation/taste. This is not seen at Tingyi products yet as management’s focus is still on RMB4 product and how to push it again after adjusting pricing. With that in mind, we find it difficult to get constructive on sustainable noodles sales growth - while the next two quarters will benefit from the base.
