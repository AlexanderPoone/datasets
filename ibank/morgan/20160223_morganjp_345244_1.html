Hang Seng Bank

FY15 results slightly below on weaker PPOP, special dividend is a positive but not a surprise

HSB held its FY15 result conference call on Monday afternoon. FY15 earnings came in slightly below our expectation and market consensus due to weaker PPOP but stable asset quality and special dividends were the positives. We revise down our earnings forecasts by 4% for FYI6/17 and retain our Neutral rating with a new Dec-16 PT of HKS 146, given limited valuation upside.

  • •    Group FY15 earnings of HK$27.2bn (excluding preferred dividends of HK$310m) were up 80% YoY and implied ROE was 20.7%. Excluding the impacts of Industrial Bank, underlying profits grew 4% YoY with ROE of 13.8%. HSB declared a final dividend of HKS2.4 and a special dividend of HK$3 per share, implying a payout ratio of 61% for FYI5 (71% in FY14).

  • •    What was positive: stable asset quality and strong capital position. Impaired loan balance went down HoH in both HK and China in 2H15, and both credit costs and impaired loan ratio improved HoH (15bps & 0.4% vs. 18bps and 0.43% in 1H15, respectively), in line with management guidance of stable asset quality outlook. CETI ratio improved to 17.7% helped by HoH decline at RWA upon unwinding of market risks associated with the proceeds of IB stake divestments; CETI ratio will remain at a veiy comfortable level of 16.6% even after the special dividend payout.

  • •    What was negative: weaker PPOP on NIM pressure and lower fee income. PPOP was flat YoY mainly due to continued NIM contraction, -6bps HoH in 2H15, while management holds a cautious outlook in FYI6 upon risk-aversion and negative, but manageable, impacts from extra reserve requirements on CNH deposits placed at onshore China. Brokerage and fund sales fees dropped 32-40% HoH and it will be challenging to see YoY growth in FYI6, in our view, given the high base in 1H15.

  • •    Special dividend a positive but not a surprise. The 6.5% share price rally today (vs. +0.93% for the HSI) should already reflect the special dividends, which should be viewed as a one-off hinged upon the divestment gains, according to management. HSB continues to stand out given the defensive nature of the underlying operation and management quality but with 13-14% ROE outlook, we still struggle to justify additional valuation upside when the share price is already at 1.8x P/B and expectation of NIM expansion upon a US rate hike is likely to be further postponed.
