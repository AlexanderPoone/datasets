  • •    Overview: Living through the transitional pain. Financial markets end 2023 unconvinced about a durable growth recovery in China, recognizing the drawn-out nature of the economic rebalancing away from the property sector to advance manufacturing. We expect only a limited growth impulse in 2024, which will likely limit the shelf life of any bullish China trades going forward.

  • •    CNY FX: Not out of the woods yet. External headwinds are on course to moderate for CNY FX next year as core yields embark on a durable downtrend, which portend a path of lower USD CNY. However, a bounded lift in China’s growth, still punitive carry and geopolitical uncertainty would warrant a relatively shallow correction. We see room for seasonal CNY strength to play out over the short mn on year-end corporate conversion (we stay received ly CNH CCS), but still lean bearishly on CNY TWI over the medium term, seeing itasa low-yielding proxy for USD especially in periods ofDXY weakness next year.

  • •    CNY rates: Leaning bullish. The ongoing deleveraging campaign requires the PBoC to lean structurally dovish, while easing external headwinds and less acute stress in FX stability should provide the PBoC more leeway in offering easing support. This presents overall a positive policy backdrop for duration. Fiscal-monetary coordination in China has traditionally ensured that issuance pressures stemming from a more expansionary fiscal stance are relatively smoothly accommodated. CGB yields currently screen high and restrictive, while cheapness in front-end rates worth fading in particular. We stay long ly CGB ASW (vs pay ly NDIRS).
