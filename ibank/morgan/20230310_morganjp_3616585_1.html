US Fixed Income Overview

Assessing the fallout of SIVB collapse across markets

  • •    Following SIVB’s collapse, yields declined and spreads widened in a flight-to-quality trade. While we believe fears of broader financial contagion are unwarranted, and we do not expect to see ongoing stress from this week’s events, further weakness in mortgage markets is possible as SIVB’s HTM portfolio is liquidated

  • •    We review fixed income demand in 2022, which was primarily driven by households, foreign investors, and life insurance companies

  • •    Economics: Nonfarm payrolls came in at 311k while the unemployment rate ticked up to 3.6%. We continue to expect a 25bp hike in March. We project headline CPI rose 0.4% and core by 0.5% in Februaiy

  • •    Treasuries: Rich valuations and a more hawkish Fed present bearish risks, but regional bank concerns might linger and risk appetite remains low: stay neutral duration. Hold 10s/30s steepeners. We review February STRIPS activity. BEs look cheap, but we remain neutral given a hawkish Fed

  • •    Derivatives: Fears of contagion are likely overblown, and we continue to favor carry trades designed to benefit from the inversion of the Fronts/Reds curve. We turn neutral on 5Y swap spreads, but continue to look for spread widening in the front end. We also recommend tactically unwinding long volatility exposure and turning neutral

  • •    Securitized products: Mortgages sharply widened on Friday in anticipation of a liquidation of SIVB assets, leaving lower coupon 30yrsat the cheapest levels in a while. Valuations do look attractive to us across most of the stack, but there are clearly unknowns and stresses in the market that are hard to quantify

  • •    Corporates: We discuss our initial thoughts on the impact of SIVB to US HG credit and ensuing spread widening on the week along with analysis of relationship to rate volatility and the BBB/A relationship

  • •    Short-Tenn Fixed Income: Silvergate/Silicon Valley Bank were not CP/CD issuers, and fears of contagion from this week’s events are likely unwarranted. But small banks could act to shore up liquidity via tenn borrowings and/or selling securities

  • •    Near-term catalysts: February CPI (3/14), February leading indicators (3/17), March FOMC meeting (3/21-3/22), 4Q final real GDP (3/30)
