Agile Property Holdings Ltd

1H13 result review: below expectations; a long way to turnaround

Agile's earnings missed estimates due to higher margins. Overall launch schedule remains and sales in 402013 should pick up we believe. Its net gearing was lowered due to perpetual issuance. We retain our Overweight rating on Agile as we think the downside to the stock is limited and with the stock being a laggard in the sector, it could outperform its peers if it delivers an upside surprise on contracted sales in 40. That said, we do not think a turnaround of its operations will happen soon; hence, a fill-scale re-rating is not likely at this point in our opinion. Our Jun-14 PT of HKS10.0 per share is based on 6.0x 2013/14E average P/E.

Earnings missed due to lower margin: Agile reported core net profit of Rmb 1,500 mn for 1H2013, down 27% Y/Y and 3 3% below our estimate. Fully diluted core EPS was RmbO.435, down 27% Y/Y. Interim DPS was flat at RmbO.145, implying 2.1% interim dividend yield and a payout ratio of 30%. Agile’s top line was in line but earnings missed mainly because of (1) higher development costs, and (2) higher SG&A. We reduce our earnings forecasts by -9% to -13% accordingly.

  • •    Sales may pick up in 4Q: Agile has planned for an increased launch in 2H2013 and expect there to be some pick-up in contracted sales toward 4Q, but still quite far from a full turnaround in our view. Agile has bought too much expensive land in 2009 and 2010, and they are forced to develop it into high-end products to justify the margin. Such high-end products are not favored by the current policy environment; hence, take-up is slow, so is the growth. Management did mention cutting prices to stimulate sales and clear up inventoiy. However, we think management is not that determined in clearing up inventory. Preserving margin should still be the key focus in our view.

Aggressive landbanking: As expected, Agile has stepped up in landbanking. So far in 2013, Agile has purchased 19 land lots with an increased number of mid-size, fast turnover projects which should bring forth some improvement of sales in 2014 onwards. However, Agile has also bought land in six new cities. We are a bit concerned on Agile’s ability in managing so many projects in new cities at the same time.
