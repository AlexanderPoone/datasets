Greater China Semiconductors I Asia Pacific

YMTC expansion Leads to China's 2024 WFE upside

We further raise our 2024 wafer fab equipment outlook for China to factor in YMTC's 30-40kwpm expansion in 2024 along with SMIC and Hua Hong's hikes in 2024 capex guidance.

Key Takeaways

  • ■    YMTC's capacity expansion of 30-40kwpm in 2024 will Lead to more upside for WFE in China in 2024. Its investment budget could reach ~US$3.0bn.

  • ■    AMEC should enjoy the most significant upside - it should be able to get 60-70% etching share with YMTC.

  • ■    On the other hand, Hua Hong's aggressive Fab 9 expansion should lead to more pricing pressure on mature node foundries.

We see more upside for 2024e for China's key wafer fab equipment (WFE) players, factoring in Yangtze Memory Technologies' (YMTC) 30-40kwpm investment (or US$3.0bn): Our industry checks indicate that YMTC will likely start to adopt local etching equipment (60-70% from AMEC, 30-40% from Naura and others) and thus that YMTC should be able to expand capacity 30-40kwpm in 2024 (Exhibit 2).

Thus, we raise our forecasts for AMEC, ACMR and Naura as YMTC's expansion fuels substantial upside for AMEC (revenue contribution >35%) and Naura (>5%): We believe YMTC will become one of AMEC's largest clients in 2024 (>35%), according to our scenario analysis. We learned that AMEC should start to ship to YTMC in 1H24.

AMEC guided for 2024 revenue to be >Rmb8.0bn. The GM should be 1-2ppt Y/Y lower than in 2023, as the majority of equipment shipments are for Larger customers this year (vs. 2O23's being mainly to Tier 2/Tier 3 customers). The company expects its net margin to reach 20-25%.

We believe ACM Research's 2024e revenue will grow 30-40% Y/Y, backed by a strong order backlog: ACMR has clear order visibility for 1Q24-3Q24. Order Lead time is now six months. ACMR is quite positive on China's capex demand in 2024 because the majority of the investment is coming from Large customers, such as Hua Hong (Fab 9, Fab 10), mature node foundries, and memory projects.

Hua Hong not slowing down Fab 9 investment plan - positive for WFE players, but more pricing pressure for mature node foundry peers: For Wuxi Fab 9, the company will start pulling in equipment in 2024, with a US$2.Obn capex budget (vs. 2O23's US$1.Obn) to expand 40kwpm equipment for its phase 1 installation - this equipment should be able to contribute revenue starting from 1Q25. The total budget for Wuxi Fab 9 is US$6.7bn (to reach 83kwpm).
