Investment Thesis

Anton Oil is one of China’s independent oil sen ices companies, with Schlumberger as its strategic shareholder (19% interest). The company comprises four segments: down-hole operations, well completion, drilling technology and tubular services. Il derives 55% revenue from domestic China in Ill 15 (versus 72% in Ill 14. due to noticeable domestic market slowdown). Our negative view on the firm is based on:

  • •    Strong headwinds in domestic orders... The company has limited order visibility for the medium term, with several quarters of less-than-impressive backlog growth. Chinese NOCs deeper than expected capex cut is a strong headwind in the near term: while Iraq reported backlog looks high, execution might be a risk, as orders usually span multi years in that market, in our view.

  • •    ...in light of growing threat from SOEs. With SOE OFS competitors (e.g.. SSC. CNPC Oilfield Sen ice) looking to improve their own efficiencies and product offerings, we believe private sen ice providers will see more pressure in securing contracts.

  • •    Rapidly rising cost base. With major debt-funded investments into equipment and personnel in FY14, the company's cost base has inflated, in our view, and we believe margins will remain depressed in FY15/16.
