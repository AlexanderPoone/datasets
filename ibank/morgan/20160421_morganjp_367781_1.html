Lonking Holdings Ltd

Lifting PT to HK$1.80 on balance sheet resilience and anticipation of a better-than-expected recovery

Lonking’s share price is up 28% YTD, outperforming the HSCEI Index by 33%, and is the sector’s top performer. Additionally, Lonking’s share price has surged 56% from its mid-February trough (outperforming the index by 33%), driven partially by stronger-than-expected industry datapoints.

  • •    At the industry' level, C/E sales in March may have been inflated by pre-buying ahead of the emissions standard upgrade, effective 1 April, while improving new project start rates and end user utilization rates suggest a more sustainable recovery trajectory, in our view. China's loader sales jumped 30% Y/Y in March, with sales in 1Q16 edging up 2% Y Y. We expect the growth pace to soften in April and May, as part of the demand was front-loaded into March as producers offered discounts on China II machines ahead of the implementation of the new China III standaid. Lonking remains among our top picks to benefit from the cyclical upturn in the machinery sector. We see the company as well positioned to navigate the current challenging operating environment on effective cost controls and balance sheet health.

  • •    Factoring in the disappointing FY15 result, but upbeat guidance for FY16: Lonking’s FYI5 result was a big miss (30-40% below JPMe and consensus), as the company reported an unexpected loss in 2H15, driven by price cuts. Despite the earnings miss, guidance at the briefing was rather positive. Management noted a strong sales recovery at the industry level YTD, guiding for flat revenue for the industiy, but 10% revenue growth for Lonking for 2016.

  • •    We reduce EPS by 5-6% for FY16E/17E, but lift our Dec-16 DCF-based PT to HK$1.80. The FYI5 miss was driven by an unexpected 10% price cut for loaders undertaken at mid-2015. As a result, Lonking’s blended GPM slipped to 22.8% in 2015, down 2.8ppts Y/Y, driven solely by the GPM erosion for loaders (down 4ppts Y/Y). Management noted at the briefing that both pricing and volume for loaders have recovered visibly since the beginning of the year. Reflecting this, we raise our revenue growth assumption to 6% p.a., but reduce our blended GPM assumption by 2-3ppts for FY16/17, in addition to changes to A R provisions. Last, we make various adjustments, including working capital turns, and therefore lift our PT despite the mild earnings cut.
