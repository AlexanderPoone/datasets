Property inventory improving, but consumption weak

Investment activities mixed in May: (1) Improved: Home sale area rose 16%oya, compared to +8%oya in April. (2) Strong: Apparent oil demand (+10%oya). (3) Weak, but growing: IP (+6%oya), power demand (+2%oya) and power production (+l%oya). (4) The pace of contraction moderated in excavator sales (-33%oya), commercial vehicles sales (-8%oya), cement production (-6%oya) and heavy industry power demand (-l%oya). Total construction starts fell 12%oya in May, compared to the fall of 17%oya in April. YTD residential construction starts were down 18%oya. (5) Decreasing: Apparent steel demand (-6%oya) and CISA steel production (-2%oya).

Weak consumption: May’s passenger vehicle sales growth was surprisingly weak, at only l%oya, compared to 10%oya in January 2015. Retail sales stayed unchanged at 10%oya in May 2015.

Lower tax revenue and land sales: YTD government revenue is just+5%oya. More worryingly, revenue from land sales fell 40% in 5M15.

Money supply and credit growth improved modestly: China’s M2 money supply growth accelerated to 1 l%oya in May, compared to 10%oya in April. Bank deposits rose Rmb3tn (vs. Rmb873bn in April). Total social financing (stock) growth was 12% vs. 12% in April 2015.

Import growth was disappointing: Imports dropped 17.6%oya vs. -16.2%oya in April. Import volume of crude oil (-20.7% m/m sa), iron ore (- 7.9% m/m sa), copper (-6.7% m/m sa) and steel products (-7.9% m/m sa) decreased further.

Deflation is a risk: May’s CPI was 1.2%oya. PPI stayed unchanged in May, falling 4.6%oya, the 39th month of PPI deflation.
