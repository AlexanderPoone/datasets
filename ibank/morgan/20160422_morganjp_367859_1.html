BAIC’s IQ 16 result was a miss, as the bottom line nearly halved from Rmbl.6bn in 1Q15 to Rmb870mn in 1Q15, dragged down by the Hyundai JV and a high base effect. We downgrade BAIC to Underweight and cut our PT to HK$4.70 following a -30% earnings cut. Our top picks are GAC, BYD and Geel}’ in the China OEM space.

  • •    Recap of our investment thesis: (1) We like BAIC-Benz and expect robust volume growth of 23%/ll% in 2016/17 thanks mainly to the newly launched GLC SUV in late 2015 and a whole new E-class in Oct-16. (2) In contrast to Benz, we expect the Hyundai JV to see a volume decline (-5%) this year with margin contraction (from 8% in 2015 to 7% in 2016E). New capacity (150k units, or +14%) from late 2016 would add margin pressure when depreciation charges kick in. (3) Similarly, we are not optimistic about BAIC’s own brand venture and believe it will remain in losses in the next one to two years.

  • •    1Q16 results highlights: The bottom line dropped 47% as a result of: (1) a 46% decline in the Hyundai JV’s equity income - we estimate that the Hyundai JV’s net margin potentially contracted from 8% in IQ 15 to 5% in 1Q16 on 18% volume slippage; (2) an increase in selling expense from 7% of revenue in 1 QI 5 to 10% in 1Q16; and (3) a high base effect, as there was a Rmb400mn FX gain in 1H15, a majority of which was booked in 1Q15 due to sharp EUR depreciation. The J.P. Morgan global FX team expects EUR to strengthen 6% against Rmb by Dec-16.

  • •    Estimate revisions: We cut our earnings estimates by 32%/26% in 2016/17 to reflect the 1Q16 miss. We believe consensus estimates will follow, as we are now -30% below the Street.
