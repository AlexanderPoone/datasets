Key findings from rival’s interim results

  • •    China Glass (CG) is one of the largest glass companies in China with a focus on construction related glass. Ihl5 sales fell 17% Y/Y to Rmb948m due to a 7.4% decrease in volumes and a 9% reduction in average price. GPM fell to 7.2% in lhl5 which is about the same as the GPM of 8.7% for the float glass business of rival Xinyi Glass. China Glass management mentioned that the tough environment would likely continue into 2hl5. If conditions remain bleak in 2016, then the market consolidation with small players exiting the market should be even worse in 2016 than 2015, leaving the market in good shape when demand from construction market takes off. The company cited a chart that showed operating capacity in China falling 4% to 147,000 tons per day in China. The main reason for the falling capacity is due to the closure (permanently or for repairs) of capacity totaling 57k tons per day in lhl5 (up from 38k tone per day in 2014). China Glass has mstalled pollution control equipment on all of its production lines and believes that they are in compliance with all local regulations, even on its coal burning units. Cost per line vary but averages Rmb30m per line (about 5% or total cost of construction) but annual operating costs are minimal.

  • •    The key message from China Glass is that there are few signs of a recovery in demand yet. The drop in margins for float glass is not much of a surprise and probably won’t improve until a few months after we see positive growth in housing starts in China (-16.8% in YTD July Y/Y) which is not likely near term. Market consolidation will help but the absolute reduction in net total capacity is still low single digit (according to China Glass but also similarly viewed by Xinyi Glass management) so pricing power is unlikely to return to the construction related float glass market near term. The expansion of CG to Africa is a good sign for the market as this low cost producer is no longer expanding capacity in the oversupphed China market. The move by CG into solar PV cover glass is a long term threat. However, this modified line (prev. regular float glass) is probably less technically capable of making PV cover glass than the dedicated lines built to make solar glass that XYG operates. We understand that while CG can use its glass for PV cover glass, its focus is more for displays, furniture, appliances and other non solar applications. Producers like X YG that use natural gas as fuel have suffered a cost disadvantage (compared to coal based suppliers like CG) for over a year now but pending cuts in national gas prices should help XYG and take away some of the cost advantage that CG enjoyed. XYG and other high end suppliers that use natural gas will have some reprieve in their margins in 2016.
