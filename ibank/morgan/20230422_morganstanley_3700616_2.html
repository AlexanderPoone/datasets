Biding Time Til Buying Time

Timing the bull market in global sovereign bonds requires patience and a global perspective. We have been more cautious on duration in the euro area and the UK, while more constructive on duration in the US and Japan.

Investors remain unwilling to embrace duration, especially ahead of the Bank of Japan's (BoJ) decision this coming Friday (see Gov. Ueda's First MPM). Investors see ample reasons for the BoJ to remove Yield Curve Control (YCC).

And while investors outside of Japan have argued for a removal of YCC for most of the past 12 months, investors inside Japan now seem more supportive - and prepared - for the BoJ to move sooner (ideally) rather than Later.

The longer the BoJ waits-and-sees, the higher the chance that developments outside of Japan will thwart inflationary pressures within the country. The effect of recent developments in the US banking system remains a prime candidate.

In that context, investors remain highly attuned to indicators showing the economic impact of recent US banking stress. As such, the Friday release of the S&P Global Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI) sent rates higher, at least temporarily.
