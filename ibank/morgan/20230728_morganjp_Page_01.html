Hyundai Mobis

2Q23 OP in line; cost to stabilize, EV scale to kick in gradually - OW

Hyundai Mobis’ 2Q23 OP (W664B) came largely in line with our and Street expectations, with cost stabilization driving gradual margin improvement. While revenue grew solidly across all divisions, we are especially encouraged to see its A/S business continuously growing from last year’s high base. On the profit side, we are seeing cost deflation gradually kicking in at both the A/S and module businesses, which will likely continue throughout 2H23. Electrification revenue expands at a rapid pace, but OP rem ains slightly negative on ongoing investments. We note, however, that upon rapid revenue expansion, the R&D % of Electrification/Core parts revenue is gradually dissipating, an indication that R&D is now being well covered with scale. We expect a gradual turnaround in Electrification profits as HMC/Kia EV ramps up further. We maintain estimates broadly unchanged, with our PT raised slightly, to W290k (rolled to Jun-24); OW.

  • •    2Q OP in line. Revenue (W15.7T, +27% y-y) was a moderate beat. Module revenue grew 32% y-y, driven by HMC/Kia volume increases and mix gains. Electrification revenue was a strong beat, growing 78% y-y on the back of robustxEV volume growth atHMC/Kia(+37%y-y; BEV+64%y-y). A/S also delivered robust sales growth (+11% y-y), above our estimate, with solid demand continuing in all regions. OP was largely in line, at W664B, with OPM at 4.2%, and we continue to see cost pass-through efforts and stabilizing freight costs driving a gradual normalization in profit margins.

  • •    Module: Module sales grew 32% y-y, with robust growth seen across all divisions - Electrification (+78%), Core parts (+20%) and Module assembly (+19%). Top-line growth was driven by HMC/Kia’s rising volume in both ICE and xEVs, mix improvement, and continued cost pass-through efforts. OP turned to profit, but was still marginally above BEP (W96bn). We think an OP turnaround should com e in a m ore gradual m anner, with m ixed factors in play: growing investments (R&D and capex) vs. cost deflation, along with the pass-through of past inflation.

  • •    A/S: Revenue continued to print strongly, up 11% y-y and reaching a record high. We note that, after gaining market share from non-genuine players during CO VID-19 and freight disruption, Mobis still retains its strong top line without turning in the previous gains. OPM was more encouraging, at 21 %, as freight cost cuts gradually kick in. We see more room for cost stabilization going forward, driving A/S OPM to recover further, to c.22%.

  • •    Non-captive order and shareholder return updates. Mobis received $27. IB in orders in 1H; by region, $23B was from the U.S., $3.2B from China and $1.2B from Europe. Key products include Chassis and IVI product for U.S. major OEMs and AV orders for an EU OEM. Mobis announced interim DPS of W1K, along with share buyback/cancellation of 630k shares (0.7% stake) worth W150B. Management reiterated its urge for strengthened shareholder return.
