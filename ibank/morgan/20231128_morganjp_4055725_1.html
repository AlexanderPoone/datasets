Greater China 2024 outlook: The burdens of normality

Mainland China: Advancing with a heavy load

  • •    Government likely to keep growth target unchanged at “around 5%”. In our view this is challenging but achievable (JPM forecast 4.9%). Macro policy will need to be growth friendly to achieve the goal.

  • •    Budgetary fiscal deficit w'ill likely exceed the 3% implicit ceiling

  • •    PBOC expected to cut rate (-10bp) and RRR (-75bp in the next 12 months);

  • •    Trillion-yuan program to mitigate local government hidden debt stress and to stabilize the housing market

  • •    Breakdown of 2024 growlh: consumption should add 3.3%pts, investment 1.9%pts, and net exports -0.3%pt. Reopening dividend for consumption recovery has faded, but housing drag can be smaller

  • •    Deflation should end in 2024, but low inflation likely to stay

  • •    Restoring confidence and fixing policy distortions are critical to maximise sustainable growlh - recent progress on tliis front, in our view, has been limited

Hong Kong SAR: Growth returns to normal

  • •    Growlh expected to return to pre-pandemic pace as reopening boost fades

  • •    Domestic consumption recovery likely to remain incomplete

  • •    As the Fed is expected to cut rates in 2 H24, HK do liar is likely to stay away from the w'eak side in 2024

  • •    Fiscal consolidation with mild budget deficit in FY24/25

Taiwan: Tech sector-led recovery

  • •    Taiwan’s tech sector likely to stage steady recovery in 2024 while global economic growlh slows; global tech supply chain adjustments in progress

  • •    Post-COVID, domestic activity has largely normalized; expect moderate domestic demand growlh next year; look for GDP growlh at 3.7% in 2024

  • •    2024 CPI inflation to ease tow ards 1.9%yoy; central bank to keep policy rate on hold through 2024

  • •    On geopolitics, Taiwan’s upcoming January elections could have a significant impact on cross-Strait and US-China relations; ECFA is a near-term policy focus
