Trip.com (TCOM US & 9961 HK)

Improving outbound travel outlook for both beta and alpha; upgrade to OW

We turn more positive on Trip.com following the Politburo meeting that urged an increase in outbound flight supply. Our bullish view on Trip.com is based more on market share gains due to sustainable factors and less on a general outbound travel market recovery. We see structural growth opportunities in areas such as outbound travel and corporate travel, driven by accelerating market share gains, optimized supply chain and improving operating efficiency. In the short term, we believe pent-up demand offers upside risk to consensus estimates for 2Q/3Q23. In 2024, we expect market share gains in outbound travel market will lead to strong earnings growth. Upgrade to OW.

  • •    Outbound travel: outgrowing the market due to benign competition and traveler behavior change. We see accelerating market share gains in outbound travel market, evidenced by Trip.com’s volume recovery vs. industry average (Trip.com recovered to 40% of pre-COVID level in 1Q23 vs. industry at 15%). We believe the outperformance was driven by 1) an easing of industry competition, and 2) traveler mix shift to individual travelers from tourist groups. We believe both factors will last for 1-2 years.

  • •    Corporate travel: outperforming the market due to supply chain enhancement. Trip.com’s hotel supply chain continued to improve in the past three years despite the COVID impact, which gives Trip.com more selection and better rates (more access to big hotels’ corporate rates). We believe a stronger supply chain will allow Trip.com to gain market share in corporate travel.

  • •    Recovery on track with potential upside on outbound. According to CAAC (Civil Aviation Administration of China), domestic passenger volume in 2Q23 was 5% above 2019 level (vs. -10% in 1Q23) and June volume surpassed 2019 level by 8%. For outbound travel, passenger volume recovered to 3 7% of 2019 level in 2Q23 (44% in June) vs. 15% in 1Q23. We expect outbound flight supply to further recover in 2H23.

  • •    Estimate revisions. We revise up our 2023E topline/EPS by 2%/3%, due to strong domestic demand. We expect to see more financial benefit from an outbound travel recovery in 2024 and lift our 2024 revenue/EPS estimates by 6%/14%. Our 2024 adj EPS forecast is 16% above consensus.

  • •    Upgrade to OW, PT rises to USS50/HKS393. Our Jun-24 PT (rolled forward from Dec-23) is based on 20x 2024E P/E. Our target multiple is at a premium to domestic OTA peer Tongcheng (16x 2024E P/E), as we believe Trip.com as industry leader has clear competitive edges in user scale, technology and profitability, etc.
