Henderson Land Development

A positive move to diversify landbanking channels

Henderson Land (HLD) won the tender for a site at So Kwun Wat with satisfactoiy margin of 25%. We believe this is a good investment and a positive move by HLD to diversify its landbanking channels. That said, we believe there are limited catalysts ahead for the stock, with only launches of final phases of Double Cove and the smaller-scale old building redevelopment projects. Maintain Neutral with PT adjusted to HK$56.

  • •    Won government land tender again. HLD won the tender for a site at So Kwun Wat, Tuen Mun for HK$3.6bn. The site is next to SHKP’s Avignon and Wheelock’s site and close to Harrow Int’l School. The AV of HK$4,621 psf is 25% and 59% higher than Wheelock’s and Kerry’s sites, respectively, acquired two to three years ago. We believe HLD can provide 10-15 houses as well as apartments of 500-600 sf on average to satisfy demand in the area.

  • •    Decent project return. Given the sea view and close proximity to the international school, we believe the land price is reasonable. Assuming the project can be priced at a 50% premium over Avignon, at HKS 10.200 psf (GFA), we estimate that project IRR and margin can reach 13% and 25%, respectively. Also, as several sites were disposed in the area, this could generate critical mass to drive demand.

  • •    Old building acquisitions and farmland conversion remain the main landbanking sources. As HLD has been focusing on old building redevelopment and farmland conversion as the main sources of landbank replenishment in recent years, it has not been veiy active in government tenders. This is another winning bid since the Middle Road commercial site (Sep-14) and Sheung Shui site (Nov-13). While we believe HLD can opt to diversify locations outside those of the two main landbanking sources opportunistically when land price is reasonable, farmland conversion and old building redevelopment will still be the major channels. That said, we believe this is a positive step as the company moves to diversify from the two existing sources.

  • •    PT adjusted to HK$56. We have adjusted our PT to HK$56 to take into account the issue of bonus shares and the recent share price correction of HKCG (3 HK). Our target discount to NAV remains unchanged, at 24%.
