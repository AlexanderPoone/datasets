Li Ning Co Ltd (2331 HK)

1Q16 Offline SSSG flat; 4Q16 trade fair order growth slowed down to HSD

Li Ning has reported low-single-di git SSSG for I QI 6 and high-single-digit trade fair order growth for 4Q16. While reported SSSG for the overall platform remained broadly stable over low-single-digit increase in 4Q15. physical store (retail and wholesale) SSSG improved from negative growth in 4Q15 to broadly flat in I QI 6. Concurrently, trade fair order growth slowed down relative to the low-teens growth reported for 3Q16.

Li Ning reported low-single-digit SSSG for its overall platform for 1Q16. broadly stable over low-single-di git SSSG in 4Q15 and slower than mid-single-digit SSSG in 3Q15. During IQ 16. both the direct retail and the wholesale channel SSSG registered broadly flat growth, improving from negative mid-single-digit and negative low-single-digit trend, respectively. Phis is consistent with management commentary that I QI 6 SSSG saw “decent improvement”. Comparable sales on e-commerce virtual stores increased by low-sixties yoy-

  • •    Trade fair order growth up high-single digit, sequentially slower. Li Ning also reported high-single-digit trade fair order growth for 4Ql 6. 4Q16 order growth was driven by low-single-digit increase in footwear as well as low-teens increase in apparel. 4Q16 trade fair order growth is sequentially weaker than the low-teens trade fair order growth reported for 3Q16. We believe the slowdown in trade fair order growth partly reflects distributor conservatism following relatively weak 4Q15 and 1Q16.

  • •    Retain Neutral. We think this update is neutral to slightly negative. Although offline SSSG improved from negative to flat, trade fair order growth turned incrementally weaker. While Li Ning has finally achieved breakeven, many of the company’s performance metrics are not showing significant improvement. If anything, we think the trends are incrementally more negative. We believe Li Ning’s growth in the near term will be supported by growth in wholesale POS. However, turnarounds are not smooth and execution risks remain. We already expect the company to return to -8% EBIT margins over the longer term. We await more datapoints supporting progress.

Investment Thesis

While Li Ning has finally achieved breakeven, many of the company’s performance metrics are not showing significant improvement. If anything, we think the trends are incrementally more negative. We believe Li Ning’s growth in the near term will be supported by growth in wholesale POS. However, turnarounds are not smooth and execution risks remain. We already expect the company to return to -8% EBIT margins over the longer term. We await more datapoints supporting progress.

Valuation

Our Dec-16 PT HK$4.40 is based on a target P/E multiple of 14x our earnings forecasts for the year ending Dec-17 (when earnings forecasts are more normalized).

Risks to Rating and Price Target

Upside risks to our rating and price target include: (I) better-than-expected implementation of Li Ning's turnaround plan; (2) better-than-expected improvement in sales: (3) significant improvement with respect to gross margins; and (4) a significant reduction of the competitive environment for sportswear in China.

Downside risks include: (I) worse-than-expected implementation of the Li Ning turnaround plan; (2) a downturn in the Chinese sportswear market; and (3) increased competitive intensity in the sportswear industry in China.
