Turkish banks

For fall details and analysis on Turkish banks, please see our report here.

Recent 40% rally should not deter; with top down stabilizers in place, the bottom-up implies further 90% upside potential

With a move to more orthodox, policies and firm central bank action, we think now is the time to revisit Turkish Banks on a bottom-up basis. Despite a 40% rally in USD terms in the last month, if recent developments restore market confidence and act as a cost of equity stabilizer, then superior future earnings momentum and ROE build still look attractive for a sector that has underperformed CEEMEA Banks by 10% ytd.

How to Play

A more attractive market backdrop, with a move to more orthodox policies and recent firm action by the central bank, has lead Turkish risk assets to rally. Turkish Bank shares are up 40% in the last month in USD terms. Looking at the bigger picture, however, Turkish banks are still down 30% ytd (USD), underperforming MSCI CEEMEA Banks by 10%. If we assume that more policy credibility can restore confidence and act as a stabilizer for cost of equity, then we think Turkish Banks are worth a look on a bottom-up, fundamental basis.

Looking ahead: attractive on a three-year view

A key differentiator in Turkey is the ROE build in the next three years. Our big view across CEEMEA Banks is that we likely have passed peak returns, with 2022e ROTEs set to be lower for all the stocks we cover. The main exception is Turkish Banks, where we see 5%-pts higher ROTEs three years out, in large part due to the fact that provisioning was elevated in 2019 post the 2018 crisis, versus cyclically low cost of risk at the majority of other stocks we cover.
