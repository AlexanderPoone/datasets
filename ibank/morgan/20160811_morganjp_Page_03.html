Results beat expectations

Wharf reported 1H16 underlying earnings of HK$6bn, 15% higher than our estimate. The beat was mainly from a higher-than-expected contribution from China development property as margins improved HoH and from Hong Kong rental income. The announced 5.5% increase in DPS is in line with our estimate. We believe the results do not contain any negative surprises and we see the Hong Kong retail rental as more resilient than expected.

HK IP-still growing H/H

HK retail rental revenue grew on an HoH basis; retail sales decline narrowed in

2Q

Harbor City and Times Square retail rental revenue increased by 5% and 6% HoH respectively. This is in contrast with Hongkong Land’s Landmark retail revenue, down 3% HoH, and our expectation of -4% HoH. The resilience in retail rental revenue should have been supported by the stable turnover rents on an HoH basis, which have held up surprisingly well.
