Overview

We recommend Tier 2 bonds and only select ATI and non-bank financials. Asia financials have returned 4.8% YTD, with ATI outperforming, returning 5.8%. The sector’s short duration of only 2.6 years provided some insulation against rate moves; spread tightening was modest at lObp. We think the sector can tighten modestly due to stable fundamentals and supportive technicals. Tier 2 bonds across the region look attractive compared to senior bonds. Valuations for ATI are in many cases closer to fair, leaving this part of the capital structure vulnerable to decompression; we are mindful of the tail risks of this instrument and look for better compensation. We are also happy to step back after Asia ATI outperformed, returning 5.8% by mid-November.

Recommendations

Among banks, we prefer Tier 2 over other parts of the capital structure. We generally prefer highly-rated jurisdictions but also look at higher-yielding options in Thailand. Senior bonds offer too little room for tightening in most regions, and fundamental risks keep us from chasing. 2023 reminded us of the weaknesses of ATI structures and while we hesitate to extrapolate developments in other regions to EM Asia, we prefer to add in this asset class when the spread to either Tier 2 or senior is wider than the recent average.
