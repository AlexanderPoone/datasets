Improving profitability on scalable cost structure and expanding network

Operating leverage is inherent in both theatre and films businesses as: 1) costs are substantially fixed and front loaded; and 2) the recurring box office sharing demands minimal variable costs, therefore improving the profitability of the company.

  • •    The film business is largely scalable because the costs are mainly comprised of the conversion costs and the amount is fixed (US$150-200k for a Hollywood film and cUS$200k for a Chinese one). The box office revenue generated by every additional theatre should lead to an increase in operating profit for IMAX without a proportionate increase in variable costs.



  • •    Hybrid revenue-sharing arrangement of theatre business: The main cost component of the hybrid revenue-sharing arrangement is the procurement and installation costs upon installation in the first year. Revenue contribution happens on a recurring basis through sharing 8-10% of box office revenue with minimal variable costs. Therefore, a screen’s gross profit significantly improves after Year 1.
