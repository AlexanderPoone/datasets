74% upside with Strong Commitment to orthodox policies vs. 29% downside in a Modest Return to orthodoxy

We sketch two main scenarios for Turkish equities based on the post-election policy environment, in-line with our FICC research team’s outlook: The Strong Commitment to Orthodoxy where we see 74% upside to current levels and the Modest Return to Orthodoxy where we see 29% downside. There are three key variables in this model: 1) FX depreciation; 2) earnings growth; and 3) the re/de-rating in the PE.


In the Strong Commitment to Orthodoxy’ scenario, we expect 74% upside based on those three factors: FX, Earnings, and rerating:

  • •    In FX, we use our Strategy’ team’s forecast that the FX weakens to TRY 25, about 25% lower from here driven by preelection stimulus exacerbating imbalances, pent-up FX demand from corporates, and the central bank also likely aiming to rebuild FX reserves.

  • •    For earnings growth, we assume 10% earnings growth in nominal TRY - not much in the context of mid-40s% inflation. We are trying to be conservative.

  • •    Re-rating is the key driver of the upside. We expect a sharp re-rating of equities from 4.6x 12m fwd PE in the nonfinancials to 9.2x, or the median PE over the last 15 years. We look at the non-financials/exclude the banks since the macroprudential measures have obscured the underlying bank earnings and depressed their multiple (now c. 2x 12m fwd consensus).
