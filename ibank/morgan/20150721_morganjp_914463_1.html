Kerry Logistics Network

Quality play on Greater China/Asia's growing and increasingly complex logistics demand; initiate at OW

Initiate on KLN with OW, Jun-16 PT HK$14. KLN provides extensive logistics services in 39 countries/territories. It has the largest distribution network and hub operations in Greater China & ASEAN, which contribute 67% & 15% of revenue.

  • •    Profitability ranks above global sector, driven by KLN’s capability to provide more extensive, higher value-added and differentiated logistics services. Customer base is well-diversified across industries and sticky. KLN also has valuable logistics space assets (a unique trait in an asset-light sector). We expect core profit to grow 24% in FY15E and 11 % in FY16-17E, driven by increased volume and higher margins from value-added services. Our forecasts have not factored in any acquisitions or investment property fair value gains which could provide upside risk to our estimates and fuel KLN’s re-rating.

  • •    Well-positioned to leverage on Greater China/Asia’s growing and increasingly complex logistics demand: Improving trade links, rising consumption and E-commerce and increased complexity of supply chains will drive more companies to outsource their logistics services to improve cost efficiency and KLN is well-positioned to leverage on this given its strong track record. Established cross-border trucking system KART will also benefit from China-ASEAN’s enhanced trade links and China's OBOR strategy longer term.

  • •    Significant upside considering its historical/sector valuations and future growth: KLN’s recent share price correction is overdone. Valuations are at 14%/15% discount to their historical average/global sector. This is unjustified given KLN’s higher profitability, strong B/S and favorable LT growth prospects. Key positive catalysts: 1) Rising intra-Asia trade and logistics demand plus E-commerce penetration. 2) More companies looking to outsource their logistics services to improve cost efficiency. 3) Trade liberalization. 4) China's One-Belt-One-Road strategy, driving increased cargo flow between China and ASEAN/rest of the world. 5) Value-accretive acquisitions.

Downside risks: 1) International freight forwarding expansion may result in stronger revenue growth but lower profit margins. 2) Excessive industry competition. 3) Value-dilutive acquisitions and integration challenges.
