We look for the manufacturing PMI’s headline composite to slip 0.3pt to 46.0 in the flash July report. Regional surveys point to a larger decline on the month but also imply a level that has been running above the actual PMI reading for several months. The notable drop in new orders last month—to just above its current cycle low—also suggests that the near-term weakness in the PMI is likely to persist. While we see some scope for a manufacturing rebound as demand holds up and inventories are drawn down, that is not yet apparent in the data.

We anticipate that new single-family home sales fell 8.3% to 700,000 saar units in June. This decline follows an unexpected 12.2% surge in May to 763,000 units. Recall that this series is very noisy and prone to revisions, and as such we tend to focus on other housing market indicators. Mortgage purchase applications continued to trend down for June following their bigger move down in May as mortgage rates again tended to trend higher. Even so, if realized our forecast would put new home sales at their highest level (other than May’s surge) since March 2022.

We look for initial jobless claims to have moved up 12,000 to 240,000 for the week ending July 22. Initial claims have been very volatile over the past several weeks, and the prospects of shutdowns for annual auto sector retooling are adding to the volatility. We anticipate that claims should trend up as the labor market cools further, but there is relatively limited evidence that process is currently underway. Continuing claims, for the week ending July 8, picked up to a four-week high after steadily slowing for the past few months.
