Our top picks in Turkish Banks

Whilst we see more upside at Turkish state banks, we continue to like Garanti and Yapi Kredi for solid returns momentum, balance sheet quality and good capitalization.

Garanti: defensive balance sheet, quality stock

A characteristic of the Turkish banking sector is that fully discretionary ‘free’ provisions continue to be set aside. For the 9M20, the five banks we cover have made free provisions equal to 16-58% of pre-tax profits. This is highest at Garanti, which has set aside TR4.3bn of free provisions compared to its TRY7.4bn PBT result, and the cumulative stock of provisions is now equal to 7% of TN AV, 100bps of RWAs in capital terms and 11% of the stock’s market cap. Garanti shares trade at 0.4x 2022e NAV, which compares to our fair value at 0.8x, hence we see 63% upside potential.

Yapi Kredi: levers at hand to re-rate ROEs most of private sector peers

Yapi Kredi’s pre-COVID-19 Return on Assets of 90bps lags private sector peers by 1/3^. Given the strategic levers we expect management to pull, we see it having the largest expansion of the pack, by 70bps to 2022e versus Akbank / Garanti at 50bps. Costs have provided a much-needed leg up in returns, and by 2022e we expect Yapi Kredi's cost-asset ratio to further close the gap on industry-leading Akbank, whereas management's focus on optimizing and diversifying the fee base provides an additional underpinning. Whilst Yapi Kredi shares have outperformed Turkish bank peers, we note that foreign ownership at c.20% remains materially lower than peers and the stock’s historical levels, which could provide technical support going forward. At 0.4x 2022e TNAV versus our fair value of 0.8 5x, we see 82% potential upside vs most of its private sector peers.
