Investment Thesis, Valuation and Risks

Array Technologies Inc (Overweight Price Target: $32.00)

Investment Thesis

Array is a leading supplier of follow-the-sun single-axis trackers used in utility-scale solar farms. The company has deployed more than 21GWs of trackers and is a leading supplier of trackers in the United States. Investors are presented with the opportunity to invest in a solidly profitable solar pure play that has multiple vectors of growth ahead: solar panel shipment growth, higher tracker attach rates, first-time international expansion, and extension of the product portfolio into new categones within the solar balance-of-systems.

Valuation

Maintain Overweight. ABRY trades at ~15x our F Y22E EV/EBITDA, a discount to the average multiple for peers. We think the multiple is attractive owing to prospects of nearly 20% EBITDA growth through 2023. Our Dec 22 pnce target is $32, based on an assigned multiple of 20x FY23E EVZEBITDA. The assigned multiple is a slight discount to forecast PF EBITDA growth, consistent with peers.

Risks to Rating and Price Target

Our pnce target and rating for ARRY could be at risk to the downside for any of the following reasons:

Competition could weigh on ASPs and gross margins relative to our expectations. Large utilities have enormous purchasing power and call the shots on many of the larger projects, potentially weighing on ASPs and gross margins. Growth might moderate when the tracker market tops 80% attach-rate for new utility-scale projects. The solar industry has experienced boom-bust cycles as a result of policy gyrations, tariffs, and business cycles, though this risk is attenuating somewhat as solar goes firmly into the money in the wholesale energy markets. US revenues could be impacted by changes to the ITC, which is scheduled to step down in 2024, and the end of associated inventory safe harboring. In the year ended 2020, Array’s largest customer accounted for 11% of total revenues, and the top five customers accounted for 41 %. Loss of a key customer could therefore be a negative. Equipment failure(s) could reflect poorly on the brand, were they to occur, and weigh on the stock multiple. The company’s end markets, systems deployment, supply-chain, and manufacturing activities could be disrupted by the COVID-19 pandemic.
