  • •    Debate has shifted to when the BoE will cut. We think this is u nlikely in 1H24, absent a recession, due to persistent wage and services price inflation.

  • •    While the labor market has loosened, evidence of structural damage and low potential growth have left wage growth running higher than several of these indicators alone would suggest

  • •    We look for growth to briefly accelerate into 2024 as falling inflation causes consumers to regain some confidence and spend a greater fraction of gains in their real incomes

  • •    This could slow the pace of labour market loosening, keep innation high and fu rther limit the scope for cuts

  • •    But we expect this boost to real incomes to fade, and the lagged impact of past tightening to catch up, triggering a behavioral response from businesses and households later in the year that results in at least a mild recession
