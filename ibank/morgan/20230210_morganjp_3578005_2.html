Interpreting the January credit report

In aggregate, our estimates of credit impulse (the gap between TSF growth and nominal GDP growth) increased modestly, to 4.9%-pt in January (vs. 4.4%-pt in December), the highest since March 2021.

Regarding a further breakdown in bank loan data, it is worth highlighting that medium- and long-term loans to the corporate sector spiked 3.55tn yuan in January (compared to 1.21 Itn yuan in December and 2.1 tn yuan in January 2022). Indeed, while the increase in medium- and long-term loans to corporates typically picks up momentum in January (averaging 1.93tn yuan in the past three years), thegrowth this time around was particularly strong, suggesting accelerating credit support for corporate sector investment (including increasing credit support for developers). 2
