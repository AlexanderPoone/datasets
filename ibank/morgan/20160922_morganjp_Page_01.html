Lee & Man Paper Manufacturing

Lifting PT to HK$10.1 - positive margin implications of rival's results

Lee & Man Paper (LMP) is the second-largest producer of containerboards in China. We lift our pricing and margin expectations for LMP following strong pricing signals arising from the latest NDP results and post results briefing comments. We prefer NDP over LMP in the containerboard space as NDP has lower margins and hence more room for upside surprise should the containerboard market move further into a tight market.

  • •    Key findings from rival NDP results. NDP’s latest plamied price hike in Oct leads us to be more positive on LMP (usually similar to NDP in pricing strategy); we now assume 2HFY16 net profit margin to improve by 5% H/H to HK$550 per ton (Rmb470 per ton). Longer term, we see better supply discipline in the market (neitlier LMP nor NDP starting new lines in 2016) as a good signal for the market and now assume a further Y/Y increase of 6% for FY17 to HK$572 per ton and 4% Y/Y increase to HK$594 per ton for FYI8. We assume peak NP/T in FYI8 and a small correction by 4-5% in FYI9 as more supply comes on line.

  • •    Lifting margins on stronger demand and better supply discipline.

We lift FY16 NP by 3% and FY17 NP by 8% due to the higher GPM assumptions for containerboard margins. Our revised EPS estimates are 7% and 12% higher than consensus estimates for FY16 and FY17, respectively. We lift our Dec-17 DCF-based PT to HK$10.1 per share (previously HK$9.3).
