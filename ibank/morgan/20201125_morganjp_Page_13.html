Turkish Conglomerates

Offer better value vs subsidiaries

Against a backdrop of improving investor sentiment on Turkey, we think there is now grounds for Koc and Sabanci to attract investors as value plays given the diversified nature of their NAVs. Both companies trade at a rare large discounts to their listed NAV, which is 22% at Koc and much wider at 36% for Sabanci (CRFSA inflated mcap is adjusted, otherwise 43% discount). Our past experience suggests a stabilized macro environment delivers an opportunity for Koc to trade at a premium to its listed NAV, while the discount for Sabanci narrows to 18-20%.




Comparing two peers

Koc Holding has the unique appeal of offering one-stop investment exposure to the fast-growing sectors of the economy such as autos, household appliances, energy and banking. Key catalysts that will continue to enhance its NAV are: 1) growth shifting to exports in autos; 2) solid outlook for household appliances post pandemic;

  • 3)    increasing mobility to help energy revenues; 4) a significantly undervalued banking sector; and 5) an improving cash position with the help of reintroduction/increase of dividend stream from subsidiaries.
