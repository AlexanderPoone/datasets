Anton Oilfield Services Group (3337 HK)

Decline in 1Q16 revenue despite record backlog; Iraq risks from contract renegotiations and asset stake sale

Anton's backlog-derived 1Q16 revenue fell 2% yoy (overseas revenue dropped 36% yoy), although the reported backlog itself reached a record high. We continue to advise caution when analyzing Anton's backlog given its longer execution. Furthermore, we see risks in its Iraq business due to the current contract renegotiations and intention to sell 40%' of subsidiary assets. We still maintain UW on the stock.

  • •    1Q16 decline despite record backlog. Anton reported RMB3.5bn backlog at end March (RMB0.8 domestic and RMB2.7 overseas) and RMB 1 .Obn new contracts signed in IQ 16 (0.2bn domestic and 0.8bn overseas). The company’s reported backlog numbers are at a record high. The derived IQ 16 revenue (from backlog and new contracts data) reached RMB226mn, 2% lower than RMB230mn in IQ 15.

  • •    Continue to advise caution on backlog analysis. As discussed before (see note), we advise caution when using Anton's seemingly high backlog to forecast growth. Although the RMB3.5bn backlog seems high enough to cover the annual revenue (Anton 2015 revenue at RMB1.8bn), we understand 78% of the backlog is from overseas markets, where contract execution may span 2-3 years and is hence uncertain. This has been reflected in its repeated below-consensus revenue recognition and write-downs in backlog balance.

  • •    New risks from Iraq on contract renegotiations and asset sale. We see further risks from Anton's Iraq market momentum (75% of overseas total revenue, see note), because 1) from our recent Middle East trip we understand the Iraqi government is seeking to renegotiate on terms of some contract renewals with the developers/service provider, and we think this may cause certain delays on some projects and will also likely put pressure on the service pricing; 2) Anton's proposed plans to sell 40%' of its Iraq subsidiary to China HBP would apparently weaken the EPS contribution from the Middle East market going forward, which management had expected to be the main growth driver in the near term, although the transaction would likely ease its stretched balance sheet in 2016.

  • •    Questions for analyst briefing on 23 April. We suggest the following major questions for Anton's analyst briefing on 23 April (9-10AM, HKT, dial in details: +852-2112 1700/PIN 1725022#): I) Over what period does management plan to execute the company’s overseas contracts, and will there be risks of backlog write-downs? 2) Why has there been a major yoy decline in overseas revenue and increase in domestic revenue in I QI 6? 3) Are there any further details you can disclose regarding the Iraq asset sale to China HBP? Are there any restrictive terms that may be potentially unfavorable to Anton? 4) Can you elaborate on the contract renegotiations in Iraq?
