AAC Technologies Holdings

Margin pressure to continue

3Q13 result beat slightly after one-offs, but gross margin weak 3Q13 revenue rose 8% qoq, in line with company guidance but 1.8% below JPM estimates. Higher ASP contributed to most of the top-line growth. Gross margin was down 35bps qoq at 42.5%. Excluding one-offs such as disposal of interest in associates, net fair value gain on available-for-sale investments, EPS grew 11% qoq, 3.5% higher than JPM estimates largely due to lower opex-to-sales ratio.

4Q13 gross margin likely weaker on the margin. For 4Q13, management guided for revenue growth in the teens and modestly lower GPM, both on a sequential basis. AAC’s margin guidance is likely disappointing to the Street but in line with our expectation. GoerTek (002241 CH, not covered), a rising local competitor, will enjoy 5 3% /46% revenue/eamings growth in 4Q13 on a qoq basis, according to Bloomberg consensus. GoerTek now supplies speaker box and other acoustic components for the iPhone and iPad.

Non-acoustic business set to rise in importance, but margin dilution possible. The company reiterated its commitment to return to long-term earnings CAGR of 30% by beefing up the non-acoustic business (RF solutions, camera, internal structure and others). In 2014, non-acoustic business is on track to contribute at least 10% of total revenue, vs. high single digits in 2013. Management believes that while there could be some short-term GPM pressure, blended GPM could be maintained at 40%+.
