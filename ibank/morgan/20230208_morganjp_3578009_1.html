USDA World Ag Supply & Demand Estimates (WASDE)

US Soybean Ending Stocks Above Consensus

Today, the USDA updated its global supply and demand estimates for 2022/23. In the US, 2022/23 ending stocks are estimated at: 1.267B bu of corn (vs. consensus 1.273B bu); 225MM bu of soybeans (vs. consensus 211MM bu); and 568MM bu of wheat (vs. consensus 575MM bu). US stocks-to-use (STU) are projected at 9.1% for corn (vs. 9.2% in 2021/22), 5.2% for soybeans (vs. 6.1% in 2 021/22), and 2 9.9% for wheat (vs. 3 7.0% in 2021/22). We view today’s report as a slight incremental negative for LIS farmers to the extent above-consensus soybean ending stocks weighs on prices.

  • •    US soybean ending stocks above consensus. 2022/23 US corn supply is estimated at 15.157B bu on a yield of 173.3bu/acre and 79.2MM harvested acres; demand is lowered to 13.890B bu (vs. prior 13.915B bu) on 25MM bu lower ethanol use. Corn ending stocks of 1.267B bu (vs. prior 1.242B bu) are slightly below consensus 1.273B bu. US soybean supply is estimated at 4.566B bu on a yield of49.5bu/acre and 86.3MM harvested acres; demand is lowered to 4.340B bu (vs. prior 4.3 55B bu) on 15MM lower crushings. Soybean ending stocks of 225MM bu (vs. prior210MM bu) are above consensus 211MM bu. For US wheat, supply is estimated at 2.468B bu on a yield of 46.5bu/acre and 35.5MM harvested acres; demand is slightly lowered to 1.900B bu (vs. prior 1.901 B bu) on 2MM bu lower food use partially offset by 1MM bu higher seed use. Wheat ending stocks of 568MM bu (vs. prior 567MM bu) are slightly below consensus 575MM bu.

  • •    US farm gate prices raised by $0.10 for soybeans and lowered by $0.10 for wheat. 2022/23 US farm price projections include $6.70 corn (vs. $6.00 in 2 021/22); $14.3 0 soybeans (vs. $13.3 0 in 2 021/22); and $9.00 wheat (vs. $7.63 in 2021/22). Including direct government payments, cash receipts horn the three major crops are projected at $168.0B (JPMe) in 2022/23 (vs. prior $167.7B), up 3% YoY.

  • •    Global stocks of major crops forecast down 2% YoY in 2022/23. Global STU for 2022/23 is estimated at 25.4% for corn (unchanged), 26.9% for beans (vs. prior 27.0%), and 34.0% for wheat (unchanged). As expected, Argentina soybean prod uction was revised lower to down -7% to 41.0MMT (vs. prior up -4% to 45.5MMT). Meanwhile, Ukraine corn exports were again revised upwards to down -17% to 22.5MMT (vs. prior down -24% to 20.5MMT). Combined 2022/23 global ending stocks of the three major crops are projected to be down 2% YoY to 667MMT vs. 665MMT consensus and 668MMT last month.
