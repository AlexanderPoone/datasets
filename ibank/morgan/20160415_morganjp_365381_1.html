China: 1Q GDP growth printed at 6.7%oya, with details stronger than expected

China’s 1Q16 GDP growth eased slightly to 6.7%oya (J.P. Morgan: 6.8%; consensus: 6.7%), compared to 6.8%oya in 4Q15. Our seasonal-adjustment process suggests that real GDP growth, in sequential terms, eased to 6.0% q/q saar in IQ 16 (J.P. Morgan forecast: 6.3% q/q saar), compared to 6.6% q/q, saar in 4Q15. Meanwhile, in nominal terms, 1Q16 GDP rose 7.2%oya compared to 6.0%oya in 4Q15.

While 1Q16 GDP came in broadly in line with expectations, details of the March activity data came in stronger than expected. Both industrial production and fixed investment came in stronger than expected, while retail sales were in line with our expectation.
