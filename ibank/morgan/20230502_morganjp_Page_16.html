Long-term trends in Turkiye: Mean reversion or trend following?

(II)

The fall in the stock market index is matched by the gradual declines in market cap/GDP particularly in the 2013-19 period.

The noisiness in the time series in ‘20 is related to the COVID-driven drop in GDP. The rise at the end is the big stock market rally in ‘22.

The jump in trading volumes and in market velocity’ (volume divided by market cap) is significant, up 4-6x in the last two years versus the norms of the ‘10s. We think this is a function of the high inflation/ low real rates driving local investors out of bonds/bank deposit into equities. Turkiye sticks out in an international context: Turkiye is 40% of M SCI EME A single stock volume and its busiest market by far, but only 5% of the index weight.
