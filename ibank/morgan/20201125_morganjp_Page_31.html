Risks to Rating and Price Target

Key risks to our estimates, rating and PT on Sabanci Holding arise mainly from 1) lower-than-expected Turkish economic growth, higher-than-expected inflation/ interest rate pressure, significant and sustained depreciation in TRY; and 2) execution risk on divestments/acquisitions/IPOs and other corporate actions in Sabanci Holding and its portfolio companies. Regulatory risks are also material in banking and tobacco investments of Sabanci Holding. Any potential sale of shares (of the holding or portfolio companies) from controlling family members, or intention to sell, could negatively affect valuations. 3) Continued pressure in the banking sector as well as sudden erosion in NPLs.

While we are comfortable from the corporate governance angle and the Sabanci family has in the past held a clean corporate governance record, some corporate governance risks always remain associated with holding company structures; some examples of this in Sabanci are the non-availability of full financial details of the key unlisted companies within the portfolio or a possibility of majority shareholder’s (Sabanci family members’) interests not being fully aligned with those of minorities.
