Investment Thesis

Cheung Kong Infrastructure (CKI) is an infrastructure holding company with assets located in Hong Kong, China, Australia, New Zealand, Canada and the UK. CKI is also the parent company of Power Assets Holdings and has a 38.87% stake in the company. CKI was listed in 1996, when Cheung Kong Holdings spun off its infrastructure assets to form CKI. CK Hutchison holds 76%+ of CKI. Most of CKI’s assets are regulated utility businesses in gas, power and water. It also owns toll roads in China and an infrastructure materials business in Hong Kong/China.

We believe upside for CKI could come from acquisitions of utilities and infrastructure assets in Australia and other developed markets.

Downside could come from weakening of GBP/HKD and lack of new acquisitions or special DPS from PAH.

Valuation

Risks to Rating and Price Target

Downside risks include: 1) share placement, which could dilute EPS; 2) a strengthening of the USD/HKD against the GBP and AUD, which would lower earnings from overseas segments; 3) higher borrowing costs, which would result in lower net earnings; and 4) slower inflation growth in the UK and Australia, which would result in lower revenue for the regulated utilities assets in those countries.

Upside risks include: 1) acquisitions of new projects; 2) better-than-expected efficiency gains from the UK utility assets; and 3) potential merger with Power Asset, which could help CKI reduce its gearing for future acquisitions.
