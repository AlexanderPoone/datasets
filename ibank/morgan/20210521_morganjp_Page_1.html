Where crypto meets FRA/OIS

Tracking CP demand from stablecoin reserves


• Stablecoins constitute a key component of cryptocurrency market infrastructure, and support the majority of turnover in major tokens like BTC and ETH ...


...and the increase in activity more broadly, particularly in offshore exchanges without direct access to the U.S. banking system, has helped drive a more than three-fold increase in their market capitalization this year


• At nearly $85bn as of this writing, more than two-thirds of which is Tether, reserve funds for USD-linked asset-backed tokens are potentially significant money market investors—comparable to the largest prime money market funds


• Recent disclosure from Tether suggests half their assets are in commercial paper, which would make them a larger holder than most prime funds as well as cash-rich technology companies (e.g, AAPL, GOOG, AMZN, MSFT and FB)


• Tether may be somewhat more limited than other issuers in their ability to access bank deposits, but there are reasons to believe that others may be active in money markets as well


• If anything the collapse in BTC, ETH, and other prices has benefited stablecoins as a relative safe haven


• The rise of Facebook-backed Diem (the successor to the Libra project) is another avenue for potential growth


• These investors are likely somewhat price insensitive and could constitute a support for tight FRA/OIS spreads going forward
