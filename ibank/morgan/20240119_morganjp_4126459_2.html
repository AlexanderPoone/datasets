In the swaps market, spreads are mostly wider despite heavy high grade financial issuance (which tends to get swapped) in recent weeks. These moves are broadly consistent with our expectations - indeed, two weeks ago we shifted to a widening bias on swap spreads in the belly despite the near term potential for issuance related swapping.

As we lookahead, we think there is more to go and maintain on r widening bias on swap spreads in intermediate matu rities, for the following reasons. First, from a tactical standpoint, the seasonal headwind from swapped issuance is likely now mostly behind us. As seen in Figure 4, high grade financial issuance tends to be heaviest in the first three weeks of January, and the heavier-than-usual issuance in the second week this time suggests that most of this issuance is by now behind us. Second, the odds continue to favor a taper-down of QT, as we noted recently (see It’s all over now, QT, J. Barry et al., 1/12/2024).
