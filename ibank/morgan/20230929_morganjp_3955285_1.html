Despite a relatively light data calendar this week, the Treasury yield curve continued to aggressively bear steepen, with 30-year Treasury yields rising 18bp to 4.71%—a 50bp MTD rise (Figure 1). The S&P 500 is down nearly 5% over the same period, though high grade credit spreads and current-coupon MBS spreads are tighter. The UAW strike enters its third week, with the strike expanding to additional GM and Ford plants, and the US government appears headed for a shutdown, if a late-breaking deal is not passed by Saturday night. In terms of the economic effects, we think a short-lived government shutdown would have only marginal impact on overall GDP, though important data releases would likely be delayed (see The economic data vs. a government shutdown, D. Silver & P. White, 9/20/23). The disruption to the data flow could exacerbate policy uncertainty in the coming days. If it’s not delayed, we expect next week’s employment report to show a 175K increase in nonfarm payrolls and a decline in the unemployment rate (see Economics).
