Big Data and Al Strategies

Assessing a range of NLP Transformer Models such as BERT, Electra, Funnel, GPT2, MPNetand variants

Natural Language Understanding Models are reaching and exceeding human levels of understanding (according to SuperGLUE). We can leverage this in Equity Investing as well. In this report, we compare the performance of 17 state of the art Natural Language Processing models on a financial dataset consisting of headlines and sentiment labels.

  • •    Funnel had the best accuracy of 83.9% on average. It is designed like BERT, but with a funneling layer like a CNN helping compress the model. It was not fast to setup and train, but one of the faster top performing models in inference mode (under 4 seconds per 1000 sentences). Best used with a GPU.

  • •    MPNet came a close second with an average accuracy of 83.7%, with a much smaller ‘Masked and Permuted neural Net’ model. MPNet was one of the fastest models, especially considering the high accuracy, and also showed acceptable speed on CPU.

  • •    Electra was the fastest model taking a fraction of a second per 1000 sentences scored, while maintaining reasonable accuracy of 76.9%. This smaller (14Mn Parameters) GAN based model was also quick to setup and train, even on CPU.

Model training and testing was on an internal JPM dataset; the J.P. Morgan Headline Sentiment dataset consists of 4,298 news headlines which have been manually assigned a sentiment label drawn from: pos, neg, neut, or mixed . Please contact us for access to this data.

A further model evaluation was run on the Financial Phrase Bank Labelled Data, published by Malo et al. (2014). AU 8434 rows were used to evaluate the models using pos, neg, neut labels.
