Anta Sports Products Ltd.

Investor event relatively underwhelming


Neutral

2020.HK, 2 020 HK

Price: HK$20.75


Price Target: HKS20.30



We attended Anta Investor Event and China Sporting Goods Industry Summit 2016 in Fuzhou on April 21. Overall, we believe near-term catalysts are limited and there may be downside risk to the share price following the recent rally.

  • •    Decente trial launch in 2H16. Anta contributed RMB 150m and held 60% of the Decente JV interest. There is an undisclosed licensing fee associated with distributing products in China. Anta plans to launch Decente in 2H16 first in the North East in similar locations as Fila’s. Initially products will be imported from Korea and Japan and gradually move to in-house production around FYI8. Anta aims to achieve similar EBIT margin (-15% currently) and sales per store (~RMB4m per store p.a.) as Fila’s once the Decente brand matures.

  • •    Rio 2016 Olympics social network + ecommerce marketing plan. Dialing back on traditional media investment, Anta’s Olympics marketing strategy centers around the combination of social network + sponsorship resources + ecommerce. More broadly Anta plans to shift towaid digital marketing and use savings from less traditional media investment to fund more content creation (eg. sporting events and trendy products).

  • •    Other takeaways. (1) Anta is primarily interested in acquiring high-end relatively niche international brands with strong branding in attractive sport segments such as outdoor, (2) Anta expects ecommerce penetration to reach low-teens level in FY16 (v HSD in FY15); (3) Anta expects gross and operating margins to remain broadly stable in FY16 albeit with slight tick-up in A&P; (4) Capex guidance RMB200-300m p.a. excluding investment in the distribution center (which will cost RMB900-1,000m over multiple years in addition to land cost of-RMB200m).

  • •    Retain Neutral. Despite a rally since April 1 2016, we believe the Investor Day update was relatively underwhelming and there may be downside risk to the share price in the near-term. Anta is currently benefiting from improving momentum and its short- and medium-term performance is being supported by its other brands business. However, consensus forecasts are already factoring in high operating margins into perpetuity and strong earnings growth over the coming years. With consensus expectations so high, we see limited risk of substantial upside in the current share price.
