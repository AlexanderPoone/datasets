Macau Gaming

Recovery rally maturing - time to be selective.

Downgrade Sands China to Neutral

  • •    Recovery rally is maturing, which has been driven by ‘diminishing negatives’ and stabilizing demand. The next stage of the rally requires profits to grow and estimates to be upgraded, in our view. The good news is, we think these will happen starting July/August, driven by 2Q16 earnings results (likely the first yoy turnaround in sector EBITDA in eight quarters), successfill opening of Wynn Palace, and positive GGR growth - both on qoq and yoy terms - from 3Q onwards. The bad news, however, is the stocks may take a breather until then, as investors could be tempted to lock in profits here - understandably so -given risk/reward post +c50% rally since January’s trough (vs. HSI +cl5%).

  • •    Admittedly, near-term risk/reward looks ‘less-than-perfect’. Recall also that 2Q is seasonally the slowest quarter for Macau; thus, a positive surprise in demand is hand to come by (usually happens during holiday/peak seasons), while sequential GGR momentum will likely turn negative (albeit temporarily) from the stronger-than-expected first quarter. We cannot say valuations are ‘cheap’ anymore either, as the rally has moved the sector’s EV/EBITDA to 12.7x on 2016E or 11.8x on 2017E (though still below historical average of 14x).

  • •    But remember - Macau stock setups will turn stronger again over the next 6-18 months, in our view, given: (1) year-over-year turnaround in 2Q EBITDA (reporting in Jul/Aug) and 3Q GGR, followed by accelerating growth into 2H and 2017E, (2) consensus EBITDA upgrade (we are 4-5% above consensus on average), (3) ‘unlocking’ of FCF from mid-2017E, because by then operators will have graduated from a three-year long peak capex cycle (capex-to-sales to drop to 5% by 2018E, from c20% in 2015-16E) and new casinos’ ramp-up will boost Op CFs, (4) possible, yet unmodelled, upside from potential policy supports. Lastly, as the demand/profit visibility improves, we foresee investors increasingly looking at FCF yields; we think this will be positive for Macau, as they look attractive on a recurring FCF yield at 7-9% (ex-expansion capex).

  • •    What should we do? (sector view): Macau stocks could ideally be bought lower given likely volatile 2Q, and we would be buyers on any meaningful profit pullbacks in anticipation of positive momentum re-building from July/Aug - a period w'here we expect estimates and valuation to move directionally higher. That said, we’re turning more selective, as ‘easy’ recovery rally is now main ring - wre prefer names with clear positives (stating the obvious), not those “stuck in the middle”.
