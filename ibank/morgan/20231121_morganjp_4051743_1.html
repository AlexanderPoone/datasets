Global economic outlook 2024

  • •    Global GDP (2.8% Q4/Q4) and core CPI (4.1%) gains well exceeded expectations this year

  • •    Alarge and synchronized monetary tightening drag has been blunted by a resilient private sector and positive fiscal and commodity price shocks

  • •    Growth is resilient but divergent. Manufacturing stalled, while Western Europe flirted with recession

  • •    Inflation is elevated but divergent. Goods prices are nowstabilizing, while service price inflation remains high

  • •    Growth is poised to slow as positive shocks fade, while rising fields and tighter credit bite

  • •    We expect subpar 2.1%ar global GDP growth in 1H24

  • •    Inflation moderation should be limited by lingering damage to supply and a shift in infiation psychology: core CPI gains should settle at 3%ar in 1H24

  • •    Boil the frog narrative sees erosion in private-sector health amidst sustained restrictive policies and sluggish growth

  • •    Pressure likely concentrated in business sector where margins should compress, prompting slower hiring and spending

  • •    Vulnerability likely to build gradually: We see a 25% chance of recession by 1H24, 45% by 2112 4. and 60% by 1H25

  • •    Ourbottom-up country forecasts do not incorporate a 2024-25 recession: US and Western Europe growth slips below 1%, EM anticipated to grow at trend-like 3.8%

  • •    We do not believe innation can be controlled on a sustained expansion path, but recent developments soften onr skepticism

  • •    US supply-side performance has been impressive this year, easing labor markets despite strong growth

  • •    Domestic demand shortfalls in China and Europe point to a potential ongoing disinflationary impulse

  • •    Soft-landing outcome dependent on innation decline that allows monetary easing to begin by about midyear
