CPI : Higher specs requirement but not the key silicon

Dual socket CPU configuration comprises 80%+ of current servers. As GPUs and Al ASIC (TPU) are key drivers to accelerating processing, the number of CPUs do not increase in Al servers. However, the CPU specs of Al server are much higher than the regular ones. According to Nvidia, the default CPUs in IXiX AI00 and IXiX HI0O are AMD EPYC 7742 (Rome) and Intel 4th gen Xeon 8480C (EagleStrcaml processors.

The prices of both CPUs are 5-10x higher than mainstream server CPUs.

Memory and storage: Meaningful content increase to facilitate Al workload

The DRAM content is around 600 GB per regular server while the default DRAM specs of Nvidia’s IIGX/DGX series is 2 TB. There is also 40GB 80GR GDDR per GPU.

Assuming 8 GPUs in a single Al server, total DRAM content could be 2 TB+ 320GB 640GB GDDR. Besides, we also see higher specs of DRAM in Al servers such as DDR5 adoption in CPU DIMM and high bandwidth memory (HUM) for GDDR.
