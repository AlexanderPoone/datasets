Xilinx

Miss on Guidance On Weakness in Wired/Wireless And Slower Reacceleration in Datacenter; Reit UW

Xilinx reported mixed results for F3Q20 (Dec-Q) with revenue below the midpoint of guidance / consensus and EPS above the midpoint of guidance / consensus driven by an expected pause in Data Center along with expected declines in Wired/Wireless due to the ongoing transition of FPGAs to ASICs for 5G base stations, and declines in AIT and low SD% growth in ABC due to muted demand trends across its broad customer base. Revenue of $723M was down 10% Y/Y and down 13% Q/Q with gross margin of 68.0% and above consensus of 67.5% due to an increase in ABC (higher GMs) and a decline in WWG (lower GMs). Advanced products declined 4% Y/Y (down 18% Q/Q), accounting for 70% of sales, but there was continued broad-based growth in the Zynq SoC platforms (+26% Y/Y) despite a weaker Wireless business. F4Q20 (Mar-Q) revenue guidance of ~$765M (up 6% Q/Q) was well below consensus of $827M and previously stated guidance of up 15% Q/Q while GM guidance of 69.5% was above consensus of 68.4% due to lower than expected WWG revenue (lower GMs) offset by moderate growth in DCG (higher GMs) and strong Q/Q growth from AIT and ABC (higher GMs). The miss in guidance is due to greater than expected declines in WWG (Wired and Wireless both down) due to a slowdown in the 5G rollout, the ongoing transition of FPGAs to ASICs for baseband products, and broad-based weakness across the Wired business. This is being offset by broad based growth across Xilinx’s different businesses with DCG returning to moderate growth vs. original guidance of strong growth due to a slower ramp with Xilinx’s storage and network customers along with growth in AIT (A&D and ISM are expected to be up as multiple programs ramp) and in ABC (auto expected to recover as ADAS demand improves vs. F3Q20). We are lowering our FY20 and FY21 estimates and our new PT moves from $88 to $85 which assumes that the stock trades at a premium 22-24x multiple times 2HFY21 earnings power of $3.70 and we remain UW.

• Mixed Dec-Q results driven by declines in Wired/Wireless and temporary pause in Datacenter as expected. Xilinx reported Dec-Q sales of $723M (down 10% Y/Y, down 13% Q/Q), below the midpoint of guidance / consensus of $725M with mixed results across end-markets. Data Center increased 8% Y/Y and decreased 16% Q/Q, in-line with management expectations of a temporary pause in the Dec-Q and benefitted from sales of compute and networking products to hyperscale customers. WWG was down 18% Y/Y and down 29% Q/Q as expected due to the continued transition from FPGAs to ASICs at key basestation customers and some program related delays with one of Xilinx's OEM customers. AIT was down 5% Q/Q and ABC was up 2% Q/Q as expected due to muted demand trends across its broad customer base. GM of 68.0% was above consensus/JPMe of 67.5%/ 68.1% due to declines in WWG (lower GMs) and an increase in ABC (higher GMs). Opex was $318M (guide $33OM) and EPS of $0.68 was well above consensus of $0.61 on higher than expected margins and lower than expected OpEx. Zynq platform revenues grew 26% Y/Y, despite the negative impact of a weaker Wireless business.

Underweight
