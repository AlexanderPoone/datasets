Capital allocation is a key long term development we are tracking

We continue to pay attention to capital allocation at Koc and Sabanci and highlight both companies as being on track to improve returns. We favour Koc, however, due to its well-diversified portfolio in terms of region and sector. Koc generates 33% of its sales from international markets and, including Tupras, 55% of its NAV is well protected against fit risks, making the stock a good hedge against Turkey’s macro vulnerabilities. Koc Holding also has a sensible capital allocation strategy by focusing on growing its global footprint, investing in industries where it maintains a leadership position and only initiating acquisitions with a solid IRR target, i.e. high teens in Turkey and low teens in other markets in USD.

Sabanci Holding has recently increased its focus on capital allocation with a target to reduce its reliance on the banking sector and improve transparency in its portfolio through redefining its portfolio with spin-offs, acquisitions and IPOs.
