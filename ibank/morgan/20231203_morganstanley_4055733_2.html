  • # 5 Adyen enters new partnership with Finnish retailer S Group. This week (1st Dec) Ayden shared that it will support S Group in connecting its online and offline sales channels and in improving its Loyalty programme capacity, as well as provide new omnichannel functionalities. Through the partnership, S Group will be able to offer new payment methods and install new payments terminals which will be gradually rolled out from Spring 2024. According to the press release, S Group manages over 500m transactions per year and has >1.9k Locations across Finland and Estonia.

  • # 6 N26 announces it expects to turn a profit in 2024. This week (29th Nov) N26 shared its product strategy and business overview alongside the announcement of the company's key financials for FY22 and its outlook for 2023/24. It aims to Launch a new stock/ETF trading product in 1H24, post the Launch of N26 Crypto and its Instant Savings Accounts. In FY22, N26 grew its "revenue-relevant" customer base by 300k to 4m, which it expects to grow further at a mid-single digit % range in FY23. It also expects to deliver >30%Y revenue growth for FY23 vs 24%Y growth in FY22, driven by increased customer account usage and activity. Transaction volumes grew 22%Y to €97bn in FY22 and N26 expects a further 13%Y growth in FY23. As at FY22, N26 had €7.2bn in customer deposits, expected to reach €8bn in FY23. In FY22 the company reported a net Loss of -€213m driven by increased investments into infrastructure, although it expects this will be closer to -€100m in FY23 and that 2H24 will mark N26's shift to monthly profitability.
