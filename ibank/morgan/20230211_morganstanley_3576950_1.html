The NeverLanding Story

Much Like The NeverEnding Story, the no-Landing version in which inflation reacceLerates is a fantasy. In a true 'no landing' scenario, inflation comes down without a growth slowdown. Otherwise, central banks should engineer a Landing to stop 'The Inflation' from engulfing our wonderland world.

Global Macro Strategy

We discuss the upcoming US CPI report and Governor Waller's speech on inflation. We discuss implications of the BoJ Governor nomination for JPY markets. Finally, we address whether falling US equities will save the USD.

Interest Rate Strategy

We stay neutral on US duration and curve. We continue to suggest receiving EUR SySy swap, Long March 139/141/142 Bund call fly, and short Mar 23/Mar 24 Euribor inversion trade. We close the EUR 30s50s steepeners and initiate a EUR 5s10s30s swap fly receiving 10y. We keep our 2s10s BTP flatteners and our OAT 5s2Os5O fly Long 20y. We maintain our Long OAT Nov 32 vs. Bund Aug 32, our tactical short Sy BTP vs. Bono, and our RV trade short green OAT 2039 vs. green Olo 20 39. We suggest Long 30y real yields and receiving 10y20y SONIA. We maintain JGB 20s40s flattener, Long 30y JGB ASW, and Long 20y JGB ASW vs ESTR.

Currency & Foreign Exchange

The USD could weaken along side falling US EPS estimates if valuations rise and US rates fall. We stay Long EUR/GBP. G10 FX sensitivity to relative equity performance has picked up since July. We prefer not to chase the rally in SEK and stick with short EUR/NOK instead. We preview the February RBNZ meeting and see scope for NZD outperformance as markets Likely underprice the Likelihood of a 75bp hike. We comment on the conflicting messages from the jobs report and remarks from the BoC.

Inflation-Linked Bonds

In the US, we maintain Long 10y BEs, Long Sy inflation swaps, short Feb52 BE vs. Long 30y CPI swap, and Long 6m6m CPI swaps. In the UK, we maintain Long IL28s. At the back end of the curve, RPI forwards are well supported due to Lack of Linker issuance. We also believe that high-yielding UK Fl assets will become attractive in this environment given falling inflation, Less realised rates volatility, and demand for carry.

Short-Duration Strategy

In the next several weeks, we expect an outperformance of front-end T-bills, RRP usage to be biased higher, and soft conditions in repo. We see the EFFR decline as temporary for now. We stay short 2y SOFR swap spreads.
