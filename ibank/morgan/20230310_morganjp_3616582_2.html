  • 1.    From high-frequency trackers to official monthly activity data

In this section, we make our first attempt to map the high-frequency trackers to monthly official activity data.

  • •    The deviation of subway traffic index from its 2019 average, on a two-month moving average basis, tracks the sequential momentum (on a %3m/3m ar basis) in retail sales veiy well. We expect subway ridership will sustain its recent pace, which tracks our baseline forecast for near-term retail sales.

  • •    The weekly auto retail sales published by the Chin a Passenger Car Association ( CPCA) shares a similar trend with the monthly auto sales unit we have been tracking, published by the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers (CAAM). The CAAM auto sales unit for January declined 34.8%oya, in line with the CPC A weekly tracking. We think the February CAAM auto sales unit to be published next week may post a narrower decline or possibly positive growth.

  • •    The weekly operating rates for semi-steel & full-steel tires track the au to production component of industrial production (IP). With thepost-LNY pickup in Febmary despite moderation in the first week of March, we think auto production may have picked up.

  • •    The weekly operating rates for steel plants can also give color to steel production under IP. The operating rate recoveiy points to steel production recovery as well, though relatively modestly.

  • •    The daily housing transactions of 30 major citiestracks theNBS’s floor space sold for commodity buildings, especially in recent years. With better-than-expected recovery in Febmary, we think the NBS’s floor space sold may pick up as well, though still staying in contractionary territory if taking Jan-Feb as a whole.

  • •    The deadweight tonnage of departing/arrived ships at 20 major ports is a helpful timely tracker to gauge near-term trade recovery, though it may track export volu me better, instead of export value.
