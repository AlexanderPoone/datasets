Global Metals & Mining

Security & Supply: assessing LT Russia supply risks & re-rating opportunities among global champions

In this collaborative report actoss JPM global Metals & Mining teams, we assess the outlook for Russian metals supply across the period 2022-25E and longer-term. We detail Russia’s rail and maritime transport networks and limitations, in order to assess the potential and timescale for export trade routes to be restructured if export risks increase. JPM global teams identify regional Metals & Mining champions with exposure to Russia-centric commodities and low country risk, which have potential to grow premium equity multiples over time. These companies include: BMP. Rio Tinto, Freeport, Teck, Southern Copper, Norsk Hydro, Boliden, South32 and Vale. Our cross sector analysis of supply chains in early March (link) concluded there had been limited disruption to Russian metal exports immediately post 24th Feb, but risk premia for Russian metal exports remain high, as evidenced by sustained near record prices for Russia-centric commodities.

  • •    Russia metals supply risk premia is justified, but we identify low risk to supply of nickel, copper, zinc, PGMs 2022-25E: in a status quo scenario, we do not expect severe risk to JPM’s Russian supply forecasts to 2025. This reflects Russian metals producers' strong cash flows at current prices, relatively low debt maturities in 2022 and capital controls effective until Sept’22 prevent hard currency payments to non-Russia shareholders.

  • •    Longer term, procurement & funding are risks to unapproved projects: Russia’s mining industry typically procures specialized mining equipment from non-Russian manufacturers. If geopolitical risks are protracted, procurement, reduced access to equity / debt capital, higher cost of capital, are likely to negatively impact project approvals. This has potential to impact new project approvals and Russian supply longer-term (2025+).

  • •    Aluminium faces most significant & immediate disruption risk: Near term supply risks are greatest in aluminium, reflecting Russia's potential import shortage of alumina, since JPM estimates ~60% of Rusal’s alumina import needs are closed off. Potential for alumina shortages are a risk to JPM’s forecast of 4.2-4.4Mt aluminium from Russia 2022-25. Rio Tinto, Norsk Hydro and South32 produce aluminium in lower risk jurisdictions.

  • •    Analysis of Russia’s rail & port networks & trade routes: Russia’s sea ports along its western border have total capacity of >500Mt pa, more than twice the capacity of the Far East basin’s capacity. In railways, Eastern field (Trans-Sib & BAM links) has total capacity of -144Mtpa vs ~270Mtpa capacity of North-Western Field (to Baltic sea ports) and Southern Field (to Azov-Black Sea basin ports) rail links. The re-routing of Russia’s traditional export flows Westward to Eastwards may take time and will be constrained by infrastructure limitations.

  • •    Regional Champions across JPM global coverage: We identify high-quality global miners with exposure to Russia-centric commodities but with majority of earnings derived from (perceived) low risk jurisdictions. EMEA: RIO. BHP, Anglo American, Norsk Hydro, Boliden, Antofagasta. Australia: BHP. RIO, S32, Oz Minerals, Sandfire Resources. N. America: Freeport. Teck Resources. LatAm: Vale, Southern Copper Company.
