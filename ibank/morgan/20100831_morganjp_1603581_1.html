31 August 2010

China Railway Group Limited

1H10 core profit surged by 53% Y/Y after excluding one-offs; maintain OW on robust revenue and steady margin recovery

CRG (390 HK, OW) released 1H10 and 2QJ0 results post market close.

  • •    1H10 core net profit surged by 53% Y/Y: Based on HK GAAP, CRG's core net income of Rmb3.3 bn, after excluding the one-off effects from F/X gains & losses, was up 53% Y/Y on a 30% rise in top-line revenue. The interim result achieved 43% of our FY estimate, in line with expectations (JPM FY10E: Rmb8.4 bn, in line with Bloomberg mean estimate of Rmb8.3 bn). Revenue came in at Rmbl86 bn, meeting 46% of JPM’s FY estimate. The blended gross margin of 6.3% during 1H10 improved by clO bps on Y/Y basis and c50 bps on H/H basis. We expect the gross margin to further improve in 2H10 driven by first-time profit recognition from projects fast tracked during 1H09. One negative was the negative Op-CF during 1H10, which appears mainly driven by CRG’s stocking up on inventories and land reserve in light of rising new contract orders. Refer to Table 1 for results details.

  • •    New contract orders during 6M10 reached Rmh372 bn, up 45% Y/Y; order backlog increased to 2.3x of annual sales (up 26% from end-FY09): New contract orders for the core infra construction segment rose by 45% Y/Y—higher than CROC’s 19% rise during the same period, suggesting potential market share gains by CRG. CRG's order backlog as of June 2010 increased to Rmb856 bn, equating to 2.3x annual group revenue (up from 2.Ox in FY09).

  • •    Maintain Overweight on CRG: YTD, CRG’s performance has been largely in line with the HSCEI Index. We remain positive on CRG given: (1) potential upward revision on MOR’s railway target and annual capex; (2) attractive valuation, at 12x P/E, 4.8x EV/EBITDA and O.lx EV/order-back-log on FY10E, vs. c20% EPS CAGR based on our forecasts. We retain our Dec-10 DCF-based (4.5% risk-free rate, 5.0% market risk premium, and 2% terminal growth) HK$6.40 price target. A key risk is lower-than-expected railway construction revenue or profitability.
