EUR/USD looks set to retrace further after breaking initial support

EUR/USD pressures the 1.0707 50-day moving average as the market pulls back from the 1.1035 Feb 2 high and 1.0898-1.0944 medium-term resistance zone. A second set of bearish technical signals developed in Jan as the market carved out what looked like the 5,h-wave of a rally sequence from the 0.9537 Sep 28 low. While the persistent strength in the month following the first set of bearish signals triggered in Dec fiustrated, the pair finally looks set to see a deeper retracement of the medium-term rally. Provided EUR/USD sustains the break below the 1.0802 tactical top pattern breakdown, we expect at least a test of the 1.0483 Jan 6 low (4,h-wave of lesser degree/initial target after a 5-wave trend) and 1.0463 Oct 38.2% retrace (Exhibit 1). Other medium-term support includes the 1.032 4 200-day moving average. We view the 1.01 area as a likely floor over the medium-term, as that area not only includes the Oct 61.8% retrace but also marks the 4Q22 base pattern breakout.
