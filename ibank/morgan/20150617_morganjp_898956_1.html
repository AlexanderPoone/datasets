We cut our Zhaojin’s FYI5-17 earnings estimates by 2%, 26% and 62%, respectively, to reflect higher capex and interest expenses. While we consider the company’s initiatives to acquire growth and lower costs positive, near-term earnings will come under pressure given rapidly growing interest payments. We see increased likelihood of equity fundraising and dividend cuts. Stay UW with a PT of HKD4.5.

Debt-funded acquisitions. Zhaojin announced to purchase a 60% stake in Dongfang Yanjin from its parent for a consideration of RMB 10.1m. This followed with its recent acquisition of Haiyu Gold Mine for Rmb2.7Bn. Dongfang is an engineering design company, with FY14 net loss of RMB5m It is expected to lower Zhaojin’s mine engineering design cost in the future. Haiyu Gold Mine is a greenfield project which is forecast to increase Zhaojin’s attributable gold resources by c37% and long-term mined-gold output by c90% from 2014 levels. We believe these two acquisitions will be funded by bank borrowings and working capital.

  • •    Stretched balance sheet. While we view Zhaojin’s initiatives to leverage up balance sheet for future growth and cost reduction as positive, near-term earnings will come under pressure with growing interest payments. We estimate interest coverage ratio to deteriorate sharply to 2.5x (from 3.6x in 2014) and net gearing (ND/E) soaring to 134% (from 85% in 2014) by end-2016. This may force Zhaojin cutting dividend payout to 10% or lower (from 33% in 2014, 40% in 2013).

Lowering earnings estimates. With no immediate earnings contribution from the two acquisitions, we cut Zhaojin’s FY15-17E earnings by 2%, 26% and 62% to reflect higher capex and interest expenses. We see increased likelihood of an equity fund-raising and dividend cuts. Our FYI5-17 earnings estimates are c55-86% below consensus.
