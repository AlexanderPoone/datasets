While the market has been paying a lot of attention to the company’s China expansion in the past six to nine months, we believe the resilient performance of Link's core business in HK is still under appreciated by the market. Link has been actively managing its balance sheet to drive return, including disposing of mature assets to buy back units at higher yields and borrowing at a low cost to acquire assets in China. We remain positive on the defensive exposure and return-oriented mindset of Link. At a 4.5% FY16E dividend yield, the stock still looks attractive to us and we maintain our OW.

  • •    Solid DPU growth of 10.5%. Link REIT reported 1HFY16 DPU at HK$0.9899, up 10.5% Y/Y and 4.1% above our estimates, mainly driven by revenue growth and NPI margin improvement. With the increase in value of the retail properties and car parks in HK mainly driven by NPI growth and improved asset quality, NAV per unit grew 5.3% H/H to HK$54.24.


  • •    Retail sales for HK portfolio stayed resilient. Link still recorded overall psf tenant sales growth of 6.0% Y/Y, where Food & Beverage was up 10.8%. Link was able to attract new tenants like H&M, which opened a store at Stanley Plaza. On the other hand, car park rentals were exceptional as revenue grew by 10.2% Y/Y on the back of strong demand from shoppers and neighbouring residents. We expect revenue to continue to grow steadily with the stable retail rental reversion of 23.6% and high demand for car parks.

  • •    China will remain small. Link has given guidance of limiting China exposure to 12.5% of total portfolio value. Given China exposure at 7% now, we believe there is still room for Link to expand in China but it will remain prudent and selective. For the Beijing EC Mall acquired this year, Link already recorded reversion of 33.7% in 1HFY16. The recent completion of the acquisition of Corporate Avenue will further boost DPU in 2HFY16.

  • •    Pmdent risk management to prepare for rate hike cycle. Link has carried out various measures to reduce interest rate risks as rate hike cycle approaches. That includes: (1) maintaining a longer life of debt at 4.9 years; (2) increasing debt hedging ratio to 63%; (3) issuing cheaper debt to lower effective interest rate to 2.58% and; (4) keeping gearing at a comfortable level of 16.9% (with a 25% cap). Although REITs will inevitably be impacted when rates increase, we believe Link has been adopting the right measures to mitigate potential risks.
