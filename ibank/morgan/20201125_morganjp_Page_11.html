As such, we believe that Turkish bank valuations stand out positively, even despite the recent 40% rally in USD terms. Current valuations of 0.45x 2021e PBV are at some of the deepest discounts across CEEMEA, both relative to other stocks as well as relative to their own trading ranges. This compares to returns that are set to accelerate to the upper end of the historical range and stand out therefore relative to their own average returns profile as well as versus CEEMEA Bank peers. Even taking the elevated cost of equity into consideration, we think Turkish bank valuations look attractive within the CEEMEA Bank universe; we think shares shall remain beholden to high cost of equity and we do not foresee shares trading past book value, yet current 0.45x 2022e P/TBV looks undervalued versus our fair value of 0.8x. We see 90% upside potential on average for Turkish Banks shares, and factoring in continued lira depreciation that we run through our operating models, this would be c.65% in USD terms.
