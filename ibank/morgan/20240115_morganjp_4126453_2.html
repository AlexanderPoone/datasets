Dispersion pattern in HK stocks

During earnings season, stock prices often experience increased dispersion due to the influx of new information. This variance in earnings outcomes relative to market expectations leads to divergent stock performances, thereby increasing the dispersion in stock prices. To identify earnings sensitive stocks, we calculate the stock versus index realized volatility' over a one-month window encompassing two weeks before and after earnings are released. We then evaluate the median excess realized volatility against the current stock versus index implied volatility. This comparison helps determine the market’s pricing of expected dispersion compared to historical data. The hit ratio, defined as the likelihood of past earnings’ excess realized volatility surpassing the current implied volatility spread, is calculated. This ratio ranks stocks based on the probability of profitable dispersion trading during earnings at the present entry level.

To illustrate the dispersion pattern beyond the earnings period, we present an analysis of the stock versus index realized volatility spanning 12 calendar months. Although most stocks exhibit increased excess volatility during earnings seasons, selecting stocks with a historical tendency to offer greater dispersion beyond earnings remains a key interest for investors.
