KBC is one of the largest producers of laminates, PCBs and industrial chemicals in China. We see more signs of stronger demand and pricing for laminates, PCBs and chemicals, which combined with subdued costs of production, should lift GPM by almost 3% (excluding property sales) to nearly 20% for KB (still well below record 24-25% GPM in past peak demand periods). We lift our Dec-17 PT to HK$32.

  • •    Laminates, PCBs and chemicals benefit from stronger PC and construction demand. We have not made any changes in the property-related business in this update and focus our adjustments on improving conditions in the three core businesses. Demand from PC is recovering and other new industries (autos, LED lighting and healthcare) contribute to strong demand for laminates in 2016. We now lift our EBITM for laminates in 2H16 by 2.5% compared to 1H16. Likewise, PCB demand should rise alongside laminates as the customer profile is quite similar. Chemical prices have been rising since June on mainly stronger construction-related demand.


  • •    Lifting NP on stronger pricing. We have revised FY16 and FY17 sales up 1.8% and 2.4%, respectively, to reflect stronger laminates, chemicals and PCB demand. We assume an increase of 1.1-1.2% in GPM as selling prices rise faster than the cost of production. The increase in NP of 8.4% in FY16 and 11.8% in FY17 is mainly due to higher prices and GPM for all core operations. We lift our DCF-based Dec-17 PT to HK$32 (previously HK$27).

  • •    Price, valuation and risks. Our DCF-based price target (Dec-17) of HK$32.0 implies a CY18E P/E of 8.5x. The key risks to our PT are raising cost of copper and oil based chemicals and falling demand from electronics producers.
