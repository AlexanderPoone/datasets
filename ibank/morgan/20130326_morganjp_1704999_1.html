Agile Property Holdings Ltd

FY12 result review: Above expectations; a cheap stock with more catalysts in 2H13E

Agile reported stronger-than-expected earnings, on the back of better margins driven by lower construction costs. We believe Agile’s 2013 sales performance will depend on the sales of a few high-end projects in Nanjing, Changzhou, Guangzhou and Chengdu. Agile’s valuation looks cheap at 5.2x 2013E P E and a 44% discount to NAV. However, for the stock to rerate, we believe we need to see sales improve to more than Rmb3.5B per month, which might not happen in the near term given that most new launches are concentrated in 2H. We stay OW but trim our Dec-13 PT to HK$13.10.

Results beat estimates. Agile reported core net profit of Rmb4,636MM for FY12, up 44% Y/Y and 19% above our estimate. Fully diluted core EPS was Rmbl.305, up 22% Y/Y. Full year DPS was Rmb0.384, implying a 5.2% dividend yield and a payout ratio of 23.1%. Agile’s delivery area and ASP were in-line, but earnings were higher than our estimate due to better margins on the back of lower construction and financing costs.

  • •    Maintain OW, but need to see more rerating catalysts. We maintain our Overweight rating on Agile with a reduced Dec-13 PT of HK$13.10 due to increased policy risk and sluggish sales. Our PT implies 43% upside. Agile’s result was better than expected, prompting us to raise our earnings estimates. Valuations also look cheap, with the stock trading at a discount to its mid-cap peers. However, sales have been weak and the achievement rate has been slow, preventing the stock from getting rerated. Looking at the launch pipeline, most of the key projects will not be launched until 2H13.

Buy in and hope for a sales pick-up. Given the current policy environment, it is unclear what the market sentiment will be like in 2H13, so there are certain downside risks to Agile’s sales target and our estimates, which could result in underperformance of the stock. That said, Agile has a solid balance sheet and cheap financing costs, and at the current valuation we do not believe the stock will trade much lower from here. We see the current stock price as a good entry point in anticipation of a pick-up in sales in the second half of the year.

Overweight
