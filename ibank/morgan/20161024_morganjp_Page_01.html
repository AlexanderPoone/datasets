Sa Sa International Holdings Limited

Profit warning expect 1H17 earnings down -35-45%;

However, double-digit growth during Golden Week

Sa Sa has reported its sales update for 2Q16 and Golden Week. Pleasingly, Golden Week SSS grew -12% on the back of -19% increase in number of transactions from Mainland Chinese tourists. Concurrently, however, Sa Sa also issued a profit warning stating it expected net profit to decline -35-45% in 1H17.

  • •    Golden Week SSSG +12.4%. For Golden Week Holiday from 1 to 7 October 2016, retail sales for HK/Macau recorded +13.8% growth and SSSG increased 12.4%. Number of transactions of Mainland Chinese customers increased 19.1%, while average sales per transaction decreased 4.7%. We note that SSS for Golden Week 2015 declined 8.7% yoy, implying two-year stack of +3.7%.

  • •    2Q17 SSSG -2.5%. Sa Sa also reported 2Q17 SSSG for its core HK & Macau market of-2.5% (vs 1Q17 -4.8%, 4Q16 -17.6%, 3Q16 -12.2%, 2Q16 -10.1%). Total turnover for HK & Macau retail declined 2.2%. Average sales per transaction declined 5.8% and total number of transactions increased 3.9%. For local HK customers, number of transactions increased by 1.8% (v 1Q17 -1.5%), while average sales per transaction decreased by 5.5% (v 1Q17 -7.1%). For Mainland Chinese customers, total number of transactions increased 5.9% (v IQ 17 +2.7%), while average sales per transaction decreased by 6.9% (v IQ 17 -6.4%). We expect part of the improvement to have come from Sa Sa's own proactive efforts to adjust its product offering towards more trendy Korean/Japanese products.

  • •    Profit warning - 1H17 expected earnings decline -35-45%. The main reason for the decline was due to drop in sales and gross margin for HK/Macau, weakness in some overseas operations and decline in online profitability.

  • •    Retain Neutral. On one hand, we believe momentum is improving with tourism trends bottoming and Sa Sa gaining share given better execution. However, we expect earnings will continue to remain under pressure in the near term given negative gross margin mix shift. Beyond FY17E, sales visibility also remains low.
